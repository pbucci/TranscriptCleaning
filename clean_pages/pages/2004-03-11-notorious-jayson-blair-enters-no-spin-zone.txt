			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">The Notorious Jayson Blair Enters the No Spin Zone</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-03-11T13:43-05:00">Published March 11, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;March 10, 2004.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the &quot;Follow-Up&quot; segment tonight, the saga of&nbsp;<b>Jayson Blair</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Jayson%20Blair">search</a>).&nbsp; The former &quot;New York Times&quot; reporter resigned after some of his stories were found to have been plagiarized and fabricated.&nbsp; The entire incident caused a huge scandal at the &quot;Times&quot; -- you know all this -- which remains America's most powerful newspaper, even though its editor,&nbsp;<b>Howell Raines</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Howell%20Raines">search</a>), was forced out because of the Blair situation.</p>
<p>With us now is the aforementioned Jayson Blair, the author of the new book &quot;Burning Down My Master's House: My Life at 'The New York Times'.&quot;</p>
<p>You know, this is going to make you feel good, I think.&nbsp; I don't care what you did.&nbsp; You don't have to apologize to me or anybody else on this program.&nbsp; That's between you and &quot;The New York Times&quot; and the people who read that newspaper.&nbsp; I don't care what you did.&nbsp; I don't care if you took drugs.&nbsp; I don't care if you're bipolar.&nbsp; I don't care.</p>
<p>What I do care about, though, is the nation's most powerful and influential newspaper and what is happening over there.&nbsp; As you know, we have issues with &quot;The New York Times.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>JAYSON BLAIR, FORMER &quot;NEW YORK TIMES&quot; REPORTER</strong>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So let's walk through that.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; How big did race play, in your opinion, in you getting the job at the &quot;Times&quot; and then doing your job at the &quot;Times&quot;?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I ultimately do not know what role race played and me being hired, but I can talk a little bit about generally how they view those issues.</p>
<p>I'm sure you've read &quot;Coloring the News&quot; by Bill McGowan.&nbsp; I'm sure you've heard Arthur Salzburger talk about how diversity is a priority at &quot;The New York Times.&quot;&nbsp; A lot of African-American and minority candidates often, you know, are brought in through these programs.</p>
<p>Arguments have been made that we, you know, are not necessarily as prepared as other white reporters at the &quot;Times&quot; and it affects our news coverage.&nbsp; I know, in my personal case, you know, my credentials beat out white, black, green, blue, and...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So getting hired, you felt you had the stuff?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Felt I had the stuff.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Once you're in there and you get in trouble, some guys didn't like you, wrote letters, and...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well -- yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; It's a mixed bag...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... because you have the affirmative action side, you have the people who push affirmative action, who see me as a pawn in their game to prove that, you know, I'm the great black hope, and they're not really paying attention to me and they're pushing me as a pawn, and then you have the people who are upset about affirmative action, and there's this sort of white...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; They're trying to get you.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... understandable white backlash from affirmative action.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I get it.&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; They're trying to get me, which prevents me and them from really doing a good job of doing our jobs, and that's fairly...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right, but who won?&nbsp; See, look, if I was at &quot;The New York Times&quot; and I did what you did, I don't think they would have given me the rope they gave you.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Am I right or wrong?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; You know, there's an argument to be made.&nbsp; I don't ultimately know, but, you know, I don't believe I necessarily would have been given the same amount of rope.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because reading your book -- and I read it last night -- they gave you a lot of rope over there.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; No, they did give me a lot of rope.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; If you worked for me, I would have bounced you out of there much...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; No, I'll be the first to say they gave me a lot of rope.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Now did they do that because they feared you, did they fear confronting you, because it was politically incorrect to call you on the carpet?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well, there's certainly --&nbsp;<b>Jon Landman</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Jon%20Landman">search</a>), the Metro editor of &quot;The New York Times,&quot; talks about how he had concerns and that he was afraid to take it to management because he would be viewed as a racist.&nbsp; I don't think that's necessarily fair that he was put in a position where he felt...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Do you believe that Landman's...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... in that environment...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... Landman's sentiment was reality?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I mean I feel that John did things that, you know,...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, whoa.&nbsp; Landman didn't like you.&nbsp; He thought you were a -- you know...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Smug.&nbsp; Whatever.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He didn't like you, all right, but he was afraid to tell Howell Raines and the crew...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Bottom line...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... because, at least according to John's version of events...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He didn't want to be branded a racist.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Exactly.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Is that true?&nbsp; I mean would he have been branded a racist?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; He was already branded a racist.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He was already branded a racist?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Why?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; And he would have been.&nbsp; Not for good reason.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Because he was tough on everybody?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; He was tough on a lot of people.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Because I'm a racist every day by somebody or other.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well, Tavis Smiley called me a racist a couple of days, too, so...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Well, whatever.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I'm in there with you, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You are in there, and they're afraid to really supervise you the way you should be supervised because somebody might say...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Which is not doing me a favor.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right, obviously.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; What you needed was to get slapped around and slapped around hard very early on.&nbsp; So you say that the editor Howell Raines at the time, OK, quoted, &quot;believed as I did in social change.&quot;</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Now...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; And now I don't mean that as a euphemism for liberal ideas of...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I -- but you don't say what social change.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What did this guy believe in?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well, one of the problems -- and I don't necessarily agree with this notion -- was Howell's reign at the &quot;Times&quot; was he -- you know, I believe that &quot;The New York Times&quot; newsroom does have a social change agenda, and it's very liberal, it's certainly anti-conservative.</p>
<p>You could make the argument that it's a pro-liberal, anti-conservative social change agenda, and Howell didn't just push it, but he made it obvious.&nbsp; It's normally more subtle and more hidden and...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; So they wanted...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... masked and cloaked.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So they wanted a new society, more secular society, more liberal society.&nbsp; Is that fair to say?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; That is fair to say.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And if you didn't buy into that, what would happen?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I can't think of anyone there who didn't buy into that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All of them -- all right.&nbsp; Let me give you...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; People like John who were neo-conservatives...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... a real simple question.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Go.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; If you walk into the newsroom and you said that &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; is my favorite program, I love that O'Reilly guy...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well, look at what...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... what would happen to you if you were a &quot;New York Times&quot; reporter?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I'd be laughed out of the newsroom.&nbsp; I mean, people would brand me as a neo-con, and, you know, they'd stop talking to me.&nbsp; They would...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Really?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; It would hurt my stories.&nbsp; People would say that I -- you know, there are a handful of people who have conservative...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's right.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; John Tierney.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, they've got a couple of token -- Safire and these guys.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; But they're outcasts.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; They're outcasts inside the...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So the prevailing wisdom inside the nation's most powerful newspaper, all right, is...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; It's not open to middle America, let's put it that way, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; It's a very sharp agenda that they push, not only in their editorial pages, but in their news pages.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Unfortunately yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; And if you don't tow that line...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; And that hurts all of us.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... then your career at the &quot;Times&quot; is hurt?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Yes, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Are you telling me the truth, because they're going to say this guy lies -- this guy Blair lies about everything.&nbsp; He's trying to get us.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Look, Bill, you know, the first step for me -- you know, I lived a life of lies, I lived a double life.&nbsp; The first step for me in changing on the in -- I mean on the outside is changing on the inside, and that means coming clean.&nbsp; I'm telling...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So you're telling me the truth.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... you the truth.&nbsp; I mean, Bill, I am liberal to moderate personally.&nbsp; It does nothing for my political views to lay this point out, but it is the truth...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... and it's the truth I outline in the book, too.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Why does the publisher and all of -- why do they tolerate this kind of a -- you know, &quot;The New York Times&quot; has a proud tradition -- I mean has a proud tradition...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I mean...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; .., and now it has become an ideological hack.&nbsp; Why do they tolerate it?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Well, I mean I think that the new publisher -- there's a book being done right now about the years -- by&nbsp;<b>Bill McGowan</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Bill%20McGowan">search</a>)&nbsp;who wrote,&nbsp;<b>&quot;Coloring the News&quot;</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=%22Coloring%20the%20News%22">search</a>)&nbsp;about the years of Arthur Salzberger, Jr., as the publisher of the paper, and he has a liberal agenda that he wants to push, and...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So it comes right down from the top.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... and he thinks the fact that it's -- the newspaper is respected by so many other media outlets, it doesn't matter what they print, they have the Gothic title.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; You off the drugs now?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Clean and sober.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Are you going to stay off the drugs or what?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; I'm going to try one day at a time.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because that was your -- that's what did you in, right?</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; That was -- I mean the drugs and then also the manic depression, but, ultimately, I need to live a clean life and an honest life.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, but the manic depression you could have dealt with a shrink or...</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... something like that.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Exactly right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Once you start with the crack and the cocaine, you're done.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Which is another point that's worth bringing up.&nbsp; You know, it's -- there -- it's very hard when you're in an amoral place.&nbsp; I heard you on the segment before talking about how &quot;The New York Times&quot; had run a story about Ludacris and, hey, it's OK to do crack...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, they loved him.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... but they attacked Mel Gibson.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; I've worked in a very sort of much more moral environment, and I'm in a much more moral environment and it's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; ... 12-step.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; The book is &quot;Burning Down My Master's House: My Life at 'The New York Times'.&quot;</p>
<p>Jayson Blair, we appreciate you coming in.</p>
<p>BLAIR:&nbsp; Thanks, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
