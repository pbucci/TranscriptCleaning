			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Andrew Dice Clay enters the 'No Spin Zone'</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2013-07-26T00:00-04:00">Published July 26, 2013</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="2568332879001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/2568332879001/andrew-dice-clay-enters-no-spin-zone" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/072613_factor_clay_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>
							<div class="caption">
								<p itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization" itemscope="" itemprop="sourceOrganization"><span itemprop="name" class="source"></span></p>

						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; April 3, 2012. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE)</p>
<p>BILL O'REILLY, FOX NEWS HOST: So, you and Woody Allen, both comedians at heart, but total opposites. I mean, he wanted you in this movie.</p>
<p>ANDREW DICE CLAY, ACTOR AND COMEDIAN: Yes, I mean it was like a --</p>
<div></div>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO)</p>
<p>-- freak thing to me because I get a call from --</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>-- my manager, Bruce, going, &quot;Woody Allen wants to meet you tomorrow.&quot; And I thought he's teasing me, you know. Because, to me, I would never think Woody would understand what I do.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: When she had all that money, she wanted nothing to do with you. Now that she's broke, all of a sudden, she's moving in.</p>
<p>SALLY HAWKINS, ACTRESS: She's not just broke. She's all screwed up. And it's none of your damn business, OK. She's family.</p>
<p>CLAY: She stole our money.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, why did he want you.</p>
<p>CLAY: He saw there was a lot more depth to me than what I do as a stand-up, let's say.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: &quot;Excuse me, is this the end of the line.&quot; I said, &quot;No, moron. It's the front and we're all standing (bleep) backwards.&quot;</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: I have to be honest, I'm very humbled by the whole experience and very grateful for it because acting is what I always wanted to do that's why I started and performed on comedy stages to like hone characters and develop my own method of acting.</p>
<p>And it's been a crazy rollercoaster ride. People seem to be very happy with what I've done.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Good. I've been a big critic of entertainers who sell product to children. And the stuff is raw, profane, disrespectful. And impressionable children pick up on it.</p>
<p>Now, that's a very controversial stance that I've taken. Do you have any feeling about that.</p>
<p>CLAY: Well, you know, my big concern, not so much with comedy and music, is more what goes on with the Internet because that's the easy access to me.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right.</p>
<p>CLAY: I mean I'm probably the most vile comic on stage because I -- you know, my persona is a tough-talking Brooklyn guy.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: Wait, I've got to feel it. I've got to (bleep) feel it. Shut the (bleep) up.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why did you decide to be so raw in your act.</p>
<p>CLAY: Well, once I decided to really delve into the stand-up and I would see that a lot of comics don't really understand performance art, meaning walk around, meaning really entertain other than their jokes, to give people a show. I'd put it that way.</p>
<p>It was almost like, &quot;I wanted to put and build a comedic hero.&quot;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: Jack and Jill went up the hill, both with a buck and a quarter. Jill came down with 2.50, oh!</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: When I grew up, I said, &quot;There was never a comic that had that bigger than life, tough-talking attitude.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>And through the years, that's how the material would build. It's almost like --</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>-- if you came to see that guy on Broadway, it would be a show.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>But because he's a comic, I went through -- you know, a whole thing with the media for years, you know, &quot;Who's Dice. Who's Andrew Clay.&quot;</p>
<p>Well, that's my stage persona. And there'd been others, --</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: Eddie Murphy, Richard Pryor, Lenny Bruce.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>Only they didn't put the whole persona with it. They didn't put the big leather jackets with --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes.</p>
<p>CLAY: -- the studs and the rhinestones. And I really just wanted to give people something like a -- almost like from a comic book, a hero.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: If somebody came to you and said, &quot;You know what, you're talented and you were very successful. You became the hottest stand-up in the country at one point, but you've coarsened the country.&quot; How would you answer that.</p>
<p>CLAY: Well, I don't feel I did because the people coming to see me are adult, you know. And it's all about the jokes, you know.</p>
<p>But what was made out of it years ago is not what it is today. Because even my material today, it's just as raw, and I like performing like that, it's who I am as a comic.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Success justifies that criticism.</p>
<p>CLAY: It's not about justifying. It's about people wanting something, you know. If you want PG stuff, there's plenty of it out there.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Last question. Woody Allen and you on the set, he's a remote guy. He doesn't deal with actors that much.</p>
<p>He kind of just comes in and tells you what to do, you do it, and he says, &quot;See you around.&quot;</p>
<p>CLAY: Wait, you know what it is, Woody does his directing, casting the parts.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right.</p>
<p>CLAY: It's almost like, &quot;All right, that's my direction.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>I got the perfect person to play that role.&quot;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: Believe me, she knew Ginger, OK. She knew.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>CLAY: I am just absolutely grateful that I finally got to do the kind of role that I've been wanting to do for 20 years.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, Mr. Clay, nice to see you as always.</p>
<p>CLAY: Great to see you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK.</p>
<p>(END VIDEOTAPE)</p>
