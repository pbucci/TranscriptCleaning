			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Palin Reacts to Obama's Oval Office Address on Gulf Disaster</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2010-06-16T00:00-04:00">Published June 16, 2010</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>	                
		            </div>
	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p><img style="margin-left: 10px; margin-right: 10px; float: left;" class="alignright" src="http://www.foxnews.com/images/607235/0_61_320_Palin.jpg" border="0" width="224" height="169" /></p>
<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; June 15, 2010. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> Joining us from Wasilla,</p>
<p>Alaska, former governor of that state, Sarah Palin. All right, did you</p>
<p>have a big beef with the president tonight in any way, governor?</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/printer_friendly_story/0,3566,594753,00.html#" onclick="foxGrabPlayer('4241155','','Palin on Obamas Primetime Presser');return false;">Click here to watch the segment!</a></strong> <img src="http://www.foxnews.com/i/redes/icon-video.gif" border="0" /></p>
<p><strong>SARAH PALIN, FOX NEWS CONTRIBUTOR:</strong> I did. I kind of</p>
<p>have a big beef with you too though, Bill, if you don't acknowledge</p>
<p>that President Obama is wrong on his call for a need for energy policy.</p>
<p>Certainly we need that, but he is wrong not to acknowledge that we</p>
<p>still need on a three-legged stool the conventional sources of energy</p>
<p>to be drilled here. Otherwise, Bill, we are going to be dropped to our</p>
<p>knees and bowing to the Saudis and Venezuela and places like Russia</p>
<p>that will keep producing oil and petroleum products, and we will have</p>
<p>to ask them to produce for us because we will still be dependent upon</p>
<p>these sources of energy.</p>
<p>In addition though, too, shifting more towards the renewables which,</p>
<p>of course, we need. And the other leg of that stool is conservation.</p>
<p>President Obama, it scares me, it saddens me that the CEO of our nation</p>
<p>does not understand that inherent link between the conventional sources</p>
<p>of energy that we're dependent upon and our security, our prosperity,</p>
<p>our freedom.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK, what was the — when you were the governor of</p>
<p>the state, you dealt with oil companies all the time. What was the most</p>
<p>difficult thing that you had to deal with when you were having meetings</p>
<p>with them face to face?</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> Believing that their perception of what the</p>
<p>circumstances were in any situation that we dealt with them, whether it</p>
<p>be a spill or lax infrastructure maintenance or the value of the</p>
<p>resource that was being sold. In any of those issues, it was believing</p>
<p>what they were telling me and my administration in terms of their</p>
<p>perspective on what the facts were.</p>
<p>Now, here's where the problem lies with President Obama in waiting</p>
<p>so long, you know, eight weeks before meeting with the CEO of BP and</p>
<p>with the high-ranking officials that have been calling the shots. He</p>
<p>has allowed this industry player to get to define the facts. So they</p>
<p>are just in this position of having astronomical maximum liability</p>
<p>exposure. He's allowed them to define the facts of this spill. You can</p>
<p>never be allowed to do that as a CEO and be on a level playing field,</p>
<p>being equals there at the table when you allow one side of the table,</p>
<p>in this case BP, in dealing with this spill to define the facts.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. But 49 percent, according to a poll</p>
<p>today, we'll have later, 49 percent of Americans still want BP to run</p>
<p>the show. And only 45 percent want the government to run the show. So</p>
<p>most Americans aren't down with the Obama administration calling the</p>
<p>shots.</p>
<p>Now, President Obama basically is, as Charles Krauthammer pointed</p>
<p>out, a dreamer. I mean, he wants, I think, good for the nation as far</p>
<p>as energy is concerned. You made a very valid point. We can't throw the</p>
<p>oil people under the bus. If we do that, our economy is going to tank</p>
<p>totally. And if we spend billions of dollars on pie in the sky stuff</p>
<p>that doesn't work, we're going to go bankrupt. So what is your solution</p>
<p>here, governor? What would you do tonight? Tell the nation tonight what</p>
<p>you would have said your main point in that speech. Go.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> Stopping the gusher. That's the No. 1 priority of the nation.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But nobody knows how to do it.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> We need to make sure that all technology is being thrown at this problem.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Nobody knows how to do it.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> Well, we haven't had the assurance that president — we</p>
<p>haven't had the assurance by the president that that has been his top</p>
<p>priority. Instead, what his top priority is, Bill, is cap and tax. It</p>
<p>is using this crisis, not letting it go to waste, but to use this</p>
<p>crisis to increase the cost of energy.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right, if that happens, there will be an outcry.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> And that is going to stall any kind of economic recovery that we have.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Are you telling me that you don't think the president's top priority is stopping that leak? Is that what you are telling me?</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> What I'm telling you is that is not what I am hearing</p>
<p>and what the American public is hearing from the top official in our</p>
<p>government. And that's why those poll numbers show that, no, the</p>
<p>public, we don't know where to turn. If we can't trust BP to be able to</p>
<p>fix this leak, we know we can't trust government because they've had</p>
<p>eight weeks of overseeing, of regulating and kind of coaching this</p>
<p>whole process, this whole issue of stopping the leak. And they haven't</p>
<p>succeeded in doing it.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> So the people are very, very frustrated.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And that's right.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> We have to know — we have to know that President Obama's No. 1 priority is to stop the leak.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But I'm assuming that it is. And I am assuming that</p>
<p>it is. But, look, the reason I'm pleased to have you on the program</p>
<p>tonight is that there is not a governor in the United States who has</p>
<p>more experience than you do dealing with the oil companies. You've</p>
<p>already said you can't believe them, that their word doesn't mean much</p>
<p>when you are debating issues as far as the oil company's interest and</p>
<p>the interest of the people. You can't believe them. OK. Now, the oil</p>
<p>company BP says we don't know how to stop the leak. We're going to try</p>
<p>X. We're going to dig another well. We're going to do this. We don't</p>
<p>know. We don't know. Obama obviously doesn't know how to stop the leak.</p>
<p>Do you know how to stop it?</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> Well, then what the federal government should have</p>
<p>done was accept the assistance of foreign countries, of entrepreneurial</p>
<p>Americans who have had solutions…</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Who?</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> …that they wanted presented.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Who?</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> They can't even get a phone call returned, Bill. The</p>
<p>Dutch. They are known in the Norwegian. They are known for — for dikes</p>
<p>and for cleaning up water and for dealing with spills. They offered to</p>
<p>help and, yet, no, they too, with a proverbial can't even get a phone</p>
<p>call back. That is what the Norwegians are telling us, and the Dutch</p>
<p>are telling us. And then the entrepreneurial Americans, the company in</p>
<p>Maine that has the boom and the absorbents, those companies that are</p>
<p>waiting for the Obama administration eight weeks later for the</p>
<p>regulators to come in and say, OK, we'll purchase from you now. We'll</p>
<p>do all that we can. That's where some of the frustration is.</p>
<p>Now, we saw the same thing though with Katrina, didn't we? So, I'm</p>
<p>not going to point fingers and make this a partisan issue at all, point</p>
<p>fingers at different administrations. But it is that inherent problem</p>
<p>that we have with government, not necessarily being prepared, because</p>
<p>our priorities in government are wrong. National security, safety of</p>
<p>the people, needs to be the top priority. That's where we need to be</p>
<p>funding instead of funding these other things on the periphery that</p>
<p>really just get in the way of the private sector's progress, their</p>
<p>ability to produce and to thrive and to prosper, Instead, our</p>
<p>priorities in the national government have been screwed up.</p>
<p>Now, as governor of Alaska, what I did in dealing with the oil</p>
<p>companies, and I'll bet you, 75 percent of my time was being taken up</p>
<p>by energy issues here in this state. I had to set up our Petroleum</p>
<p>Systems Integrity Office so that we could be there on the front lines</p>
<p>making sure what the oil companies were telling us was legit, when they</p>
<p>were dealing with their corroded pipes that we found out and other lax</p>
<p>maintenance issues.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Good point.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> It took us putting that as the highest priority to</p>
<p>protect our resources, to protect our environment, including not just</p>
<p>the physical environment but the human environment here.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. Well, that's a good point. There wasn't</p>
<p>anybody from the management service or whatever — Mineral Management</p>
<p>Service out on the pipeline checking it out. That's for sure.</p>
<p>Hey, Governor, always a pleasure. Thanks for coming on.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> Now, but Bill…</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Go ahead, real quick.</p>
<p><strong>PALIN:</strong> OK. We can't — we can't afford though to — we can't</p>
<p>afford to demonize these energy producers to such an extent though that</p>
<p>they go under. We do need to work with them though, but we need to</p>
<p>verify everything it is and hold them accountable for all that they</p>
<p>have done in this situation.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right, governor. Thanks again.</p>
