			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Congressman Tells Why He Jumped the Democratic Ship</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-08-12T16:07-04:00">Published August 12, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <strong>&quot;Hannity &amp; Colmes,&quot;</strong> August 10, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;Hannity &amp; Colmes&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 9 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>ALAN COLMES, CO-HOST</strong>: In a political move that has angered Democrats and sent shock waves through the House of Representatives, Louisiana Congressman&nbsp;<b>Rodney Alexander</b> (<a href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Rodney%20Alexander" target="_blank">search</a>)&nbsp;jumped across the aisle and switched parties.</p>
<p>The one-time Democrat is the newest member of the Grand Old Party.</p>
<p>Joining us now for the exclusive first interview, the newest Republican congressman, Rodney Alexander.</p>
<p><strong>SEAN HANNITY, CO-HOST:</strong> Woo-hoo!</p>
<p><strong>COLMES:</strong> Congressman, let me put a couple of words on the screen that you've been called by your former fellow Democrats: coward, turncoat, traitor and confused. What's your reaction to that?</p>
<p><strong>SEN. RODNEY ALEXANDER, R-LA.:</strong> I don't comment on the first ones. Confused, I was. But I'm not anymore.</p>
<p><b>COLMES:</b> All right. Congressman, look, I don't have a problem. People have the freedom to switch parties in this country. I criticize you for the way you did it.</p>
<p>You waited until 20 minutes before the end of the filing deadline to switch parties after saying you were proud to be a Democrat shortly before that, giving the Democrats very little chance to put up an opponent.</p>
<p>And that's why Democrats are angry at you. Not just that you switched. It's the timing of it. It's how you did it.</p>
<p><b>ALEXANDER:</b> Well, in the first place, it was more than 20 minutes. It was on the last day.</p>
<p>But I qualify, in Louisiana, we have three days to qualify, from 8 to 5, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday. I qualified on Wednesday as a Democrat. And another individual qualified as a Republican.</p>
<p>And on Thursday evening, another individual qualified as a Democrat. And at that time, I began to understand that the base that I desperately needed to win re-election perhaps wasn't there and as solid as I had expected.</p>
<p>Because, for several months now, I have been defending my vote within my own party. Because I'm pro-life. I'm pro-family. I'm pro-gun. I'm for a strong defense. I'm for the good things...</p>
<p><b>COLMES:</b> So are many Democrats, Congressman. You know, but a lot of people gave you money, including the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee. They poured money into your first campaign. They poured hundreds of thousands of dollars.</p>
<p>Other people gave you money, believing you were standing up for them as a Democrat. And those people, don't they have a right to be offended by pouring money into you, putting you where you are today and then have you go in the other direction?</p>
<p><b>ALEXANDER:</b> There were several people that put money into my campaign. But a lot of people put money into my campaign, because I'm Rodney Alexander and I haven't let them down.</p>
<p>I have served as a Democrat for two years now. And represented my district as well as I could. I have worked as hard as I know how, 100 percent into my district.</p>
<p>The people of Louisiana are the ones that I represent and will continue to do so.</p>
<p>If there are those — and I have made the statement that if my Democratic colleagues — that have given to me this election cycle and the Democratic leader PACs, I will refund that money.</p>
<p>And if there are others out there who gave to me under the impression that I was going to always stay a Democrat and were not giving that money to Rodney Alexander, then I will refund that money if they will let me know.</p>
<p><b>HANNITY:</b> That's fair. Congressman, welcome to the right side. We're glad to have you. Welcome aboard.</p>
<p>You know, I love how liberals lecture us about being pro-choice, but they won't give us choice in schools, won't give us choice about gun rights, won't give us a whole variety of choices.</p>
<p>You made a choice because it was right for you and you're called a coward, a traitor, confused. You said it hurts like the Dickens. I bet that does hurt.</p>
<p><b>ALEXANDER:</b> Well, it does. I understand the frustration, because you know, there were those who felt like the Democrats would regain the majority in the House.</p>
<p>And because I did what I did, well, it weakened those chances. But I did what I did because I wanted to do what I could do for the best of my district.</p>
<p><b>HANNITY:</b> You also said you're ashamed of what the Democratic Party now stands for.</p>
<p>One of the things I noticed at the convention is, I didn't see&nbsp;<b>Evan Bayh</b> (<a href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Evan%20Bayh" target="_blank">search</a>)&nbsp;there at all. I barely saw Joe Lieberman.</p>
<p><b>Zell Miller</b> (<a href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Zell%20Miller" target="_blank">search</a>)&nbsp;is no longer wanted in this party anymore and I go look at Zell Miller's voting record. He's a Kennedy Democrat, JFK Democrat. He's in the tradition of Scoop Jackson, Truman, JFK and all these guys.</p>
<p>You seem to be the real Democrat. If I was you, I'd be proud of what you did here and don't let their name stabbing or their backstabbing or their mean spiritedness bother you. I promise you, we have open arms here in the Republican Party. We're a diverse party.</p>
<p><b>ALEXANDER:</b> Well, thank you. And again, I understand the frustration. In Washington, there's competition for that leadership roles. And I hate that some of my senators back home have had bad things to say about me. And I understand.</p>
<p><b>HANNITY:</b> Don't worry about Mary Landrieu. I'll talk to her.</p>
<p><b>COLMES:</b> Some people weren't that kind of Jim Jeffords when he went the other way. Thank you very much for being with us, sir.</p>
<p><b>ALEXANDER:</b> Sure.</p>
