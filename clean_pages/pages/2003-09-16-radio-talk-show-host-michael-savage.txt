			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Radio Talk Show Host Michael Savage</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2003-09-16T13:07-04:00">Published September 16, 2003</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;September&nbsp;12, 2003. <strong>Click</strong> <a target="_blank" href="http://fn.emediamillworks.com/fox/"><strong>here</strong></a> <strong>to order the complete transcript.</strong></p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the <em>Personal Story</em> segment tonight, one of the most controversial media people in the country is radio talk-show host Michael Savage heard on more than 300 radio stations across the country.</p>
<p>Mr. Savage also has a bestselling book entitled <em>The Savage Nation</em> and he joins us now from San Francisco.</p>
<p>You know, we're both on the same radio station out in the Bay City, KNEW, and I was out there a few weeks ago driving around, and I heard you attacking about the war -- secular war that we've been talking about here on <em>The Factor</em>, and you think that it's almost a much bigger orchestrated campaign than I've been defining.&nbsp; Why don't you give us your vision on that?</p>
<p><strong>MICHAEL SAVAGE, RADIO TALK-SHOW HOST</strong>:&nbsp; Well, the argument is being presented as though Christianity is not the issue, it's the issue of a few atheists or Buddhists or Hindus who are offended by the cross.&nbsp; That may be true, but that's not the larger picture.</p>
<p>I'm sorry -- you and I probably disagree on this -- in advance.&nbsp; To me, the rated radical left is attacking the church.&nbsp; They're attacking religion.&nbsp; They want religion wiped out.&nbsp; Now I don't mean burn the churches.</p>
<p>We keep hearing about separation of church and state.&nbsp; Well, first of all, we all know that's not written in the Constitution.&nbsp; The Constitution says there shall be no establishment of religion by Congress, but that means that there should be no disestablishment of a religion by Congress, meaning Congress has to remain neutral on the issue.</p>
<p>So, if a man is a Buddhist and wants to have a Buddha in his courtroom, I suppose he could have a Buddha for comfort.&nbsp; If he wants to wear a cross on his neck or she wants to wear a cross for his comfort or her comfort, that's a religious belief.&nbsp; If a Jewish person wants to wear a skull cap in court as a judge, that's his religious right.&nbsp; It's his way of saying God is above me.</p>
<p>Now the issue is to me -- and you're -- it's cultural Marxism.&nbsp; What do I mean by that?&nbsp; What did the early communists do when they took over Russia?&nbsp; They overthrew the church.&nbsp; There could not be a strong church with a strong state.&nbsp; There was tyranny, it's true, from the -- from the czar, but the state wasn't as strong as the czar.&nbsp; Now you have a state, meaning the communists, replacing the church.&nbsp; So they had to knock the church out of the box.</p>
<p>I see the same thing going on here, Bill.&nbsp; That's the fight today.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; I don't see it quite...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I don't -- I don't...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I don't see it quite the way you do, but I'll get to that in a minute.</p>
<p>Now you're not a churchgoing guy.&nbsp; That was another thing that caught my ear as I was riding around.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Not at all.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You don't go to church.&nbsp; You're not a real strong God guy.&nbsp; So why are you so...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Wait, wait.&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; Bill -- Bill -- Bill, I'm a very strong God-believing man.&nbsp; I just don't go to houses of worship, except maybe once or twice a year, because whether you -- well, whether people accept it or not, I am in touch with God all day long.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; That's fine.&nbsp; That's your belief.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I talk to God.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But, you know, very rarely do you get somebody like you who are -- who was so angry -- well, you're an angry -- you know, you do that anyway, I mean, just to get attention.&nbsp; I understand...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Oh, no.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, no, no, no, no.&nbsp; Part of your schtick deal is to overstate.&nbsp; Look...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Well...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You and I talked before.&nbsp; You're not...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; It's wrong.&nbsp; You're wrong.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You're not a stupid man.&nbsp; You've got...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Bill -- there are things worthy of anger, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... a Ph.D.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Bill, Bill, Bill.&nbsp; There are things worthy of anger.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But you overstate -- you overstate to get attention, and that's a legitimate technique.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I disagree.&nbsp; I think you understate to get attention.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, if you...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; That doesn't mean that you're doing it for effect.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... think I'm understated, that just shows how overstated you are!</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Now...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Well, it's a different style.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But, look, let me ask my question.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Let's put it that way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Let me -- let me get my -- I see it as an attempt to Western Europeanize the United States.&nbsp; The secularists want no judgments made about personal behavior at all.&nbsp; You see it as an imposition of an economic system, Marxism, on the country.</p>
<p>See, I don't see that because most of these people driving this love their big, luxury houses, love their luxury cars, and their banks accounts.&nbsp; I don't think they want...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; As the -- as did the Leninists who had the big dahkas (ph) in Russia.&nbsp; Remember there was never really all poor under communism.&nbsp; There was a very, very powerful class of the bureaucrats.&nbsp; They had the houses.&nbsp; They had the chauffeured cars.&nbsp; They had the bodyguards.&nbsp; That's exactly what Leninism brings, and that's where we're going in this country, and that's what worries me.</p>
<p>But you talk about Western Europe or Europeanizing the country.&nbsp; Absolutely.&nbsp; You hear it every day.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, that's what it's about.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; You hear it -- well -- well, that's one of the things that it's about, but part of that is deemphasizing the power of the church.&nbsp; I think you'd have to agree with me.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, I would.&nbsp; I mean the secularists don't want any -- look, why is Mel Gibson being, you know..</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Crucified.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Pardon the pun.&nbsp; Because they don't want this movie to be seen by millions of people to regenerate interest in Jesus who had a strict moral code.&nbsp; They don't want any moral judgments.&nbsp; They want legalization of drugs, gay marriage.&nbsp; You go down the list, no moral judgments.</p>
<p>You want to have two guys making out in front of your 4-year-old?&nbsp; It's OK with them.&nbsp; A guy smoking a joint, blowing the smoke into your little kid's face?&nbsp; OK with them.&nbsp; And I'm not exaggerating here.&nbsp; This is exactly what the secular movement stands for.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Bill, be careful.&nbsp; You're overstating now, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I'm not.&nbsp; I've seen both of those things happen, and...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I have, too.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... I've -- and I've confronted the people who did that...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... and I know how they're thinking.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I raised children in San Francisco, Bill.&nbsp; It was not a pretty thing to walk down the street in this city and be sneered at as breeders by the (UNINTELLIGIBLE).</p>
<p>Now let me say this.&nbsp; We keep hearing about separation of church and state as though it's a reality when it's an invention.&nbsp; Why don't we hear about separation of degeneracy and state?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right, but let me -- let me...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; You go into...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... ask -- let me stop you here.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; You go into a schoolroom.&nbsp; Wait.&nbsp; You go into a schoolroom.&nbsp; They're teaching kids in the third grade how to put a condom on a cucumber.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, that's wrong.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I'd like to talk about that.&nbsp; Why do we hear...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, you do.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; ... throw religion out?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You do, and you've made a lot of money talking about it.&nbsp; But let me -- I want to make one more point.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I have.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; We've got about 90 seconds to go.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I'm a capitalist, by the way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Why do you want to demonize groups?&nbsp; See, I don't -- I don't like it when you say the sodomites.&nbsp; I don't -- I think that's -- that diminishes you.&nbsp; See, there are good gays and there are bad gays.&nbsp; There are good...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; Well, I don't even want to talk about that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... straights and bad straights.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; It's the most boring topic on the earth, is the homosexual issue.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, but you use inflammatory words like...</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; It's boring.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... &quot;sodomites.&quot;&nbsp; Why?</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; I don't care -- I love -- all right.&nbsp; You've asked me.&nbsp; I'll answer you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Good.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; OK?&nbsp; I don't care what people do in the privacy of their own homes.&nbsp; I've said that a thousand times.&nbsp; As long as children are left alone, as long as children are not proselytized, I don't care.&nbsp; It's not my business, hetero or homo or trisexual or bisexual or asexual.&nbsp; It doesn't matter to me.&nbsp; I think sexuality is boring, and I think it's very, very personal.&nbsp; Not an important topic to me.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Did you just say sexuality is boring?</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You've been working too much.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Michael Savage, there you go.</p>
<p>SAVAGE:&nbsp; You got me there.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Thanks a lot.&nbsp; We appreciate it.</p>
