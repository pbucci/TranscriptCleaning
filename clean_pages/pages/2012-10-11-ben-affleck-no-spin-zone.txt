			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Ben Affleck in no spin zone</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2012-10-10T00:00-04:00">Published October 10, 2012</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="1892839738001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/v/1892839738001/ben-affleck-enters-no-spin-zone" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/101012_of_affleck_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>
							<div class="caption">
								<p itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization" itemscope="" itemprop="sourceOrganization"><span itemprop="name" class="source"></span></p>

						<p></p>
<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; October 10, 2012. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: &quot;Unresolved Problems&quot; segment tonight, what to do about Iran. Looks like Israel will not take military action until after the presidential election.</p>
<p>But it's clear the Israelis are not going to allow Iran to develop a nuclear weapon and will use military force against the Iranians if necessary.</p>
<p>Meantime, sanctions against Iran are hurting that country deeply as its currency is falling apart. Actor, Ben Affleck, has directed a film about Iran and I spoke with him yesterday.</p>
<div></div>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE)</p>
<p>So, you make a movie about a time in history that a lot of people forget, 1979, Iran has American hostages, all right.</p>
<p>BEN AFFLECK, ACTOR AND DIRECTOR: Indeed.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: It's really hurting the Carter administration. It's one of the reasons he lost to Ronald Reagan.</p>
<p>But why do you, a guy who wasn't even conscious at that time, why do you want to make a movie like this.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: You know, it was such a great story. For one thing, it's a thriller. It's actually comedy with the Hollywood satire. It's a complicated CIA movie, it's a political movie. And it's all true.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. So, you wanted to make an entertainment though about a serious subject, rescuing Americans from a dictatorship, Iran.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I did. And the serious aspect of it was that this is really a tribute to the folks and our clan that's in services, and diplomats in the foreign service who are risking their lives over there, tragically seeing examples of that very recently.</p>
<p>And folks who are -- what they give up to serve us and to serve our country.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: This is a Valentine from Ben Affleck to the Intelligence Community. The same people who water-boarded, the same people who renditioned. So, what are your liberal friends going to say to you.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I don't worry too much about what my liberal friends are going to say, you know. To me, I made a movie that my friends who are Democrats and my friends who are Republicans can both watch. It's not a political movie.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, you didn't, in the back of your mind, you didn't say, &quot;Look, I'm glorifying some people who, maybe, did bad things in the name of the country to protect it.&quot;</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I've been to the CIA. I met General David Petraeus. These are extraordinary honorable people at the CIA. Make no mistake about it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. Sounds good to hear. All right, now, Iran, again, influencing presidential election because of the nuke thing.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Well, I'm worried about it in the sense that everyone has obviously a trouble spot. This is the same regime that is in my movie in 1979 with Khomeini. Now, there's Khomeini. It's the same Islamist regime.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Same mentality, right.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: And we're still dealing with them. And if they got a bomb, I think everybody thinks that would be trouble. And I'm just an amateur pundit. But my feeling about it is, you know, one has to also be judicious. Frankly, I don't think there's a lot of --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But what does judicious mean. I mean, look, here is your choice. You strangle them with sanctions, which seems to be working right now, all right.</p>
<p>Because the rial is collapsing, their currency.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: They're in bad trouble.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Or you give Netanyahu the OK to go in if it reaches a certain point and whack them.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: My understanding is that Netanyahu is not -- or Israel is not entirely capable of whacking them to the extent in which they need to be whacked.</p>
<p>And I wouldn't outsource U.S. foreign policy to any other government. I would be judicious in the sense that the higher percentage of Americans don't want to see another war.</p>
<p>And one, I think, ought to be careful before one engages in war. However, we have to have a line beyond which we say this is not acceptable in Iran. So, it's --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. So, if that line were crossed by Iran and they were very close, you wouldn't oppose military action to stop their nuclear program.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I wouldn't oppose military action, but if the question is where the line is --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's always the question.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: and I'd certainly wouldn't make the line public and I wouldn't be backed into the line.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. Now, you're a democrat, big Obama supporter this time around.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I like Obama. On the major issues, I think Obama has done a good job. I'm going to vote for him.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: If you had to point to one thing about President Obama that he's accomplished that has impressed you, what would it be.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Well, I mean, accomplished --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Pick one.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: -- makes it sound tricky because he's advocated -- and he's tried to do a number of things and he's been stymied. His major policy goal of changing the tax structure so that the wealthy pay their fair share, to me, is of towering importance. I think the surplus was --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Do you believe in income redistribution. Do you believe in --</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Anytime we change the tax code doesn't necessarily have to be income redistribution --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, no. But I mean, if it's taken from the guys like you and me.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: -- coming from the images of Eugene Debs and socialism, I mean, that's not what I'm talking about --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, no, no.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: -- changing the tax code a little bit.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But there is a philosophy among liberal Americans. And you and I are fat cats. We're one percenters.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Indeed.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. So, you know, the philosophy is, &quot;Well, Affleck and O'Reilly, they can pony up 40, 45 percent of what they got and we'll spread it around.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I thought we were just going to return to the pre-Bush tax cut numbers. But, yes, I agree, you and I -- I don't know, you know, what your nut looks like, but I don't spend so much that I can't afford to pay a little bit more in taxes.</p>
<p>And I think that's important. I think the stimulus was a good thing by and large. I think it was absolutely necessary. I think it saved us from getting deeper into recession. But I'm not here to give you Obama talking points, you know.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, no, no. Listen, we're interested in you and what you think. Last question, if the President loses to Mitt Romney -- because you're a Massachusetts guy --</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Yes, I am.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- and he was the Governor over the base state --</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Indeed.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- are you going to --</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I know Mitt Romney. I met him. Very pleasant guy, very nice guy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- so, you're not going to move out of the country or something. You'd be all right. You'd be OK.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>AFFLECK: I hope not.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. You'd be all right.</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Do I have to have to leave the country. I don't think there's -- I think President Obama is in good shape. I would put money on President Obama.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You'd put money. How much money you want to put on it. Because I got Dick Morris back here. He'll --</p>
<p>AFFLECK: Not Mitt Romney money. I can't even mention $10,000.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. The movie is &quot;Argo.&quot; Ben Affleck, we appreciate it.</p>
<p>(END VIDEOTAPE)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And &quot;Argo&quot; has gotten great reviews. It opens on Friday.</p>
