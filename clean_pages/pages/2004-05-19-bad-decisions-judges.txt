			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Bad Decisions by Judges</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-05-19T17:14-04:00">Published May 19, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;May 18, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;In the &quot;Unresolved Problems Segment&quot; tonight, bad decisions by judges.&nbsp; This evening on the CBS TV program &quot;Judging Amy,&quot; I appear and scold Judge Amy for her ruling regarding a violent teenager.&nbsp; Obviously the show is fiction, but the problem is real.</p>
<p>In Florida, Judge O.H. Eaton sentenced Mark Vansandt to 10 days probation after Vansandt pleaded guilty to having sexual intercourse with a 12-year-old girl.&nbsp; He was 20 years old at the time.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Judge Eaton said, &quot;(Vansandt) is not a pedophile.&nbsp; He's not a rapist.&nbsp; He's not a violent sex offender.&quot;&nbsp; The judge went on to say the man was simply &quot;immature.&quot;</p>
<p>Joining us now from Orlando is Norman Wolfinger, who is with the prosecutors office and knows the judge and the 12-year-old girl's mother, whose identity we are protecting for obvious reasons.&nbsp;</p>
<p>So madam, when you heard 10 days probation for the guy who raped your child, statutory rape, what did you think?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;, MOTHER OF SEXUAL ASSAULT VICTIM:&nbsp; I was outraged.&nbsp; I just couldn't believe that he could just get a little slap on the hand for what he did.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Did you address the court at all before the sentencing?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; I attempted to address the court, but the judge kind of interrupted me and told me I couldn't say things like that even though my statement was looked over and I really don't believe he even listened to me.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Do you have any opinion on why Judge Eaton gave such a light sentence in such a heinous crime?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; My opinion, I don't think he cares about children.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, I concur, because we have a couple of other cases here.&nbsp; Now tell us about your daughter.&nbsp; She's 14 years old now?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; Yes, she is.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What effect did this have on her life?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; My daughter has lost all her self-esteem, she has been violent towards me because she thought I was betraying her for turning Mark in.&nbsp; It has been a very hard battle these last two years with her.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; She evidently consented to the sex, not that that matters, it doesn't, but the judge took that into consideration I guess, right?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; I don't see how a 12-year-old can consent to having sex, but yes, that's what they say.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Now is your daughter getting counseling, is she going to be able to get through this do you think?</p>
<p>&quot;MARGARET&quot;:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; I think she's going to make it through.&nbsp; She has been getting counseling, and since the sentence was made, she's really opened up her eyes with all the outcry of how the public is so upset about it.&nbsp; And she has really started to realize what was done wrong to her.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; You're having a little trouble with your ear piece.&nbsp; That's OK.&nbsp; We'll go on to counselor Wolfinger.&nbsp; You know this Eaton guy, and I mean, I've got three cases right here, we're going to do one tomorrow night where a 5-year-old was killed and this Eaton guy gave the killer, the stepfather, five years in prison.&nbsp; What's the matter with this guy, Judge Eaton?&nbsp; What's the matter with him?</p>
<p>NORMAN WOLFINGER, PROSECUTOR:&nbsp; Well, I would say he's a very intelligent judge but sometimes we see things through a different window I believe.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, explain that.&nbsp; I mean, nobody watching tonight, Mr. Wolfinger, is going to agree with giving a 10-day probation sentence to a 20-year-old who rapes a 12-year-old, and a five-year sentence to a guy who kills a 5-year-old.&nbsp; There's nobody watching this broadcast who is going to agree with that, sir, so there has got to be something the matter with the judge.&nbsp;</p>
<p>WOLFINGER:&nbsp; Well, you know, this is one of those sentences where you just have to say to yourself, what are you thinking?&nbsp; What are you thinking on this?&nbsp; And certainly at the same time he was sentenced to consecutive four years probation for a possession of cocaine case, so what we're dealing with is 10 days sexual offender probation for lewd and lascivious activity, a 20-year-old with a 12-year-old, and you know, basically a meaningless sexual offender sentence because you can't complete the conditions.&nbsp; It's an impossibility.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, because after 10 days he's out of the state supervision.&nbsp;</p>
<p>WOLFINGER:&nbsp; Well, no, no he's not.&nbsp; He has four more years as a drug...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; The cocaine, yes, but look, look, that's not the point.&nbsp; There's supposed to be crime and punishment in this country.&nbsp; There is supposed to be justice.&nbsp; Now we're looking at this guy now.&nbsp; Has anybody from your office confronted Judge Eaton or said, what the hell are you doing?</p>
<p>WOLFINGER:&nbsp; Many times.&nbsp; We're going to be appealing this like we have cases in the past.&nbsp; We've been in touch with the attorney general.&nbsp; I've been in touch myself with the legislature today.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Is there any outrage about this judge down there?</p>
<p>WOLFINGER:&nbsp; You know, there has been in the -- you know, we do have times where we fight him over sentences and what we're left to do is appeal.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; But is there any public outcry about it?</p>
<p>WOLFINGER:&nbsp; Some, but you know...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Well, after tomorrow, there will be more because we're going to have another story about a murder that this judge botched.&nbsp; And you know, we got rid of the other judge in Sarasota, maybe we can do the same here.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Madam, we're sorry for your daughter.&nbsp; Counselor, thanks for helping us out.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; In my opinion, Judge Eaton is a danger and we'll have that story tomorrow and we'll be right back.&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<p></p>
