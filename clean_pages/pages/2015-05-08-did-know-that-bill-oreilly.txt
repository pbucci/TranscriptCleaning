						<p><i>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; May 7, 2015. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.<br /> Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</i></p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>&quot;Did you know that?&quot; segment tonight, I hope you'll pardon this upcoming exercise. As you may know, we feature FOX News correspondents and contributors telling you about their backgrounds. Well, tonight it's my turn.</p> 
<p><b>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</b></p> 
<p><b>ARTHEL NEVILLE, CO-HOST, &quot;AMERICA'S NEWS HEADQUARTERS&quot;: </b>I want to go back to third grade. If you can remember that far back? Something you're doing caused the sister Lorena and sister Mary to say William, you are a bold fresh piece of humanity.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's he where it all started. I was a little thug, all right? In St. Bridget School. And me and a couple of others, we just wanted to have a few laughs, so I would kind of bored with it, you know, third grade, so I would just do annoying things and then a nun would come down and call us names and give us a little slap with the eraser, whatever it is. I know this is interesting with the folks. Number one, when I was ten, my father says you got to work so I cut lawns and I shoveled snow in the winter.</p>
<div></div> 
<p><b>NEVILLE: </b>Yes.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Okay, all right. Then I graduated to Carville, the ice cream stand when I was 16-years-old. And then I got my life guard certification, safety instructor.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> And you more than doubled your salary.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>Yes. And I made some pretty good money out of Long Island teaching people to swim and I saved a couple of people. But it wasn't enough. I said, now I got to go to college and now I got to have some money, so we started a painting business. And we actually painting houses, we undercut the union painters which wasn't really smart.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> Right.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But they weren't going to kill us. So, we could run fast. But we painted these houses, and I had crew of four or five, we made humongous money at shoe boxes full of cash. And that put me through spending money and other things through college.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> I want to talk to you about your journalism studies at Boston University.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>My mother went there. She has a physical therapy degree from there and I went there to go to journalism school, get a degree in broadcast journalism and it was the greatest because all the Watergate stuff and the busing was huge in Boston. So I got to cover stories on a freelance basis while I was in school and I would get a little money for writing stories and then hand in those stories as papers to the pin head BU professors, so it worked perfectly and had a great time.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> In the spring of 1969, right? Your friends were accepted to the third year abroad program. Now, you were not accepted at first.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>Well, my third year in college, at Marist College, I was playing football and, you know, I kind of thought I might want to stay and not go. But then one of the guys at Marist said, you know, you're really not smart enough to go abroad anyway.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> Yes.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>So of course I went. And once I got to London and learned that there was something different than Levy Town and Poughkeepsie in New York, and your whole world expands. We had adventures. Europe on five dollars a day. You know? We stayed in Youth Hostels. But I learned more in that year than the other preceding 20 years combined.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> You write in your book &quot;Bold Fresh Piece Humanity&quot; but your core belief you say is that life is a constant struggle between good and evil. This eventually led to you create The Factor. Tell us more about why you created The Factor.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I was up at Harvard at the Kennedy's school -- so I created The Factor up there.</p> 
<p>Hi, I'm Bill O'Reilly. Thank you for watching on our very first day.</p> 
<p>A show that would going to be opinion based on fact. But really feisty and really obnoxious.</p> 
<p><b>UNIDENTIFIED MAN: </b>That's one problem, the second problem is --</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>Let me stop you there. I disagree with you. I disagree.</p> 
<p>I knew that in prime time up against the dancing and the singing and the wrestling that you'd have to have something very provocative, so I put it together and it worked.</p> 
<p><b>UNIDENTIFIED MAN: </b>You wouldn't send your children to this war, Bill.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>My name isn't listed in the army, you don't know what the hell you're talking about.</p> 
<p><b>UNIDENTIFIED MAN: </b>Very good. Congratulations.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>Yes. I mean, I'm a patriot, and don't denigrate the service or I'll boot you right off the set.</p> 
<p>But I think that the folks depend on us. I write The Factor as you know.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE: </b>Uh-mm.</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> We delivered it every night. It's a challenge to stay number one and so I like the challenge.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE: </b>Now, there is the books, the films, other pending productions. Was this part of a strategy?</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY: </b>I think God has a plan for me and I just let it unfold as it happens. And I think that's the advice I give everybody else. Do your best, get into something you like and then things start to unfold.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE:</b> Final question, Bill. What would you like written on your tombstone?</p> 
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> He finally stopped talking.</p> 
<p><b>NEVILLE: </b>Thank you.</p>
