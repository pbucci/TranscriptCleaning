			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">San Francisco Tragedy</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2005-10-21T18:45-04:00">Published October 21, 2005</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> October 20, 2005, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Impact&quot; segment tonight, 23-year-old <b>Lashaun Harris</b> (search) has apparently murdered her three babies by throwing them into <b>San Francisco Bay</b> (search) last night. The three boys are 6, 2 and 1.</p>
<p>Authorities say Ms. Harris said she was hearing voices. The woman is obviously disturbed. That's the body of the 2-year-old. The other two babies are still missing. They have not found them in the bay.</p>
<p>Now the question is, what do we do when we know a parent's abusing his or her child, when we know a parent is mentally ill? Do we intrude?</p>
<p>With us now is <strong>Dr. Robi Ludwig</strong>, a psychotherapist. This is the same old story here with Lashaun Harris. Has the first baby at 17, knocks out two of the babies. Then she's 23 with three children, living in a shelter apparently on medication. Shelter manager says talked to her the day before she threw the babies in the bay. She was fine, everything was fine. And bang, she snaps.</p>
<p>So poverty, mental illness, all of that plays into it.</p>
<p><strong>ROBI LUDWIG, PSYCHOTHERAPIST:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Obviously an extreme case. You don't see these major psy cases too often. But what we do see, and what you see all the time are abusive parents.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> In public.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Mm-hmm.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> So what is our obligation as decent people when we confront that situation?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> I think it's really important that we make the intervention, because you never know if your intervention is going to be the one that actually changes the course of a person's life. We're really more connected to one another than disconnected. And we all pay a price when someone's allowed to grow up in a violent situation. These are the criminals that end up harming us later.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right, let's get specific.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> OK.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You're in the grocery store. And this — it's happened to me, I'm sure it's happened to most of our viewers. You're in a grocery store, OK? You got a little kid, and bang. OK? Hits the kid because the kid's doing something.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And he gets whacked like this. Now what do you do?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> First of all, you have to assess your own level of safety. You don't want to go up to an abusive parent and get killed yourself.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> But if you've assess the situation, and you feel that you...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But you see it, all right?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Yes. It's absolutely our responsibility to intervene and to make sure that the child is safe. That's why we have these protective laws.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right, so what do you do? You're in a grocery store. You see the...</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> You either go up to the individual and say, hey, this is not the way you want to be handling the situation. You need a time out.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And that individual might whack you in the head.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right. So you want to assess that. Or you can go to a manager and say this person is abusing a child. Call the police.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> How about calling the cops? Everybody's got a cell phone.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> That's right. Call the police.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> 911?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Let the police — 911. Let them make an assessment. Or you can call child protective services if you have more information.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right. I had to deal with this one time. This is what I did. It was a guy and a little kid. And the kid was in the way of the guy. And the guy — fists.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You know, if it's a little whack on the behind, I'm not going to intrude. I don't think that's child abuse. But taking your fist...</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> ...blasting the kid, and the kid is, you know.</p>
<p>So I got — immediately, I got some witnesses, some people who were in the aisle. I said, look, I'm going to stop this and you got to watch this. So I had people right there. So it wasn't he said, she said. OK?</p>
<p>And then I went over. I'm bigger than the guy. And said you hit the guy again, I'm going to do it to you. All right? I was very confrontational. Startled him. He stopped. And then I basically went to the store manager. I should have gone to the cops, but I didn't. And it was my — I should have called the police.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> But you would now.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I would now.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Because of education and awareness. And sometimes that's all it takes is letting society, you know, listen. An individual has the power to make a difference. It's our responsibility.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But it is vital that you get other people to see what's happened.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> I think that's a brilliant idea.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You have to.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> That's brilliant.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You have to anywhere, because it can't be just you and him.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY</strong>: You can get in trouble.</p>
<p>Now you live in a neighborhood, a suburban neighborhood. Lashaun Harris is living three doors down from you. You know she's a nut, OK? You know she's unstable.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You know she's got three kids. And you know it's chaos. You know something could happen. What do you do?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> If there's imminent danger, if you really feel this person is in danger of harming a child right now, then you call the police and you say this person...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But there's no crime.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> No. This person is in danger of harming themselves or others and needs to be hospitalized.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> The police aren't going to do that.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Yes, they will.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, they won't.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Yes, I mean...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Not in my neighborhood they won't.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Well, I've called the police and I've said...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You've got to — they've got to see — the police have to see before they can take them into custody.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Well, the other thing you can do is you can call a family member and say I'm very concerned about your family member.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Lashaun Harris?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Does this person have a medical doctor, a psychiatrist, a medical professional?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I don't know. I don't know.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> You can call several people.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> This is the hard one. Number one, they live in the neighborhood so they can take reprisals on you. That's number one. OK?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Well...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Number two, you know, because everybody knows crazy parents in the neighborhood to beat the living daylights out of their kids. Everybody knows that.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> You can call child protective services. It's their job to investigate.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Oh, but that's chaos, you know?</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> It is chaos. Maybe you can do several things all at once. You call a family member, you call people in the community, you call a doctor. You call the police. You do it on several fronts. I mean, that was one of the problems with Andrea Yates. Child protective services was called. They came in, and they didn't follow through.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> I wonder what would have happened...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Happens all the time.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> ...if the doctor was called, the family member was called, the police are called.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> You call a lot of people.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But bottom line on this is we all have a moral obligation if we see a wrongdoing.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> It's got to be a wrongdoing. It can't be a little swat on the behind to intervene.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Right. And the individual has the power — absolutely.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Individual has the power to make the difference.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Thank you, doctor.</p>
<p><strong>LUDWIG:</strong> Thank you.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> We appreciate you coming in, as always.</p>
