			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Talking Points</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">The Carnage Continues in Iraq</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2003-11-04T14:04-05:00">Published November 04, 2003</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>Hi, I'm Bill O'Reilly.&nbsp; Thanks for watching us tonight.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Hi, I'm Bill O'Reilly.&nbsp; Thank you for watching us tonight.</p>
<p>In just a few moments, we'll have a <em>Factor</em> exclusive.&nbsp; I have seen some of that Reagan movie. --&nbsp; And I'll tell you what it's all about.&nbsp; Here's a hint:&nbsp;CBS is in big, big trouble.</p>
<p>But first, the <em>Talking Points Memo</em>, the carnage continues in Iraq.&nbsp; As you know over the weekend, 16 American soldiers were killed when a terrorist shot a helicopter out of the sky.&nbsp; All the polls say the same thing.&nbsp; Millions of Americans are losing faith in the Iraqi situation as they terrorists are doing what they do best, killing and scaring people.</p>
<p>But this is a complicated issue.&nbsp; If America cuts and runs in Iraq, it'll be a huge defeat for us.&nbsp; Terrorism will rise all over the world, as the bad guys realize the U.S.A. can be intimidated.&nbsp; Last week on <em>The Factor</em>, I asked [Democratic Presidential Candidate]<b>&nbsp;Al Sharpton</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Al%20Sharpton">search</a>)&nbsp;what he would do.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p>REV. AL SHARPTON (D), PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATE:&nbsp; We're going to correct a wrong, and would say I will participate in a multilateral correction of the&nbsp;&nbsp; problem.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What does that mean?</p>
<p>SHARPTON:&nbsp; That if we go and have Kofi Annan and the United Nations...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>SHARPTON:&nbsp; ...not Halliburton, not Cheney's friends, lead the redevelopment in Iraq, I think that you can repair Iraq and also bring...&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So you're going to put the U.N. in charge of Iraq, all right?</p>
<p>SHARPTON:&nbsp; Absolutely.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Well, handing things off to the U.N. would mean certain defeat in Iraq.&nbsp; That group certainly couldn't handle the Ba'athist killers.&nbsp; So the Bush administration must fight it out.&nbsp; I mean, you think the U.N. can do that?&nbsp; They failed in&nbsp;<b>Rwanda</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Rwanda">search</a>).&nbsp; They failed in Bosnia.&nbsp; I mean, come on.</p>
<p>The question is are the Bush people employing the right strategies?&nbsp; We'll discuss that in a moment.</p>
<p>But to be fair, most of Iraq is now pacified.&nbsp; According to a joint&nbsp; investigation by <em>Time</em> magazine and ABC News, certainly not pro-Bush partisans, 80 percent of Iraq is now making great strides towards stability.&nbsp; It is on in the pro-Saddam areas in the heart of the country that chaos continues.</p>
<p>Now I was happy to see that investigation because it gives those of us who feel the Iraq action is justified hope.&nbsp; And hope is badly needed right now.&nbsp; Like him or not, President Bush is convinced the war on terror must be fought in Iraq.&nbsp; And since retreat is not a responsible option, I think fair-minded Americans should be as patient as possible.</p>
<p>Six months from now, if helicopters are still being shot out of the sky, President Bush's political future will be in doubt.&nbsp; But if we are able to break the back of terror in Iraq, and that country becomes an ally, the payoff for America is huge.&nbsp; That's what at stake and&nbsp;that's <em>The Memo</em>.</p>
<p><strong>The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day</strong></p>
<p>Time now for &quot;The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day&quot;...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Time now for the &quot;Most Ridiculous Item of the Day.&quot;</p>
<p>Last week, while promoting my new book <em>Who's Looking Out for You?</em> I had this exchange with [PBS talk show host]&nbsp;<b>Charlie Rose</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Charlie%20Rose">search</a>).</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p><strong>CHARLIE ROSE, <em>THE CHARLIE ROSE SHOW</em></strong>:&nbsp; George Clooney was here on this program.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; He hates me, right?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Yes, he does.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I have...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; And I said to him...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And, by the way, I don't have anything against him.&nbsp; I have nothing against him.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; He's a good actor.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He's all right.&nbsp; He's not...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Oh, you loved&nbsp;<b><em>Oceans 11</em></b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Oceans%2011">search</a>).&nbsp; You loved...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He's no Sean Penn.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Sean Penn you love?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He's a brilliant actor.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; He's a brilliant -- he may be our best actor working today.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What else do you want?&nbsp; Clooney doesn't like me.&nbsp; What?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; So Clooney says to me -- I said, &quot;why don't you go on his show?&quot; and he said I -- I want to talk to him, I want to debate him, but I don't want to do it on his show.&nbsp; He said I want to do it on a neutral ground when O'Reilly...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, bring him here.&nbsp; I'll come in with him.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Hello?&nbsp; If I bring Clooney here, you'll be here?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Absolutely.&nbsp; I'll be here with him.&nbsp; Sure.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So it will be interesting to see if Mr. Clooney appears.&nbsp; I do write about him in <em>Who's Looking...</em>, and I hope I was fair.&nbsp; If I wasn't, it would be ridiculous, and perhaps he'll point it out to me.&nbsp; So we'll keep you posted.</p><div class="show-callout oreilly-factor fn2">
