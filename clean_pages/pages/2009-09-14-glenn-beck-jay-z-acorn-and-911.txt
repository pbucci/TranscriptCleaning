			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Glenn Beck on Jay-Z, ACORN and 9/11</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2009-09-14T15:08-04:00">Published September 14, 2009</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; September 11, 2009. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;At Your Beck and Call&quot; segment tonight: an interesting situation involving the rapper Jay-Z. As you may know, last January, on inauguration weekend, he said this.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>JAY-Z, RAPPER:</strong> Never thought I'd say this (EXPLETIVE DELETED). Baby, I'm good. You can keep your (UNINTELLIGIBLE). I don't want no more Bush. No more war, no more Iraq, no more white lies. My president is black.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> However, tonight, Jay-Z is holding a benefit at Madison Square Garden here in New York City to help the families of police officers and firefighters killed on 9/11. So what are we going to make of this guy?</p>
<p>Here now, the always provocative and sometimes very sensitive Glenn Beck. Jay-Z, now, are you buddies with him?</p>
<p><strong>GLENN BECK, HOST, &quot;GLENN BECK&quot;:</strong> Love him.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You love him?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Oh, he is — you know, this might come as a surprise to a lot of listeners or viewers, but I am — I blend in the rap crowd.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I've heard you hang with Snoop Dogg.</p>
<p><a onclick="videoMPlayer('9449626','');return false;" href="#"><b>Click here to watch the segment!</b></a></p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> It's crazy. I don't want to drop names or anything like that. But actually, Bill, I have — you know, he mentioned you on his last...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Did he, in one of his songs?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> You know that, right? This is when he said this ain't black vs. white, my — anyway, he went off to — and said a lot of things. Please tell Bill O'Reilly to fall back.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> It's not 2010 or 1864. I don't know if you're aware of that.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I don't know what it means.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> But are you aware of that?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Good friend of mine that works here at New York, Lisa Paige for 92.3 FM.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Here, your book. Could you show — there's Lisa. There's Jay-Z.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Lisa went, and she — can you show him signing the book? Do you have the other picture? There he is.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> He's signing &quot;Bold Fresh.&quot;</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> He's signing the book. Here it is.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Does he have any clue?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes. Oh, yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> He knew it was me?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Oh, yes. Right here. &quot;Peace, Bill. Jay-Z. Off that.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> &quot;Off that.&quot; What does that &quot;off that&quot; mean?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> I don't know. I believe it's pronounced, &quot;Off THAT.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But you're a rapper guy.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Look, I don't know. Look...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You don't know what it means.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> You won't understand.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Of course.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> I mean, you're not — a lot of people — they look at you and say he might be a guy in rap.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I don't know what they're talking about. I freely admit I have no idea what they're saying. But what are we — what are we supposed to think of this guy who does this little racial rap at the inauguration weekend and then holds a benefit? What do you think? Is he a good guy or what?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> I'm going to come clean with you, Bill. I have no idea who the hell this guy even is.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I think he's a good businessman.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Look, I mean, I'm — I'm tired of the race thing. I don't think the race thing works anymore. You know what I mean? There are racists.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It works in the hip-hop community.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes, whatever. But there are racists. There is real racism. Every time you throw it out meaninglessly...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right. It denigrates the real problem.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes, yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now, ACORN, another scandal, it's like one a day now. This is Washington. Yesterday it was Baltimore. They had to fire two people there. Now we have a guy and a lady going in undercover.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> They fired two people in Baltimore?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> They said yesterday this was defamatory and fraudulent.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> They had to fire them, because they knew you and I were on the case. Now, these guys, the undercover guys, went to Washington to — posing as pimps and prostitutes. Roll the tape.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> You can have a business. She's not going to put on (UNINTELLIGIBLE) that she's doing prostitution.</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> She's not going to put on that. But that's the reason for — that's the reason for us getting her the house.</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> She can be a sole proprietor, and she's making money, not saying that it's those kind of services that she's — whether she wants to sell gold, whether she's on the telephone, whether she's in marketing, whether she provides services as a marketer. Do you understand what I'm saying?</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So they're basically telling them, &quot;You can buy a brothel. Here's how you do it.&quot;</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> What's wrong with that? These people are compassionate. They're just trying to help out.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> The ACORN people have gotten $56 million taxpayer dollars. I'm saying no more money. Not another taxpayer dime goes to this.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> You know what? Look, America, I'm going to announce — tomorrow I'm going to be on the television from 1 until 3 here, covering the March on Washington and the tea parties and the 9/12 project all over the country. And I'm going to announce the next — the next phase. And that is, has the heart of our problems and the thing that we can unite on is corruption.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> This whole system is corrupt.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right. Whole ACORN thing top to bottom, right.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> It's beyond ACORN. You can't talk about fixing health care until you clean up the corruption.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But ACORN itself is a corrupt organization.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes, but you know what?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You and I are investigating the Louisiana connection, the state attorney general down there.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> The Louisiana connection, which also in the same building is ACORN and SEIU.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Who does the president surround himself with? ACORN and SEIU. Well, gee, there's corruption there. We can't take anything.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No more — no more taxpayer money, and if we find out about it…</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Nothing.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right. No more bidding. No more anything. If we find out about it, we're going to expose all the politicians that are involved.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Did you see the ratings last night on this whole network?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes, very high. Very high. You did very well.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> But here's the reason why I bring it up. Did you see the ratings on the other networks? We were the only ones that were covering this story. The New York Times didn't cover it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No, of course not.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Nobody covered it. Nobody covered it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right, today is the anniversary of 9/11. You got a little emotional on your program. I was watching you. A little emotional. Nothing wrong with that. You and Jack Parr. Remember Jack? Way back?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes. I'm not that old.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No. But you were — you were angry and rightfully so that they haven't been able to put the buildings back, right?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Whose fault is that?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> The system's fault. The politicians, the corruption, the special interests. Do you know — do you know there's no investigation? There's nobody calling for an investigation in ACORN. There's nobody calling for an investigation why this country hasn't built a building in eight years on that space. How is that possible in America?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> They're working on it.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> No investigations on any of these things, Bill. But do you know that they just appointed a 12-person panel to look into the Cambridge police officer? A 12-person panel.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> This is about the Gates thing, right?</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes. That's amazing.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> An interesting fact on your program today: that it took 400 days to build the Empire State Building.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> And that includes putting King Kong on top of it.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> I don't know about that. King Kong goes up.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That was hard. Very, very hard.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Very long time.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Four hundred days, ladies and gentlemen, to build the Empire State Building. And it's eight years, and they still don't have anything close.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> Nothing. So Bill, let me ask you this. Are we different Americans? Are we different?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It's the power structure.</p>
<p><b>BECK:</b> It's exactly right, and it's corrupt. And it is — it is in bed with special interests. Because I guarantee you, if you take all that system out, if you take those politicians, the special interests, and all of the — all of the little politically correct groups out of the way, we would have built it with our hands within a year.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right. Beck is down there with a hammer on Sunday, so go see him. Glenn Beck, everybody.</p>
