			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Geraldo Reacts to Young Marine Shot Dead by an Illegal Alien</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2006-12-14T13:29-05:00">Published December 14, 2006</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> December 13, 2006, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> Thanks for staying with us. I'm Bill O'Reilly.</p>
<p>In the &quot;Geraldo&quot; segment this week, the crackdown on illegal immigrants and those who hire them and the shocking, <em>shocking</em> report about the Duke lacrosse rape case.</p>
<p>And here he is, star of the syndicated program, &quot;Geraldo at Large&quot;, Geraldo himself.</p>
<p>Here's his mother, you know, and the lawyer and he was this Marine, and he comes back, and this guy kills her — and you know, and Homeland Security knew that he had this [rap] sheet, and nobody did anything.</p>
<p>If you were the father of that Marine, you'd do the same thing, wouldn't you?</p>
<p><strong>GERALDO RIVERA, HOST, &quot;GERALDO AT LARGE&quot;:</strong> Definitely. But I want to say — I want to say a couple of things I think it's very important for me to say. Because if I don't say it, no one else is going to say it.</p>
<p>I feel terrible for that family and their loss and the two other victims of this <b>Miguel Padilla</b>, who by all accounts is a terrible person. And he's sentenced to die now. His appeals will drag on for a while, because of some complicated issues...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> The Mexican government wants him back. You know all of that.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Well, the Mexican government, like many governments, does not have a death penalty.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> A death penalty, right.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> But I want to say this as crystal clear as I possibly can. I feel sorry for every family of the over 16,000 murder victims in our country. This is not an issue of illegal immigration. This is an issue of a scum bag murderer who killed three people.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But it's more than that. You must see that. If you have a system that will not deport violent people when they shouldn't be here in the first place...</p>
<p><strong>RIVERA:</strong> Oh, I have no objection.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's the story. The system is broken.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> I believe that once an illegal alien falls into the criminal justice system...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right, and they still let him go.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> ... and he does his time, he has to get deported.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> There was no time!</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> He has to get deported.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right. There was no time. Two — two convictions.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> But still — her beef is that he had these processes, and they were misdemeanor convictions.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But they were violent convictions, and they pled him down. They pled him down.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Her beef is that once he was in the criminal justice system then they should have referred...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Booted him out! Right!</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> And on that I have no argument.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> But that's not about the 11 million or more illegal aliens in this country...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No. Nobody is condemning...</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> ... like most meat workers. That is the biggest jive mass bust. I'm telling you, what they did to Swift &amp; Company. I did a documentary in 19 — in 2000, in the year 2000, on the Hispanic burgeoning population. I based — I did every one of those meat-packing plants. I went all through the heartland of this country. Everybody knew who worked at the Swift and Company.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So they have to start punishing them now!</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> And for the government to raid and say they're raiding it because of some other criminal act, that is a bunch of baloney and they should be ashamed of themselves...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Well, wait a minute.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> The people raiding the meat packing plants should have been going after the Miguel Padilla's of the world, these violent people and deporting them.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You've got to do it all. You've got to do it all.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> You have to regulate and normalize these people's existence. The fact that they could work in the Swift and Company plant, every church is a Spanish church in that town. Every restaurant's a Spanish restaurant.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I'm not — I'm not opposed to that.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> They speak Spanish on the streets. Anyone who wants to pretend they didn't know is a hypocrite.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Listen, you have to do it all. You have to figure out a way to normalize it.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> You have to have a comprehensive immigration plan.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You've got to punish Swift and other people who do this.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Swift and Company, closed. The — they produce, I don't know, a huge percentage of our processed meat in this country. They closed for the day. I don't know when they're going to reopen. Who benefits from that? Who benefits? Some P.R. guy at I.C.E., who says, look...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It sends a message to other people.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Do you feel safer that they closed Swift and Company because they're illegal aliens?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Do I feel safer? You know what I feel safer about? That finally the federal government is looking at the border, is looking at the businesses. And I want them to start looking at that.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> This is Iowa.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It doesn't matter where it is!</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> People have families. They have roots there.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Everybody has family.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Do you what it's like to work in a meat packing plant? Do you know what it's like to kill those cows?...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Geez, I can't believe you.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> And carve those steaks? Do you know why they're working there, they're almost exclusively illegal? Because no citizen will take that crappy job.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's bull. I don't believe that for a second.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> It is not, Bill, why do you think they're doing it?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Because Mexico can't provide them with jobs. That's why!</p>
<p><strong>RIVERA:</strong> But United States unemployment is four percent. Where are you going to find people to fill those jobs? You can't. You can't.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Then they come here legally. Then they come here legally.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Build your seven foot wall for seven hundred billion dollars.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I'll put you in charge and you can be in charge of the guest worker program.</p>
<p><strong>RIVERA:</strong> I should be!</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Let them come here legally.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> I'd throw Padilla's behind out of this country. These hard-working people deserve these jobs.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You are basically saying to every person south of the border come on here and come here anyway you can.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> The Minuteman and the nativists who are against these people, do you want this job? I'm going to give you $40,000. Do you want to cut the heads off cows? Do you want that job?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You can do that legally. You can set up the program where they would get those jobs.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> You know why the union is fighting for them? Because they are the union. And that's why...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I know that. They want them as members.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Those people should have green cards. They are vital to the national security. No one else wants that job.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> If it were your program this thing would never stop. And it's got to stop.</p>
<p>All right. Now we've got to get to Duke.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now this is in the Raleigh...</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Terrible, Duke. What happened is terrible.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes. This is in the Raleigh newspaper today: DNA found on the rape accuser.— And we know this is a bogus case now, we've said this for months. — Does not watch the three defendants. It's over, right?</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> It should have been over when they had the rigged lineup.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> The photo lineup where only suspects were included. There are constitutional infirmities. This case cannot survive even if it got a conviction, could not survive appeal.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> This should have been dropped. The fact that you have now potential prosecutorial misconduct. What is it? A DNA test result that was not given to the defense. It is fundamental justice in our country...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Can we arrest and go after <b>Nifong</b>? Can we put him in jail? Can we do that?</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Well, I don't know who's responsible for suppressing the information.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It's Nifong, is the guy.</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Maybe it was the police investigator. I mean, it certainly deserves someone to check it out.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But say it's Nifong. Say Nifong held back the DNA report...</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Intentionally?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes. —that clears the three kids. Would you put him in jail for that?</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> I would argue — I would argue that...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Can you and Greta get him in jail? You're both attorneys. Can you and Greta get the guy in jail?</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> That would be a violation of the criminal code of North Carolina.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I want this Nifong in jail.</p>
<p>And I want you and Greta to put him there. All right? That's not too much to ask?</p>
<p><b>RIVERA:</b> Not so much. As long as you let these other people stay in the country and do their jobs.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes, everybody, Geraldo. And they'll all live at your house.</p>
