			<h1 itemprop="headline">Watters' World: Lady Gaga edition</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2014-05-19T00:00-04:00">Published May 19, 2014</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/3576420042001/watters-world-lady-gaga-edition" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/051914_factor_watters_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; May 19, 2014. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: In &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, &quot;Watters' World,&quot; The Lady Gaga Edition. I know, I know you don't care. But there's an interesting story here.</p>
<p>A young woman rises up to become a pop superstar, makes millions, then kind of flames out, leaving behind some very devoted fans, Americans who are obsessed with her.</p>
<p>So, all kind of spooky. So, we sent a spooky guy, Jesse Watters, out to clarify.</p>
<div></div>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE REPORTER: The Gaga hype machine is in full effect.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE REPORTER: People want Gaga to be an instant success.</p>
<p>(CHEERS)</p>
<p>LADY GAGA, POP SUPERSTAR: Thank you so much for buying the tickets to see our show tonight.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>JESSE WATTERS, FOX NEWS CORRESPONDENT: What's going on, my man.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: Just waiting in line for the one and only Lady Gaga.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>ROBERT DE NIRO, ACTOR: Are you gonna propose. Because if you're not, quit staring at me.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Yes, I'm dancing, arms up.</p>
<p>WATTERS: OK. Don't do that again.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Oh.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Did you do a lot of narcotics.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: No.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you know what narcotics are.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: No.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>MORGAN FREEMAN, ACTOR: I'll be damned.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you on acid right now.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: No, nope. No drugs.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you sure those pants aren't just a little bit too tight.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: I mean, I think they look fine.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you sure.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: Yes.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>JULIE LOUIS-DREYFUS, ACTRESS: They're painted on.</p>
<p>JERRY SEINFELD, ACTOR: You're walking like Frankenstein.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: What do you guys do.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 3: I actually work in Medieval Times.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>JANEANE GAROFALO: Welcome to Medieval Times. I'll be your serving wench, Melinda.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: You don't look like a typical Lady Gaga fan.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: Because I just like the jeans and T-shirt.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: You don't look so good.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: Oh, what, what, what, what, what, what, what, what.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: I could do a lot of damage.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you like women.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: You could say I'm a lesbian.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>JASON BATEMAN, ACTOR: I don't know what that means.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you have a boyfriend.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: Yes, he's right there.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That's my cameraman.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: No.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: Ewww, no.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Ewww?</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: No.</p>
<p>WATTERS: He's a handsome man.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WILL FERRELL, ACTOR: The room is starting to spin real fast because of gayness.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: I'm really scared now. What does Lady Gaga mean to you.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: She's my second mom.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 6: She shows me like some of life lessons that no one really get to teach you.</p>
<p>WATTERS: What life lessons has Lady Gaga taught you.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 6: Umm, oh my, God.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: She stands for equality of all the gays. And I think that we need gay marriage here all throughout America.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Did Lady Gaga bring you out of the closet.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: I was well on my way but she definitely helped.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: You know something, I believe you.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Bill O'Reilly, wants to ask you a quick question. This will be fun.</p>
<p>CONAN O'BRIEN, TBS HOST: I'm late for rehearsal.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE REPORTER: I've noticed a certain resistance to Gaga so far this year.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE REPORTER: We have some indication that Mother Monster's star power may be on the decline.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Lady Gaga has been struggling recently. She's not as famous. Her album sales are down. Why are you still attracted to her.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Because music is not for money. Music is for talent and for art.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Canceling the last tour kind of hurt her a little bit.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: Gaga has been doing whatever she wanted since the very beginning. And if the mainstream accepts it, they do. And if they don't, there's all these fans that love it.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>(APPLAUSE AND LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you think Lady Gaga is a flash in the pants.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: I don't think so.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you a flash in the pants.</p>
<p>(CRICKET SOUNDS)</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you ever watch the news or anything like that.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: Only to check the weather, so I'll know what I'll wear for that day.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you a Republican or Democrat.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: I don't know.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you know the difference.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: Umm, no.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you think that Democrats are going to put someone on the Benghazi Committee.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>CHRIS FARLEY, ACTOR: This is a little awkward.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: How's Obama doing.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: Who.</p>
<p>WATTERS: The President.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: Of what.</p>
<p>WATTERS: America.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: Oh.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>SYLVESTER STALLONE, ACTOR: Embarrassing, you know.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: I like to live in my bubble of ignorance bliss.</p>
<p>WATTERS: OK. How's that working out for you.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: I've got my own problems.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you a FACTOR fan.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: I am a FACTOR fan.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes? What's your favorite part of the show.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: Probably when he makes fun of everybody.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I'm Watters and you're in my world right now.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 6: Oh, really.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes. How does that feel.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 6: Pretty amazing.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Ugh.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>All right, here's Watters. Is this just a gay thing. Is that what it primarily is now.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I think it's about half of its gang.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes?</p>
<p>WATTERS: There's a lot of gender-bending and transsexuals.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Because there performers like -- Cher is one --</p>
<p>WATTERS: Sure.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- that attract big gay audience.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: But is this what this is evolving into --</p>
<p>WATTERS: I mean, I don't know if it's evolving. I think it's always been a big gay-themed crowd.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Because -- but she was real mainstream with Bad Romance and all those songs.</p>
<p>WATTERS: She was. And, you know, I like Lady Gaga. I like that kind of music. I'm not embarrassed to talk about it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You should be.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>No, some of her songs are catchy but --</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- but it looks to me like that's over, the mask adulation.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: As you said, her album sales and ticket sales are down.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Right. She's down, I think from 4.58 million albums sold in 2008 to only just about 700,000 --</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, she's down.</p>
<p>WATTERS: -- last year. So, she's had some hip surgery. You know, the shock value is wearing off.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Miley Cyrus has kind of outpaced her in terms of that.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>So, we'll see where she goes from here.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You'll see.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I will -- I will --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, I'm not really going to follow her but you keep me posted.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That's my beat. I'm on a Lady Gaga beat.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You know, you're getting real popular in certain areas there, Watters. You know you are.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Which areas are you talking about.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I'm not even going to get into it. All right, there he is.</p>
