			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Michael Reagan on Politics, His Father and His New Book</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-10-13T16:51-04:00">Published October 13, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>

	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'REILLY Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> Oct. 12, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> &nbsp;&nbsp;In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, as you may or may not know,&nbsp; Michael Reagan was adopted by Ronald Reagan and his first wife, actress&nbsp; Jane Wyman.&nbsp; The couple subsequently divorced, and Michael stayed with his&nbsp; mother.</p>
<p>Mr. <b>Reagan</b> grew up to become a powerful radio voice in America as well&nbsp; as a best-selling author with strong opinions on the state of the union.&nbsp;&nbsp; His new book is called &quot;Twice Adopted.&quot;</p>
<p>And Michael Reagan joins us now from Los Angeles.</p>
<p><strong>MICHAEL REAGAN, AUTHOR, &quot;TWICE ADOPTED&quot;</strong>:&nbsp; Good to see you, Bill.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Let's talk a little politics first.&nbsp; You just heard Lanny&nbsp; Davis say — and I believe it's true — that Americans are conflicted,&nbsp; divided over Iraq, and nobody can definitively say who has the better plan,&nbsp; Bush or Kerry, because it's a hope on both parts.&nbsp; How do you see it?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Well, it's interesting.&nbsp; Everybody wants to see a plan.&nbsp; It's&nbsp; like the president of the United States should write down a plan, show&nbsp; America, get the approval of America and go forward.</p>
<p>I mean, these are the things that Colin Powell works on, Donald&nbsp; Rumsfeld works on, the president works on.&nbsp; You certainly don't want to&nbsp; tell the terrorists what you're about ready to do, and John Kerry knows&nbsp; this, and John Edwards knows this also, and I don't think John Kerry and&nbsp; John Edwards have put together any plan at all except to bring in more&nbsp; people.</p>
<p>There's 30 nations helping the president right now.&nbsp; We've got to win&nbsp; this war.&nbsp; This is what he wants to do, and he knows that it's going to&nbsp; take America to lead the charge.&nbsp; It always takes America to lead the&nbsp; charge, Bill.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, why...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; It would be nice to have somebody else step up, but they&nbsp; don't.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; They don't.&nbsp; I — listen, we all know what geopolitical&nbsp; reality is, and that's why I think the senator, if he keeps pounding the&nbsp; ally thing, is going to hurt himself.</p>
<p>But why should you, Michael Reagan, have any confidence that President&nbsp; Bush will win the war in Iraq or at least turn that country into a decent&nbsp; nation with the WMD fiasco, with the aftermath fiasco?</p>
<p>You know, a lot of independent voters are saying why should I have&nbsp; confidence in an administration that's obviously run into difficulties&nbsp; here?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Well, I think they say they've run into difficulties because&nbsp; that's what the news has really covered.&nbsp; They cover the death that's going&nbsp; on in Iraq, the American military personnel that are dying.</p>
<p>But here in Los Angeles, you know, when they turn on the news at&nbsp; night, I hear about drive-by shootings.&nbsp; Do I leave L.A.?&nbsp; Do I think L.A.s&nbsp; gone to hell and I should never live here again?&nbsp; No.&nbsp; There's a lot of&nbsp; great things going on, too, that are not covered.</p>
<p>I have trust in this president to follow through in his promise to&nbsp; those that died on September 11, that we're going to get the people&nbsp; responsible for this, we're going to get Osama bin Laden, but we don't want&nbsp; to have another North Korea sitting in the middle of Baghdad with a Saddam&nbsp; Hussein.&nbsp; You didn't want him to have weapons of mass destruction.</p>
<p>No, they didn't find weapons of mass destruction, but Saddam Hussein&nbsp; fooled the world.&nbsp; He didn't just fool maybe our CIA.&nbsp; He fooled John&nbsp; Kerry...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; He fooled everybody.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; ... John Edwards, everybody.&nbsp; So we're blaming the president&nbsp; of the United States that — somehow that he was...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I'm not.&nbsp; I'm a forward-looking guy.&nbsp; I mean, I...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Absolutely right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I've come to the conclusion the WMDs thing was an honest&nbsp; mistake throughout the world, not just us, but I'm looking forward and I&nbsp; want — I don't want Americans dying over there in Iraq.&nbsp; I don't want it,&nbsp; you know.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; No.&nbsp; Nobody wants — but nobody wants Americans to die in&nbsp; war, but we do die in war.&nbsp; That's what war is about.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right, but this isn't...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; We do die.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; This is an optional war.&nbsp; Now I believe you have to win it.&nbsp;&nbsp; Now you're in it, you win it, you know, but it's an option.&nbsp; It's not like&nbsp; Los Angeles where you're living there and that's your home.&nbsp; This is&nbsp; halfway around the world.</p>
<p>So what I'm saying to people is, look, you've got to make a decision.&nbsp;&nbsp; It's a hard decision to make because we don't know because we're voting on&nbsp; hope.</p>
<p>All right.&nbsp; So your brother is on MSNBC.&nbsp; He's got the black&nbsp; turtleneck.&nbsp; He's got the little dickey on, and he's out there trashing —&nbsp; I mean trashing — Bush.&nbsp; It's not moderate.&nbsp; It's full-brunt assault,&nbsp; almost Michael Moore-esque.&nbsp; How do you react to that?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; You try not to talk about those kinds of issues.&nbsp; I mean, we&nbsp; have agreed to disagree on the issue.&nbsp; I know Ron is one of those people&nbsp; who hates George Bush, like Michael Moore hates George Bush, like many&nbsp; other people who are voting this year hate George Bush.&nbsp; They still look at&nbsp; the 2000 election...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Do you know why he hates George Bush?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; He is — he was born — I — he is liberal.&nbsp; He thinks George&nbsp; Bush stole the election back in the year 2000, as Jesse Jackson just over&nbsp; the weekend down there in Florida was saying the same thing.</p>
<p>Many of the liberals feel the very same, that George Bush is not the&nbsp; president, Al Gore won the presidency.&nbsp; How dare he steal the presidency&nbsp; from our president?</p>
<p>I mean, I believe Ron the last election voted for Nader and would&nbsp; probably vote for him again.&nbsp; You don't hear him say that he's for Kerry.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; He just hates Bush.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; He's so against George Bush, yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But it's interesting because Bush carries on a Reagan-esque&nbsp; philosophy, get the evildoers, impose democracy, just as Ronald Reagan did.&nbsp;&nbsp; They did it in different ways, different styles, but the basic steely&nbsp; determination that democracy is the savior of the world, evildoers have got&nbsp; to be hunted down are common between the two men.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; But, Bill...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; See, that's what I don't understand, the vitriol coming&nbsp; out.&nbsp; I guess Ron Reagan admired his father, didn't he?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; I mean, we all admired our dad, but he disagreed with&nbsp; him vehemently on politics.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; He did?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; So — oh, yes, absolutely.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; So Ron Reagan disagreed with his father from what?&nbsp; Teenage&nbsp; years?&nbsp; Or when did that...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Teenage years.&nbsp; He just — you know, we used to say he's a&nbsp; product of the '60s.&nbsp; And I've said this before.&nbsp; I mean, USS Ronald Reagan&nbsp; — we just home ported her in San Diego, but Ron wouldn't be there, Patty&nbsp; wouldn't be there because they are not pro military.&nbsp; They're not for, you&nbsp; know, peace through strength like their father was.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; That's really fascinating.&nbsp; That's really interesting.&nbsp; I&nbsp; mean, I just find it so fascinating in a home where you had such a strong&nbsp; definition through the father that the two siblings would just reject it&nbsp; entirely.</p>
<p>All right.&nbsp; Let's...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Well, that's why Maureen and I were here, so we could cancel&nbsp; their votes on any given November day.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, I know, but that even makes it more — your late sister&nbsp; and yourself make it more fascinating to see the family dynamic, you know.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; You didn't want to be at Christmas dinner, Bill.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; You didn't want to be at Christmas dinner.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I would have had gravy all over me, Michael.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Oh, yes.&nbsp; It's just one thing you never wanted to do.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Both sides would have not liked me.&nbsp; I would have been&nbsp; flying.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; That's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Now, look, &quot;Twice Adopted&quot; is an important&nbsp; book.&nbsp; It's a very personal book.&nbsp; But, you know, there are a lot of books&nbsp; out there, and people — 25 bucks is no easy deal.&nbsp; Why should people buy&nbsp; this book?</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; You know, because with this book, for whatever reason, I'm&nbsp; just able to write the book through the eyes of a child.&nbsp; So many things go&nbsp; on in the world today.&nbsp; You talk about it on your radio show.&nbsp; You talk&nbsp; about it on your television show.</p>
<p>What do you talk about?&nbsp; You talk about child pornography.&nbsp; Have you&nbsp; ever talked to somebody who was the photo of child pornography, of what&nbsp; it's like to be the photo and what that does to somebody's life, because&nbsp; you'll learn what it does to somebody's life.</p>
<p>You'll learn that when I had to develop the photo that a predator took&nbsp; of me when I was 8 years old, had me develop it, put his hand on my&nbsp; shoulder, and said wouldn't your mother like to have a copy of this, you&nbsp; will find out it took me six years to get out of my mother's house to&nbsp; protect her from me because I thought then I was truly the predator.</p>
<p>Or how I hated the fact that my father was running for governor or&nbsp; president because I so worried that those photos would come out and people&nbsp; would know the truth about me, how hateful I was and the kind of person&nbsp; that I was.&nbsp; You're going to hear for the very first time what it's like to&nbsp; be the photo in child pornography.</p>
<p>I was also molested, and I was able to look through the eyes again of&nbsp; a child, what up — what a child sees.&nbsp; We're always asked, bill, why&nbsp; didn't you tell somebody?&nbsp; You're going to find out why we don't tell&nbsp; anybody.</p>
<p>And, hopefully, this is going to be a help to parents who are dealing&nbsp; with children who hate themselves, who don't understand why maybe their&nbsp; mother gave them away when they were adopted.&nbsp; You're going to find out&nbsp; what a child feels like.</p>
<p>Or my definition of divorce, Bill, where I say it's where two people&nbsp; walk into a child's room, take everything that's dear and near to that&nbsp; child, break it into a million pieces, walk out and expect the child to put&nbsp; it back together.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, I...</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; That's the kind of stuff they're going to find out.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I think you just sold your book, and it's certainly a&nbsp; cautionary tale that Americans should know about.</p>
<p>Michael, always a pleasure to see you.&nbsp; Thank you very much for coming&nbsp; on.</p>
<p><b>REAGAN</b>:&nbsp; Thank you, Bill.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.</p>
