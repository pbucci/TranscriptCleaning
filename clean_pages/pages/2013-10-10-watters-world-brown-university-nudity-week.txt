			<h1 itemprop="headline">Watters' World: Brown University nudity week</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2013-10-09T00:00-04:00">Published October 09, 2013</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="2733281441001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/2733281441001/watters-world-brown-university-nudity-week" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/100913_factor_watters_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; October 9, 2013. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: In &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, &quot;Watters' World.&quot; You may remember a couple of years ago, Brown University in Rhode Island used student activity fees to partially fund a sex exposition.</p>
<p>Jesse Watters went up there to ask about it and was not greeted with enthusiasm. So, when we heard that Brown was sponsoring a Clothing Optional Week, we asked Watters to man up and go back to Brown.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<div></div>
<p>WATTERS: So, what is the meaning of Nudity Week.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: I think it's just providing a comfortable space for people to express themselves.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: You know, gender expression, gender performativity.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Whoa, whoa, whoa. What's gender performativity.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>DUSTIN HOFFMAN, ACTOR: This conversation is getting a little strange.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: Really, some of the kids here are really into being naked with each other.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: That's a good way for students to feel comfortable with their bodies.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Did you guys think that this is just a ruse to see your classmates nude.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 3: No, there are other ways to just see your classmates nude.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: Women feeling bad about their weight, men feeling bad about their weight. I think --</p>
<p>WATTERS: I feel bad about my weight sometimes.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: So, do I, Jesse.</p>
<p>WATTERS: You don't need to though.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: We allow people to sort of do whatever they want, whatever they feel to further their best interest.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Like if the whole thing was violence in the media, people are OK with that. But if you show one nipple, everyone goes crazy.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>BILL MURRAY, ACTOR: Dogs and cats living together. Mass hysteria!</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 6: I don't need any extra nudity in my life.</p>
<p>WATTERS: OK, you have just enough nudity in your life right now.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 6: Yes.</p>
<p>WATTERS: You don't need any more nudity.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 6: No.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: During exam week, we have like the Naked Doughnut Run.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: What's your angle of this story.</p>
<p>WATTERS: If there's a naked event on a college campus, I want to know about it.</p>
<p>A lot of students are doing nude yoga, nude body painting.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: A lot of students are playing poker. What's the educational value of that.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Well, Mathematics.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: You, guys, playing cards?</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Now, nude body painting. Is that finger painting or is it a roller.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: I'm not really sure but I assume it can be any of these things.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Were you a painter or were you a paintee.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 7: I was both the artist and the canvass.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Did it tickle.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 7: I wasn't uncomfortable at all.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Nude yoga. Sounds like a little bit of a stretch. Do you bring your own mat.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 8: No, they provide mats and you bring a towel.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>CHEVY CHASE, ACTOR: Can I borrow your towel for a sec. My car just hit a water buffalo.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: It was actually like a really spiritual experience.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Was it hard.</p>
<p>Now, what about nude theater.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: What about it.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Are there puppets or something.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: To step on stage and say, &quot;This is my body. It's OK to look.&quot;</p>
<p>WATTERS: Was your nude scene really small.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: No, it was significant.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTRESS: So huge!</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: Thanks.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: How much money do you pay to go to Brown about.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: Between 50 and 60,000.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Let's call your mom right now and tell her it's Nudity Week here at Brown.</p>
<p>This is Jesse Watters with the O'REILLY FACTOR. How are you doing, mom.</p>
<p>MOTHER OF A STUDENT (on the phone): OK.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Did you know that it's Nudity Week at Brown here, mom.</p>
<p>MOTHER OF A STUDENT: OK.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTRESS: What, what, what!</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Do your parents know that you participated in nude body painting at school.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 7: They do now.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Say hi to dad.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 7: Hey, daddy.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you ever watch the O'REILLY FACTOR on Fox News.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 8: No, I don't. I don't know what that is.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Shame on you.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 8: I'm English.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>MIKE MYERS: Actually, I'm English.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: Sorry.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: I have never watched the show on principle.</p>
<p>WATTERS: So, you've never watched the show but you disagree with everything he says. But you don't know what he said because you've never watched him.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 5: I -- I think I really just want to leave it at that.</p>
<p>MOTHER OF A STUDENT (on the phone): I love Bill O'Reilly.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you watch &quot;Watters' World.&quot;</p>
<p>MOTHER OF A STUDENT: Yes, I do.</p>
<p>WATTERS: This is Jesse Watters.</p>
<p>MOTHER OF A STUDENT: OK, well, great.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. Now, you've got a lot of heat, you going up there and then --</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes, it was a little testy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And that newspaper attacked you?</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes, they attacked me. That's right. I actually got kicked off campus --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: By who.</p>
<p>WATTERS: -- by the administrator, so I had to leave and go out immediately.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why did they kick you off campus.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Well, I guess you're not allowed to film everywhere. And I really want to dive deep into Nudity Week, so --</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, they wouldn't let you film the actual event.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I went too far.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's just as well. What did the newspaper say. I mean, you know, college newspapers -- I was on -- I don't know whether you were on on yours but I was on the &quot;Boston University Daily Free Press.&quot; They kind of like to mix it up a little bit.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: If they don't like you --</p>
<p>WATTERS: No. And they were very angry with me. Tonight, they've organized a drinking game.</p>
<p>So, every time I make fun of them tonight, they're going to drink.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Uh-huh.</p>
<p>WATTERS: So, they're probably hammered right now.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Are they clothed.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>WATTERS: I have no idea. I don't need to know that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But the week ended, right.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I think it's over but I mean --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: It's getting cold up there in Rhode Island now.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Right. It's frigid.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right. They were right on the board.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That's right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Now, this is harmless in my opinion.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Mine, too.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Nothing really wrong.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I have nothing against Nudity Week. In fact, I may have participated.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, let's not go there.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>But the only thing is student activity fees. You know what I mean.</p>
<p>WATTERS: A miniscule amount of activity fee, --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Miniscule.</p>
<p>WATTERS: -- they claim goes to Nudity Week.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Just enough to buy the paint.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That's right. Well, you don't have any props or clothing, so it's pretty much straight.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right. And it's probably all for the paint.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That's right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And I've got to say, the kids, students, who talked to you, they were very respectful. They were very --</p>
<p>WATTERS: Very articulate and respectful. I have nothing against Brown University.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No. We have nothing against Brown. We just have a few laughs at your expense.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>And, you know, we wish you well. Watters, everybody.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Thank you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Going where very few men go.</p>
