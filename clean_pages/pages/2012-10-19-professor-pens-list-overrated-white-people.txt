			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Professor pens list of 'overrated white people'</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2012-10-18T00:00-04:00">Published October 18, 2012</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="1910780484001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/1910780484001/professor-pens-list-overrated-white-people" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/101812_of_hill_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>
							<div class="caption">

						<p></p>
<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; October 18, 2012. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>BILL O'REILLY: &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, our pal, Marc Lamont Hill, who teaches at Columbia University causing trouble again -- he's always causing trouble.</p>
<p>He recently wrote an article entitled, &quot;The 15 Most Overrated White People.&quot; And here he is, Dr. Hill.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<div></div>
<p>So before we get into your dopey list, what if I wrote an article, &quot;The 15 Most Overrated Black People.&quot; What do you think would happen.</p>
<p>MARC LAMONT HILL, FOX NEWS POLITICAL CONTRIBUTOR: I think you'd be celebrated and praised for the comedic truth-teller that you are, Bill.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>Why do white people always ask that question.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Because you know there's a double standard.</p>
<p>HILL: It's not a double -- yes, it's a double standard. A double standard that white people have power. They've had influence.</p>
<p>They've been able to control the United States the last 400 years. So, now, everything that white people can do, black people can do, and vice versa.</p>
<p>You can't ask them, &quot;Why isn't there White History Month. Why aren't there, historically, white universities.&quot;</p>
<p>Well, we have them. We call white -- you know --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. So because the white establishment has the power, you can pretty much do whatever you want to them.</p>
<p>HILL: No. It just means that they're not opposite sides of the same coin. When I scream, &quot;black power,&quot; I'm talking about community development.</p>
<p>If you would scream, &quot;white power,&quot; it would be something very different. They just don't mean the same thing.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Look, I don't scream any kind of power because I don't like the division of the races. But I think you're actually telling the truth in a roundabout way.</p>
<p>In this country, you can mock white people. But in a minority setting, you can't.</p>
<p>HILL: You can mock black people.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, you can't.</p>
<p>HILL: You can mock black people.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I can do it to you.</p>
<p>HILL: Right, because we're besties.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, because you're a Communist.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>You're not a black person to me. You're a Communist.</p>
<p>HILL: So, you see red, not black or white.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, I'm actually mocking an ideology and not a color.</p>
<p>HILL: I feel the same thing as you. I mean, it's great. I'm glad we can come together on this.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Sure. All right. Let's get into your stupid list. You wrote it on Columbus Day --</p>
<p>HILL: Yes, in honor of Columbus.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: -- because Columbus, in your opinion, is overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Yes, he epitomizes overrated white people.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: The Nina, the Pinta and the Santa Maria, all right, going in uncharted waters, overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: And unexpected waters. They thought they're going one place, landed someplace else. &quot;Gilligan&quot; got a TV show over this and, somehow, we give Columbus a whole day. I mean, come on.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: They didn't have maps back then. See, nobody had sailed there. But why is he overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Because what we do is we give him credit for creating something in a way that ignores indigenous people --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Indigenous people.</p>
<p>HILL: Yes, native Americans, Indians -- whatever you want to call them, indigenous people to the lands.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But Columbus had nothing to do with that. He just discovered this continent and brought it back to Spain because there's a lot of folks here.</p>
<p>HILL: And the problem is when we talk about discovery now is he acts as if no one else was already there. As if traditions weren't there, cultures weren't there.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I don't think Columbus was acting.</p>
<p>HILL: We act that way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: He was trying to dodge the arrows. He knew people were there.</p>
<p>HILL: Dodge the arrows. See, that's the kind of --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, I'm sorry. There were no arrows.</p>
<p>HILL: Did you get that media man. He said, &quot;Dodge the arrows.&quot;</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right. There were no arrows coming at him. All right, now. And, now, you're into really, really dangerous territory.</p>
<p>HILL: Uh-oh.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Elvis is overrated. Elvis Presley.</p>
<p>HILL: I can talk about the President, Jesus.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: This is the best entertainer in history.</p>
<p>HILL: Oh, my God. Maybe, the best white entertainer. Frank Sinatra has something to say about that, too. Come on, Elvis.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You just admitted it. No, you just admitted he's the best white entertainer. You see, how can he be overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Because there's about 74,621 black entertainers who are better than Elvis.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, I see. They're better.</p>
<p>HILL: Snoop Dogg is better than Elvis. You know, I'll put them on the same tier.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, there it is. OK, that's how naughty you are.</p>
<p>HILL: I'm kidding. I'm kidding. Snoop Dogg is not Elvis. Elvis was extraordinary in many ways. I'm not saying he's not good. He's just overrated. He's not the King of Rock and Roll.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Ronald Reagan is overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, of course, bringing down the Soviet Union, having a good economy. That's all overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Expanding prisons, shutting down mental institutions, shrinking public assistance for the most vulnerable people, many of whom are white, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You're just mad because are you a Communist and the Soviet Union fell apart.</p>
<p>HILL: Broke my heart. Broke my heart.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's right. That's what that's all about.</p>
<p>HILL: No, but Bill Clinton. You know, it's not a partisan thing.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And then you have Clinton on the list.</p>
<p>HILL: He's overrated.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why.</p>
<p>HILL: Because black people keep calling him &quot;The First Black President,&quot; he did these extraordinary things, Prison Litigation Reform Act, Welfare Reform Act, Three Strikes.</p>
<p>All of the stuff that are hurting black people. Black people still praise the democratic party and Bill Clinton.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why do they like him though. You don't like him, they do.</p>
<p>HILL: Because he goes into a church and some days claps on beat. He plays his saxophone and let you see how he plays to the most low-end, low- barred troops of black culture.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Are you saying that African-Americans are not discerning in their opinion.</p>
<p>HILL: No, you're saying that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, you just said it.</p>
<p>HILL: No, I'm saying with Bill Clinton, they've consumed the beverage. I won't say Kool Aid because that would be racist.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: The final one is President Obama's economic team, Tim Geithner --</p>
<p>HILL: See, I thought you're going to go Babe Ruth.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, because, Babe Ruth, that's a baseball player. We're talking serious here.</p>
<p>HILL: Excuse me. Right. I forgot, we've said Elvis.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why is -- well, Elvis is an icon. So is Babe Ruth. But let's just get on to Geithner.</p>
<p>HILL: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why is President Obama's economic team overrated in the face of Geithner.</p>
<p>HILL: Because with Geithner, it's somewhere at the beginning -- I mean, we can go from --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes but why. Why are they overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: Why, because, I think, we came with an expectation that he was going to create an entirely new economic landscape for America --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: A Communist one.</p>
<p>HILL: -- a new vision for poor people, a new vision for the vulnerable. And Obama is following the same -- not a Communist one.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No.</p>
<p>HILL: I just want a progressive one. I just want him to bow at the feet of Wall Street.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, because the Obama administration hasn't seized private property, you think they're overrated.</p>
<p>HILL: No, I just want people to --</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>-- Wow, wow.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, I am exposing you, Hill.</p>
<p>HILL: You have a manual for this.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I'm exposing you tonight.</p>
<p>HILL: Oh, please. So, we agree on Justin Bieber then. That means, we agree that Justin Bieber is overrated. We agree that the Royal Family - -</p>
<p>O'REILLY: The kid is 10 years old. Why are you picking on Justin Bieber --</p>
<p>HILL: I'm not picking on Justin Bieber. I'm just saying --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: How can you be overrated with hair like that. You can't.</p>
<p>HILL: That's awesome hair.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, Marc Lamont Hill, everybody.</p>
