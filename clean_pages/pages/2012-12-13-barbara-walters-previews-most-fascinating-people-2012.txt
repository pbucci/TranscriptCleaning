			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Barbara Walters previews most fascinating people of 2012</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2012-12-12T00:00-05:00">Published December 12, 2012</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="2031852149001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/v/2031852149001/barbara-walters-previews-most-fascinating-people-2012" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/121212_factor_walters_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly</em></p>
<p><em>Factor,&quot; December 12, 2012. This copy may not be in its final form and</em></p>
<p><em>may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m.</em></p>
<p><em>and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<div></div>
<p>O'REILLY: Second &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, &quot;The Most Fascinating People of 2012,&quot; according to Barbara Walters. Her yearly special on the subject airs tonight on ABC.</p>
<p>Here's the list. Hillary Clinton, Ben Affleck, Seth MacFarlane, Chris Christie, Honey Boo Boo, author E.L. James who writes with the S&amp;M stuff, Gabby Douglas, and the pop group, One Direction.</p>
<p>Here now, Barbara Walters. So, who gave you the best sound byte.</p>
<p>BARBARA WALTERS, BROADCAST JOURNALIST: Right. I want to comment on something your people were just talking about, whether Hillary Clinton is going to run or not.</p>
<p>She is one of our &quot;10 Most Fascinating People.&quot; Let me read you what she said from the mouth and not just --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Sure.</p>
<p>WALTERS: OK. Here we go. She says, &quot;It's all hypothetical because, right now, I have no intention of running.&quot;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>&quot;I just want to make a contribution. Will it be political? I don't think so. I think it will be philanthropic. It might be academic. It might be business.&quot;</p>
<p>So, you'll know much more about her tonight.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, my money is on she's running. Will you bet me.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Oh, OK. I'm telling you what she said. No, I like to get the facts. You like to do conjecture.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes. But I put my money where my mouth is. I'll bet you.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>WALTERS: I don't bet. I come on because I love you. I don't come on to make money.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>If I bet you, I'll win.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, you're saying that she were -- you know she's going to run. OK, on these fascinating people things, do you pick them or somebody else pick them.</p>
<p>WALTERS: No. Bill Geddie and I, who's our executive producer --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Geddie? You're with Bill Geddie?</p>
<p>WALTERS: Yes, Bill Geddie. You know, the executive producer on &quot;The View.&quot;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: He's a very troubled person.</p>
<p>WALTERS: &quot;The View&quot; has made you famous.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You have made me famous? I'm never -- I'm not on his fascinating list. Look at Christie. Does he look fascinating?</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>WALTERS: You know something?</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Wait. Look at his -- look how he's looking at you. He doesn't like you. He doesn't like you, look.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Well, he didn't like me in part because I asked about his weight. But we also asked him about his promoting -- you know, praising Obama. And that's --</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes. Got him elected.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Well, he did not get him -- he says that he didn't -- when you see the piece, that he did more campaigning than anybody else besides Mitt Romney.</p>
<p>You know what happened to Mitt Romney. And he doesn't want to -- didn't want to run now, but he talks about 2016.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Is he running.</p>
<p>WALTERS: He didn't have the fire in his belly now. But I would be willing to bet you the whole nine yards in 2016.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK. So, you would --</p>
<p>WALTERS: I'll bet you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I don't know anything but, no, Christie -- I think he wants it. I think he wants it. So, would you like to see a Christie versus Hillary. That'd be fun.</p>
<p>WALTERS: I think that would be --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You know, that'd be really fun. All right, Barack Obama, he's the President. He doesn't make the Fascinating list though. He's not a fascinating person.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Well, we've had both the President and the First Lady on --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes. But, look, let's be honest. When it comes down between Barack Obama and Honey Boo Boo, you've got to go with Boo Boo. You've got to go with her.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>Thank you, appreciate that. I got a laugh out of her. Right. There's Honey Boo Boo. There she is. She's the new Secretary of State. Did you know that.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Yes.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>Why do I do this show.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's what everybody says.</p>
<p>WALTERS: I want to ask you a question.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: What.</p>
<p>WALTERS: When one of the people we talked to in &quot;Ten Most Fascinating People&quot; is E.L. James who's the woman who wrote &quot;Fifty Shades&quot; --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, the S&amp;M woman.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Well, she says it's love story, &quot;Fifty Shades of Gray.&quot;</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, OK.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>WALTERS: And she talked about the fact that it is love story and that women like to be submissive. Do you think women like to be submissive.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No.</p>
<p>WALTERS: You don't.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I've been on &quot;The View.&quot; So, I don't think so, all right.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>WALTERS: Which made you a star.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, yes. It made me a star. OK, all of these people are - -</p>
<p>WALTERS: I only said that because you tell me that if you mention my program, it will be a success. Otherwise, it wouldn't make it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: It will be, all right. Look, you have a nice, eclectic crew there and I think you'd get a good rating with it. Barack Obama, you know, the word is that he's now really -- and I think this may be true, he's a little cockier than he used to be, now that he got re-elected.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>Did you see that when you sat down with him. Now, you're interview with him is going to be on &quot;20/20&quot; on Friday, right.</p>
<p>WALTERS: &quot;20/20.&quot; Barack Obama, the word is he's now really -- I think this may be true -- he's a little cockier than he used to be now that they got re-elected.</p>
<p>Did you see a change in his demeanor.</p>
<p>WALTERS: He seemed to me --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Be honest, be honest, be honest.</p>
<p>WALTERS: No, I'm thinking about it. We did an interview with him alone which was when he talked about the Syrian rebels and that he's going to recognize them.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>And then we did an interview with him with Mrs. Obama where he is very relaxed and very funny.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, he was tensed just with you. And when she came in, he was more relaxed.</p>
<p>WALTERS: He is comfortable with himself now. I don't think -- I think --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You didn't see he was a little cockier? You didn't see he was a little more self-assured? No?</p>
<p>WALTERS: I don't find him cocky when he's with her. I find them very funny and they spar together. And it's a different Barack Obama. It's much more personal.</p>
<p>Did I find him cocky? No, I didn't. No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, you didn't.</p>
<p>WALTERS: You, I find cocky. Him, I don't find cocky.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, I know. But I'm not any cockier than I was five years ago. The word in Washington is he's a little bit more, he's a little bit more, &quot;Hey, in your face. I won again. Don't mess with me.&quot;</p>
<p>WALTERS: I'd like to know -- you know, it depends. All these people who know everything, who haven't interviewed him or haven't talked to Hillary.</p>
<p>Everybody is in authority. I did not find him cocky. I found him --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You like him though, right.</p>
<p>WALTERS: I do like him.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Do you think he's been a good president?</p>
<p>WALTERS: I find him -- when he talks about --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You're dodging.</p>
<p>WALTERS: I'm trying to finish my sentence which is --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You're dodging me. Has he been a good president.</p>
<p>WALTERS: -- which is more than you left those other two women do 10 minutes ago.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, yes. You're out of control.</p>
<p>WALTERS: They didn't get a word in.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, stop.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Where was I. I've already forgotten.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Was Barack -- Is Barack Obama a good president.</p>
<p>WALTERS: In my opinion?</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Yes, I think he has been a good president.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK. We respect that. We respect that.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Thank you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You know you'll be paying more to the government though, right. I hope you've been setting aside a little of that &quot;View&quot; money.</p>
<p>WALTERS: I am very blessed. And so are you. And if it helps this country, and if it helps pay for the many of the things we need, I don't mind paying more taxes. And you shouldn't either.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I don't mind paying unless it's wasted on things like Solyndra and things like that. That upsets me.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Well, that's one thing and you keep bringing it up.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK. And I don't want Honey Boo Boo getting any of my money. Barbara Walters, &quot;Fascinating People,&quot; ABC.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Happy Holidays.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I don't know why you come in here. Because I'm the only one in the world that gives a hard --</p>
<p>WALTERS: Because I -- you know what, because I love you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes. I'm the only one who gives you a hard time in the world.</p>
<p>WALTERS: You know I love you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: In the whole world, the only one.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Happy Holidays, my darling, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Merry Christmas.</p>
<p>WALTERS: Merry Christmas.</p>
