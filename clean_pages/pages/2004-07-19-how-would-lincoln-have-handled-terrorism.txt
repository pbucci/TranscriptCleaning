			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">How Would Lincoln Have Handled Terrorism?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-07-19T17:25-04:00">Published July 19, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;July 16, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>JOHN KASICH, GUEST&nbsp;HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Factor Original&quot; segment tonight, how would Abraham Lincoln have handled the growing threat of terrorism?&nbsp; Would he have gone to war in Iraq?&nbsp; And what would he do with Osama bin Laden?&nbsp; The former Democratic Governor of New York, Mario Cuomo, wrote a book about it, &quot;Why Lincoln Matters: Today More than Ever.&quot;&nbsp; He recently spoke with Bill about it.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE)</strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY</strong>:&nbsp; Governor, it is a fearsome war that we're fighting against Al Qaeda and the other terrorists.&nbsp; Would you agree with that?</p>
<p><strong>MARIO M. CUOMO, &quot;WHY LINCOLN MATTERS,&quot; AUTHOR:</strong>&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; It's fearsome.&nbsp; And it's a war unlike any other in American history, because there's no doomsday deterrent.&nbsp; If they get a nuclear device, they'll kill as many of us as they can.&nbsp; Isn't it time for a change in the rules of engagement?&nbsp; Wouldn't Abraham Lincoln say, &quot;I've got to change here to meet the threat?&quot;</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; He did.&nbsp; He changed the constitution.&nbsp; He changed all the rules.&nbsp; As a matter of fact, he changed them so much, I disagreed with them -- suspended the writ of habeas corpus immediately, he started locking up people.&nbsp; Every president does that in times of emergency.&nbsp; That doesn't mean it's right.&nbsp; And I think he went beyond what was necessary.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; We have people who want to destroy us who have no rules at all, and shouldn't we change, be nimble enough to change?</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; We have to be very precise, Bill, about where the threat is.&nbsp; If you're talking about the terrorists, Osama bin Laden in Afghanistan with the Taliban, that was more properly called a war than would normally be the case with terrorism, because you had a particular defined group, the Al Qaeda, and you had a known target, and that is the war that we should have completed but didn't.</p>
<p>Lincoln would have fought that war viciously, the way he fought the Civil War.&nbsp; Once he was in it, once you dragged him in, he went all out to win.&nbsp; He was even a lover of weapons of mass destruction, as you know.&nbsp; And so, yes, there he would have fought it fiercely.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You're talking about the Gatling gun and all these...</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; Absolutely.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ... and all of these new weapons.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; Bill, he would have fought more fiercely than we did, because what we did was then do something Lincoln would not have done -- a preemptive war against Iraq, which diluted the war in Afghanistan.&nbsp; He wouldn't have done that.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But if Lincoln had intelligence information that said there were weapons of mass destruction available to a fanatical dictator that could hand them off to a fanatical terrorist, Osama bin Laden, wouldn't Lincoln have thought about removing the fanatical dictator when he had legal precedent to do so because of the cease fire violations?</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; You know, if you're saying, &quot;Did Bush have any excuse for making a mistake,&quot; probably.&nbsp; I say in the book that we have a couple of choices.&nbsp; We can believe that Bush lied...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Or Clinton and Blair and everybody else.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; I don't believe he lied.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, I don't either.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; I think he was misled.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right, so do I.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; That doesn't mean he's not accountable.&nbsp; That doesn't mean you should give him another chance to be misled.&nbsp; It was, perhaps, the most grotesque mistake in modern history for a president to make.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; If it turns out that it's a waste in Iraq -- see, if the Iraq situation stabilizes and we get control of the area there, then it doesn't turn out to be such a big mistake.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; No, Bill, I think maybe you can fool some people into thinking that, but it will have been a mistake.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Reading your book, &quot;Why Lincoln Matters: Today More than Ever,&quot; somebody's going to say, oh, this is such a scholastic thing, I can't read it.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; Yeah.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But that's not what it is.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; That's why I sent it to people like you.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yeah, if I can get through it, anybody can.&nbsp; You just insulted me, Governor.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; It's a serious book.&nbsp; But it's a small book.&nbsp; It's like his wings (ph) book.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; It's a small book, and you can read it as essayist.&nbsp; The piece on religion I'm very intrigued by, and I worked very hard on that.&nbsp; But there's religion, preemptive war, there's race...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All the things that matter today.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; Yeah.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And we appreciate you coming in, Governor.</p>
<p><b>CUOMO</b>:&nbsp; I appreciate the opportunity.&nbsp; Thank you, Bill.</p>
<p>(END VIDEOTAPE)</p>
<p><b>KASICH</b>:&nbsp; And that's it for us today.&nbsp; As always, I want to thank you for watching The Factor.&nbsp; And remember, Saturday night at eight o'clock, my show, &quot;<a href="/story/0,2933,71246,00.html">Heartland</a>.&quot;&nbsp; Hey, if you want to go out to dinner, forget about it.&nbsp; Order in.&nbsp; It's a great show, &quot;Heartland,&quot; Saturday night at eight.</p>
