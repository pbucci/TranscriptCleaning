			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Do You Have Legal Right to Decline Airport Body Scan?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2010-11-09T00:00-05:00">Published November 09, 2010</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>

			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="4411969">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/4411969/do-have-legal-right-to-decline-airport-body-scan" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/110910_oreilly_legal_FNC_110910_21-03.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p><em><strong>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; November 9, 2010. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</strong></em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Is it Legal?&quot; segment tonight: We've got three hot topics, so let's get right to attorneys and Fox News analysts Kimberly Guilfoyle and Lis Wiehl. So Wiehl, full-body scan at the airport.</p>
<p><strong>LIS WIEHL, FOX NEWS LEGAL ANALYST:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>I have a bunch of very simple questions because I am a simple man, as you both know.</p>
<div></div>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No. 1, say you don't want to go through that machine.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Do you have a Plan B? Will they give you an option?</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> In most airports you can say, &quot;I don't want to have those X-rays.&quot; You can see there where they can see every little last thing of you. Any broken bones, they'll see that. You can have a pat down by a person of the same sex, so a woman patted down by another woman.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You can opt -- you can opt out for the pat down.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Now, they also, I understand, when they're looking at the full-body scan machine, it's the same sex looking at it.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Right, right. That's what it's supposed to be anyway.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. All right. So they're trying to take that out.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And I also understand that immediately after you pass through the machine it's deleted. Is that true?</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> That's supposedly true. Both of those things.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But all of those things are subject to...</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Of course, mistake, human error.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Mistakes or somebody...</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Maybe a woman calls in sick, a guy calls in sick, and so they've got to have somebody else there.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> So legally then, Guilfoyle if you say, &quot;Look, I don't want either of these two options,&quot; you can't fly?</p>
<p><strong>KIMBERLY GUILFOYLE, FOX NEWS LEGAL ANALYST:</strong> Well, yes. And really stop complaining. People should understand that this is a threat to our national security.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>We all know why it's instituted. But you -- but the airline and the federal government has a right to say if you're not going to do either/or, you're not going.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Absolutely, because they have provided a less intrusive means for you to go through, and you do the pat down.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> What's -- the pat down is pretty intrusive though. A pilot punched out some guy because the guy was grabbing him someplace, I guess. I would rather have the machine than the pat down. I don't want to be patted down by Lenny. I'm sorry.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> I agree. And at a certain point…</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Vinny or Lenny patting me down.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Why not? Let them see it.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I'd rather hitchhike.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Come on.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I don't want them patting me down.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Then go through the machine.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I will. I don't have any problem with the machine.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> There's no Fourth Amendment right here. The Fourth Amendment is the right against unreasonable search and seizure. The Supreme Court has ruled on this. Take the pat down or the X-ray.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> It's either you go through the body scan, you get patted down or you hitchhike where you want to go.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Right. It's a small sacrifice to make. Let them see you rather than having a plane get blown up.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> It's easy for you to say, Guilfoyle.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> I have nothing to hide. Scan me.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. I don't want to get into that. All right. Now in Oklahoma, Guilfoyle...</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> ...there's a judge, this Muslim guy sued because the Oklahoma voters said no to Sharia law and international law, as far as its impact in the state of Oklahoma. They voted. It was a proposition. And they said no, and this judge said, &quot;You know, this Muslim guy may have a point, so I'm going to stay it.&quot; This is all theater to me. It doesn't mean anything. But what was her rationale?</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Well, ultimately she's saying, &quot;Look, we have to look into whether or not this is violating their rights or it persecutes a certain religion, so I'm going to issue a temporary restraining order. And it's going to be stayed until November 22, when I will decide if a permanent injunction should be issued banning this amendment.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> So one judge can, once again, overturn the will of the whole state?</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Absolutely. So it's legislating from the bench.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Wait. Overturning it. Overturning it till -- all she's saying is...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> She's going to decide.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> ...&quot;I am going to wait until November 22.&quot; All she has said so far is &quot;I'm going to wait till November 22...&quot;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> &quot;...and I'm going to have a hearing on whether international law can apply.&quot; And let me tell you, Justice Roberts at his confirmation hearings talked about...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Well, you've already said that there are some problems with the proposition the way it was written because of the international law thing.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. All right. I just don't like the one judge overturning the rule of the people.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Absolutely. It's legislating from the bench.</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> It's inappropriate. We shouldn't have Sharia law in effect.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> A few years ago, a guy, a hacker tried to bring down BillOReilly.com. Cost us a lot of money and did a lot of damage. And he was caught by the FBI after an extensive investigation, Wiehl. And in federal court he was sentenced to what?</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Two and a half years. Thirty months of federal time...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Federal time.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> ...which is hard time. And then three years of supervised release after that. It's a very strict punishment, in my estimation, for hacking into BillOReilly.com.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Now why did he get such a strict punishment?</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Well, the guidelines were 10 to 16 months, and I could see that. But what he did, his fatal flaw, is he lied to the pre-sentence report officer, the person that's writing down everything that he's telling him to write to put this report together for the judge. He said, you know, &quot;I was working at Stanley Steemer cleaner.&quot;</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> He put himself off as a pillar of the community. And it was actually a University of Akron guy, right?</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> It's a sad case. Twenty-three-year-old guy. Got a baby.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Not sad. He's a pinhead. But he lied. How did he lie?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> He lied in the pre-sentence investigation report. He said he was working at this cleaner place. He was actually online...</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Operating a business and making money.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> What was he doing?</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> He was afraid to tell the probation officer, yes.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Pot.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Pot.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> He was a pot dealer. He was a drug dealer.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> He wasn't sympathetic about this at all.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> But I would have given him 10 years.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Put him behind bars.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Guidelines were 10 to 16.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Have some personal responsibility and...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And you didn't even want to say that, Wiehl. I think you should be ashamed of yourself. So the guy -- not only was the guy a hacker trying to bring down BillOReilly.com, he's selling drugs at the same time. So, good, he's going away for 30 months, right?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> You know what he said? &quot;I was afraid my probation officer...&quot;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Afraid? All right. And he's got to pay us 40 grand.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Forty thousand dollars.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> We all get a share of that?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, because I give -- I give everything I get to charity. So charity is going to get a large portion of that 40 grand.</p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> That $40,000...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> ...don't be waiting for that check in the mail.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> Let me tell you something. He shut down your site, which doesn't allow people to get the charitable money that they want.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Why shouldn't I wait? Isn't that check in the mail?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> No. That check is not going to be in the mail for a long time.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> It might bounce.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Because he doesn't have any money.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> We're dealing with a criminal here.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Where did he bury all the pot money? We've got to find it.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>WIEHL:</strong> Maybe he smoked it.</p>
<p><strong>GUILFOYLE:</strong> In the backyard. Get a warrant.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right, ladies. Thanks very much.</p>
