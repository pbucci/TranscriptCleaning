			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Controversial Denmark Tourism Ad</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2009-09-17T14:45-04:00">Published September 17, 2009</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>

			 <p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; September 16, 2009. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight: &quot;Did You See That?&quot; An appalling situation out of Denmark. A tourism agency there put out an ad featuring a single woman trying to find out who fathered her baby.</p>
<p><a onclick="videoMPlayer('9665283','');return false;" href="#"><b>Click here to watch the ad!</b></a></p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> Hi. My name is Kan (ph), and I'm from Denmark. This here is my baby boy. His name — his name is Alvis (ph). Yes, I'm doing this video because I'm trying to find Alvis's (ph) father. We met one and a half year ago when you were on vacation here in Denmark. I don't remember where you are from and I don't even remember your name.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Excellent. With us now, FOX News anchor Jane Skinner. I can't believe the country of Denmark puts this thing out. Why would they do that?</p>
<p><strong>JANE SKINNER, FOX NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> One female commentator said it's like trying to sell the idea, &quot;Come here. We've got beautiful blonde women who will have one-night stands with you.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's what it was.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> And interestingly enough, the tourism agency, the CEO is a woman. And when you say what were they thinking? This is what she said they were thinking. We thought this was a good, sweet, really harmless story of a mature, responsible woman who lives in a free society. We were trying to create a positive view of Denmark. They put it on YouTube.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Are you kidding me?</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> It wasn't on television. It was actually on YouTube. Got close to a million hits.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Was ACORN behind this? Can we blame this on ACORN?</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> The reaction though, as you can imagine, was incredulous.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Look, Denmark is a liberal country, but they're not crazy. But they did pull this off the air.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> They did. After about four days or so.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Four days.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> Four days.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So let me get this straight before we move on. Denmark wants people to go visit, so they put up a single mom who doesn't know the name of the father.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> Oh, and she's an actress. We should point that out.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> This didn't really happen.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> That's not real and that's not her baby.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> This is a script. But you wouldn't know that.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> No.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I mean, that's pretty convincing. But they think that having a woman giving birth out of wedlock, not knowing the father, is going to make people come to Denmark.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> After a drunk one-night stand.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Excellent. Oh, yes.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> And they pull it. But you know, if you think about it in this day and age, it's linked…</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> It's on YouTube.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> It was linked to 83,000 Web sites.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> We have a problem here in the United States. Not looking at Denmark. We have all kinds of crazy student violence going on. Now, school has only been in for about a week in many places. Look what happened on a school bus. Roll the tape.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> (EXPLETIVE DELETED)</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> Sit down!</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> What was that all about?</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> All right. Bellville, Illinois, across the Mississippi from St. Louis, if people aren't familiar with the area. A 17-year-old kid, the one who is the victim there, gets on the bus. Supposedly the fight was over a seat. Nobody would give him a seat. He moved somebody's book bag and sat down anyway. Fourteen- and 15-year-olds start pummeling him not once but twice. They took about a five minute break. But 14- and 15-year-olds have been suspended. By the end of the week we're going to learn whether they — potentially they may face criminal charges in this. And a couple of the kids who were sitting there watching, laughing, egging them on have also been suspended for five days.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No one has been charged with a crime yet. At first, they said it was racial, because the assailants are black and the victim is white. Bus driver didn't break it up. Disturbing. I would have pulled the bus over and called 911. That's what...</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> Interesting. People feel — there's disagreement on that, because the bus driver says he did what protocol calls for: calling dispatch and finding the safest place. He thought the safest place was school, which was a couple of minutes away.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But you can die in that time.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> But so could he. You know, that's what the supporters of the bus driver would say. They have one of the toughest jobs in the school district.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I know.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> Because you actually want to go in and break up that mob of kids. You put yourself in danger as well.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You have cameras on school buses now. Pretty much says it all.</p>
<p><b>SKINNER:</b> Then today they put a security guard on that school bus.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right, Jane, thanks very much. Jane Skinner.</p>
