			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Is Howard Dean is Unstoppable At This Point?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-01-08T15:03-05:00">Published January 08, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;January 7, 2003.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the <em>Factor Follow-Up</em> segment tonight, Dick Morris has a column today [January 7, 2004] in &quot;The New York Post&quot; where he says Howard Dean's in pretty good shape for the Democratic presidential nomination.&nbsp; Here's Dick.</p>
<p>I'm one of the few guys that consider a holdout.&nbsp; I just think that once he gets out of New Hampshire and Iowa, it's not going to be that easy for Governor Dean.&nbsp; And even in New Hampshire, a new poll says that Clark is coming up, right?&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>DICK MORRIS, FOX NEWS POLITICAL ANALYST</strong>:&nbsp; Yes, it's going to be harder for him because when he wins Iowa, he knocks out Gephardt.&nbsp; When he wins New Hampshire, he knocks out Kerry.&nbsp; And when he wins South Carolina, he knocks out Edwards.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But I don't think he's going to win South Carolina.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; I mean, well, he or Sharpton will, I think.&nbsp; And a nine-way race then becomes a three way race.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But Edwards has no chance down there?</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; He has some chance.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I think he's got the chance down there.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; No, but you got to spot these numbers 10 points.&nbsp; Once he wins Iowa and New Hampshire, move his numbers up every place 10 points.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; What if Clark does better and almost beats him in New Hampshire, because Clark looks like he's rising up there?</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Well, that could be significant.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; But the problem that Clark has, and the advantage that Dean has, is that the primaries are so compacted, that Clark from almost a dead stop standstill, which kind of where he is now, with slight little momentum, has got to be able to raise enough money in five weeks...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, to get out into the big...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ... to go in California, New York, Texas, and Ohio.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You had an interesting observation when you came on the set about the red state/blue state segment we did.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Your first segment and your third segment are the same.&nbsp; You look at the blue states and the red states.&nbsp; In 50 United States, there's only one state that's gotten more Republican, and that's Minnesota.&nbsp; On the other hand, Texas, Florida, New York...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Jersey.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ...Ohio, Jersey, Pennsylvania have all become much more Democrat, and it's because of Hispanic immigration.</p>
<p>When I worked for Clinton in '96, I did a study where I said at 50-50, dead even like it was in 2000, what state is most in play.&nbsp; And you know what we came up with?&nbsp; New Jersey.&nbsp; Now nobody would imagine that New Jersey could go either way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Not anymore.</p>
<p>Now Bush is calculating what you said, that if he does give the amnesty, all right, he's going to peel off 30 percent of the Hispanic vote.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; This -- this is the equivalent of...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But is that true?</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; It is true.&nbsp; It's the equivalent of John Kennedy winning the black vote by submitting the civil-rights bill and Lyndon Johnson by passing it.&nbsp; The black vote used to be up for grabs.</p>
<p>Republican -- Nixon was -- got most of the black vote in the early going in the polling.&nbsp; It was when Kennedy called Martin Luther King's widow and then filed the civil-rights bill and Johnson passed it that blacks and Democrats became...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So this -- you think this will help the Republicans.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; This is the equivalent of the '64 civil-rights bill, if he can get it through.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, he'll get it through.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; It will flip the Mexican vote.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Do you think so?&nbsp; That big?</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; Not 60-30 to 30-60, but he'll get 35 percent, 40 percent, 45 percent...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's enough to win New Mexico and...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ...because the important thing to understand about the Mexicans is they're Catholic, as are most Hispanics...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ...and on social values issues, they should be Republicans.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, but, you know, all of the blue states that Zogby pointed out to have heavy Catholic populations, and I made the joke...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ...are the Catholics not going to church because the blue states don't go to church.&nbsp; And you know we're generalizing here.&nbsp; Ladies and gentlemen, I live in New York, and I go to church.&nbsp; I mean I'm there alone.&nbsp; Even the priest leaves when I show up.</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; I shouldn't -- anyway, but I don't know if Catholicism or that are kind of a religious outlook means anything in politics anymore.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Oh, it does with Hispanic voters.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes?</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; And, you know, [Mexican President]&nbsp;<b>Vicente Fox</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Vicente%20Fox">search</a>)&nbsp;was my client in Mexico, which is not why I'm saying this.&nbsp; I don't work for him now.&nbsp; But he won on a pro-Catholic thing.&nbsp; He went to the&nbsp;<b>Virgin of Guadalupe</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Virgin%20of%20Guadalupe">search</a>), and he changed the tradition of secular governance in Mexico...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ...to make it link to the...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Howard Dean's even trying to do that now, if he can figure out where Job is in the bible.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; Actually...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; A little arrow here.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; It's -- it's sort of my nightmare.&nbsp; You're trying to brief a candidate running for president, and you have to tell him where Job is.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, Job.&nbsp; But, look, his...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; It's not in the debate handbook.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's why I don't think Howard Dean is as solid as you think he is and some others.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Yes, but wait until they meet Wesley Clark.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, wait, wait, wait, wait, wait, wait.&nbsp; You get into South Carolina, and these people -- they're not going to embrace Howard Dean like in New Hampshire and Iowa, and...</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; No, but the South Carolina primary is half black, and...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; They're -- they're not big Dean fans, African-Americans.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; I think that the point is that the very system that was put in place to stop Howard Dean will now give him the nomination.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, because he's got the money and the others don't have it.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; And because it's such a steep curve over that five-week period...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; ...that Clark and the other guys can't climb.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Dick Morris.</p>
<p>You heard it, everybody.</p>
<p>Thanks, Dick, for coming in.</p>
<p>MORRIS:&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; We appreciate it.</p>
