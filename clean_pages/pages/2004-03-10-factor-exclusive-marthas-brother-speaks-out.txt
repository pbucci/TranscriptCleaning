			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">'Factor Exclusive': Martha's Brother Speaks Out</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-03-10T11:27-05:00">Published March 10, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;March 8, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the&nbsp;&quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, for the first time in five years, Martha Stewart's brother&nbsp;<b>Frank Kostyra</b> (<a href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Frank%20Kostrya" target="_blank">search</a>)&nbsp;talked with his sister outside the courthouse today.</p>
<p>Mr. Kostrya joins us now for an exclusive interview with THE FACTOR.</p>
<p>What did you say to her?</p>
<p><strong>FRANK KOSTYRA, MARTHA STEWART'S BROTHER</strong>:&nbsp; Well, there wasn't much choice -- chance to actually say much to Martha.&nbsp; She was guarded pretty heavily.&nbsp; And I said can you say anything?&nbsp; Do you have a moment to spare.&nbsp; And she said when I come down.&nbsp; And then after she came down, of course, the same barrage was around her.&nbsp; And she said, &quot;here, here's my phone number, call me.&quot;&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Mm-hmm, so have you called her yet?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; She was in a meeting.&nbsp; She said I have to go inside...&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Now you haven't spoken with your sister for five years.&nbsp; What happened?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; It was just a disagreement and I have to honor that.&nbsp; It's something that came between us.&nbsp; And that's just&nbsp; families do that every now and then.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; We did some investigating.&nbsp; Did it have to do with she thought that you sold her out to a tabloid story?&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Oh, that could have been.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; That happened underhandedly from, I guess, &quot;The National&nbsp; Enquirer&quot;.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; .called her, who had just got off the phone with me.&nbsp; I was in a lawsuit with them over photographic rights.&nbsp; And he said, &quot;I understand your brother just sold the story to `The Globe.'&nbsp; I'm from `The Globe' and I'm&nbsp; confirming it.&quot;&nbsp; And that what really sparked it.&nbsp; There was no story.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Never had been.&nbsp; They were flying some gentlemen in because they found out where I lived, and who I was.&nbsp; And they wanted to talk to me, but I didn't.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But couldn't you have gone through your mom, who I know you're close with and so is Martha, and kind of try to patch things up there?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; I don't like to bring mom in the middle.&nbsp; And Martha wasn't&nbsp; going to relent, you know, let it go.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; So she actually held on to her belief that I sold her out,&nbsp;&nbsp; which I really didn't.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Now maybe this will lead to a rapprochement with her when you call her up.&nbsp; And I hope it does.&nbsp; You know, we normally don't like to see families feuding.&nbsp; There's six children in your family.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Oh, my gosh, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Six children.&nbsp;&nbsp;<b>Martha Stewart</b> (<a href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Martha%20Stewart" target="_blank">search</a>)&nbsp;obviously explodes into superstardom.&nbsp; Did that cause problems within the family?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Not at all.&nbsp; No.&nbsp; We're very much supportive of all of her endeavors and proud, very proud.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Mm-hmm.&nbsp; Is she a person who is going to be able to handle a&nbsp; prison sentence of about a year?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; I think so.&nbsp; Dad taught us all well.&nbsp; He brought us through sort of like the Von Trappe family, if you're familiar with that movie.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; &quot;The Sound of Music.&quot;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; And we had to stand up against the wall, shoulders back, head straight up, staunch, straightforward, proud, dignified.&nbsp; Don't, you know, like put on the dial, but don't let them see you sweat.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So it was a very disciplined family?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Very disciplined.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's the -- she puts forth that when you see her in&nbsp; public, that strong, staunch.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You think that hurt her in with the jurors, who thought maybe that strength was arrogance?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; That could be.&nbsp; I hadn't thought about that, but a lot of times I intimidate a lot of people because I'm, like yourself, tall, straightforward.&nbsp; And a lot of people back away.&nbsp; They say I'm afraid of you.&nbsp; I say why?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, absolutely true.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Were you surprised when your sister was convicted?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Very much so, yes.&nbsp; In fact, I wept a little bit.&nbsp; Devastated.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because we weren't surprised.&nbsp; We feel, with all due respect,&nbsp; that she did it.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Oh, you do?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, we feel that she did it.&nbsp; The paper trail was there.&nbsp; We don't know her.&nbsp; We, being me, I mean, I predicted that she would be found&nbsp; guilty, but you were actually surprised when you heard the verdict.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; The whole family was.&nbsp; I immediately called mom and&nbsp; her phone was busy.&nbsp; So I didn't get through to her till the next day actually.&nbsp; And she said, no, it's not like what we had expected.&nbsp; In fact, everyone said things&nbsp; would be fine.&nbsp; And a lot of&nbsp; prayers and it will be all&nbsp; right.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; How old is your mother?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; She's going to be 90 on her next birthday.&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; This must be really tough for her.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Very tough, very tough, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What does she say?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; That she doesn't want to talk about it, she doesn't want to think about it because it's just so upsetting to her.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because she lives near Martha up in Connecticut.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And the two are close?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Very close, yes.&nbsp; Mom is close to all of her children.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Now Martha Stewart is an industry.&nbsp; And to do that, to&nbsp; accomplish what she has done, and I know this because myself -- I mean, you have to devote a lot of time to that.&nbsp; A lot of time.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Oh, a lot of time.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; And that impacts on personal relationships, does it not?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; It certainly does.&nbsp; As you well know, for you to do this show every day, you have to take time to do it.&nbsp; And if you're 100 percent, like Martha is, then there's little time for anything else, I would imagine.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Do you think it impacted on her family relationships?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; I'm pretty sure it did.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; You know, in fact, I think there's some published documents to prove that.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Some distance from her other siblings.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; But that's her choice.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, absolutely.&nbsp; People make choices in their lives and they decide this is what I'm going to do and this is what I'm not going to do.&nbsp; Now you told us that you believe she fell in with the wrong crowd.&nbsp; What's that?&nbsp; What kind of crowd do you think she fell in with?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; I don't think I stated that, did i?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I have it in the pre-interview with you.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Oh, pre-interview.&nbsp; No.&nbsp; I thought that - I think that what the&nbsp; question was is that I believe her defense, her attorneys failed to recognize who her real peers were.&nbsp; And looking back at one of the &quot;Newsweek&quot; magazines in&nbsp; June, maybe a year or two ago, I see a picture of Sam Waksal.&nbsp; And there's the&nbsp; Martha on one side and Jim Kerry - or John Kerry rather.&nbsp; So you would think that these are her peers.&nbsp; But no, these are the people who she is associating with in her business deals.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But she did live a fairly sophisticated, ran with a pretty sophisticated set in Connecticut and in Eastern Long Island out in the Hamptons.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Sure, she worked up to that.&nbsp; And that's what she wanted to do.&nbsp; And you know, why not go with the people that you want to be with?&nbsp; But that's not her peers.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; The peers are the Norman Rockwell people.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, let me submit this to you.&nbsp; Let me submit this to you, Frank.&nbsp; I think she lost her way because she abandoned her roots.&nbsp; She got rich and famous, started hanging with the swells, got in the with the Waksals and weasels of the world, and this is what happened.&nbsp; That if she had stayed with roots, as you just stated, it never would have happened.&nbsp; Am I wrong?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; I couldn't answer that because I don't know.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, you never thought about that?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; No, that's a lot of supposition, I believe.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, I'm good at that.&nbsp; I make suppositions.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Do you really?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But I resist, myself, going with that fast crowd.&nbsp;</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; No.&nbsp; I just think her lawyers made a wrong move.&nbsp; If you look at Norman Rockwell, you see who the peers really are that are around us, the firemen, the policeman, the man buying ice cream for his children and.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You don't think she lost touch with those people, huh?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; She may have.&nbsp; She may have.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I believe she did.</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Frank, thanks for coming in.&nbsp; We know it's a very difficult time for you and your family.&nbsp; And please extend our condolences to your mother, OK?</p>
<p>KOSTYRA:&nbsp; Thank you, sir.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Pleasure to meet you.</p>
