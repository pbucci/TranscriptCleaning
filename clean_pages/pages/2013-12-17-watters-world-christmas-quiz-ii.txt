						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; December 16, 2013. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: In &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, &quot;Watters' World,&quot; the Christmas edition. As you may know, the federal holiday falls on Wednesday, December 25th this year. All federal employees are off but get paid.</p>
<p>Also, a new Rasmussen poll says most Americans strongly believe Christmas should be a part of the public school landscape, 75 percent of American adults want that to happen, just 15 percent disagree, 10 percent don't know what Christmas is.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<div></div>
<p>So, we sent Jesse Watters out to talk to the folks about Christmas.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<p>JESSE WATTERS, FOX NEWS CORRESPONDENT: Merry Christmas.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Thank you. Happy Holidays.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you say, &quot;Happy Holidays&quot; or &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: Happy Holidays. I'm Jewish.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Happy Kwanzaa is a fan favorite in our household.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: I was only having some fun.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Why do people say, &quot;Happy Holidays.&quot;</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: I don't know. Maybe it has something to do with eastern Valentine.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: You've got a point there.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: As a Jewish guy, does it offend you when people say, &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: Absolutely not.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you think you're offending someone by saying &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p>(CRICKET CHIRPING)</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>GENE WILDER, ACTOR: I'm sorry but all questions must be submitted in writing.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Is Jesus an important figure in your life around this time of year.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: I'm an atheist, so no.</p>
<p>WATTERS: You're an atheist. Do you celebrate Christmas.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: I do.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I don't know if I can allow that because you can't kind of have both.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: Oh, yes.</p>
<p>WATTERS: What does Christmas mean to you.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 6: Sharing.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Where was Jesus born.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: In Bethlehem.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Jerusalem.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: Nazareth.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Probably in some kind of hospital, Siberia.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>WATTERS: The Three Kings came to bring him gifts.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: From Newark.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>WATTERS: Jersey.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: Yes.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>YUL BRYNNER, ACTOR: Sometimes, you leave me breathless.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: What gifts did the Three Wise Men bring.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 3: I don't remember that. Frankincense and -- I don't know, gold, silver --</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>-- I have no idea.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: Myrrh and sneakers?</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: Frankincense, myrrh and gold.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Now, what is myrrh. Do you guys have any idea.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: It's like that weird noise when a cat is in heat maybe.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTRESS: Ma, ma.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you know where the Christmas tree tradition originated from.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 7: Sweden.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 3: I'm guessing somewhere in Europe. Maybe somewhere in Siberia.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: The United States.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: I think it was Germany.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: This is just random guess. Like Germany maybe.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Nailed it.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>STEVE CARRELL, ACTOR: That's what she said.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Christmas is a federal holiday. Do you know who proclaimed it a federal holiday.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: Lincoln.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Ronald Reagan.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p>WATTERS: Take a guess. Nineteenth century.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: George Washington.</p>
<p>WATTERS: That was the 18th century.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 1: Jefferson.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Grant.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 2: OK. So, he grants us Christmas.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Good one.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Are you a caroller.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Not in public.</p>
<p>WATTERS: On the first day of Christmas, my true love gave to me.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 2: A partridge --</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 5: -- in a pear tree.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Silent night --</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 8: -- holy night.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Honestly, I have no idea.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 8: All is bright.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 4: Zooming out, into another dimension now.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: Are you finished.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 8: Sleep in heavenly peace.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: I give --</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: You're lucky there's no mistletoe here.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: Eww.</p>
<p>WATTERS: You're a huge Scrooge.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 4: Thank you.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Do you ever watch THE O'REILLY FACTOR on Fox News.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 3: Sometimes, yes.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED ACTOR: (Speaking in another language)</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE 7: I have not read &quot;Killing Jesus.&quot; Is that about the war.</p>
<p>(BUZZER SOUND)</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>BRUCE MCGILL, ACTOR: War is over, man. Wormer dropped the big one.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>WATTERS: Have you ever watched &quot;Watters' World.&quot;</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: Yes.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I'm Watters.</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE 1: I'm pumped to be let into your world, Watters. I'm psyched about it.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEOTAPE) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, here he is. Now, it seemed like everybody was kind of cooperative again.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Yes, yes, cooperative.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Nobody fled.</p>
<p>WATTERS: No one fled, except me.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, you got attacked by the pigeon.</p>
<p>WATTERS: I did.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And why, why.</p>
<p>WATTERS: He was lucky, I didn't connect there.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>I had a little power in that --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Why. You would have been arrested and Peter would have come and --</p>
<p>WATTERS: I know he would have come. No, I am afraid of pigeons. I don't like them.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, that's your problem.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>Why did they attack. Why did they attack. Why are they on this guy's head.</p>
<p>WATTERS: The guy was feeding pigeons with crumbs.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, he's feeding.</p>
<p>WATTERS: And they were so comfortable, they perched on his shoulder.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right. He's feeding them.</p>
<p>WATTERS: And I think they're like flying rats.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>So, I don't want to make time for them.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Like some Woody Allen line. I thought that was Mike Tyson. He's a big pigeon guy, too.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>Now, people write me letters. They all, &quot;Look, Watters is looking for the dumbest people. That's who he's out looking.&quot; Do you have a big sign, &quot;If you're dumb, please talk to me.&quot;</p>
<p>WATTERS: I am attracted to dumb people.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>There's this natural attraction there. I seek them out. Sometimes, I find out. But we do have --</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>-- fair piece. I just happened to me in Washington Square Park.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK. Tell the audience -- tell the audience what the &quot;Watters' World&quot; selection process is.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Oh, this is --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Who gets on.</p>
<p>WATTERS: -- this is secret, Bill. This is the secret sauce here.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, this is like secret sauce.</p>
<p>WATTERS: It's a lot of instinct. OK, I look for personality.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right.</p>
<p>WATTERS: OK. I look for looks and I look for someone that's a good talked.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Do you have auditions. Do they have to do a dance.</p>
<p>WATTERS: In my mind, OK.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right.</p>
<p>WATTERS: No, you know, I'll either look --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, before the camera goes on, --</p>
<p>WATTERS: -- for someone with a crazy hair, someone with the good looks.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Wait, wait. So, before the camera goes on, you ask them a few questions just to see what they're like.</p>
<p>WATTERS: No, not at all.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, you go cold?</p>
<p>WATTERS: We start right away.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK.</p>
<p>WATTERS: And then if they're bad, I just bail immediately.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK.</p>
<p>WATTERS: But if they're good, we warm up.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You ask permission before you can talk -- before you talk to them.</p>
<p>WATTERS: Well, I say, you know, &quot;I'm Jesse from THE FACTOR. I want to talk to you about Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And then they say another thing.</p>
<p>WATTERS: And then we go.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. I think you got &quot;Watters' World.&quot; And if you're a pigeon, get him.</p>
<p>WATTERS: No, please no.</p>
