			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Is America Going Down the Tubes?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2011-01-14T00:00-05:00">Published January 14, 2011</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="4498339">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/4498339/is-america-going-down-tubes" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/011411_oreilly_quinn_FNC_011411_20-36.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

<p><em></em></p>
<p><em><strong>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; January 14, 2011. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</strong></em></p>
<p><em></em></p>
<p></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<div></div>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight: There's a show on Broadway called &quot;Long Story Short&quot; starring Colin Quinn, the former &quot;Saturday Night Live&quot; guy. In fact, Quinn is the only person in the show. I saw it last week. Very good.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Colin Quinn joins us now. And you wrote the show.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>COLIN QUINN, COMEDIAN/ACTOR:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>You wrote it yourself, and just you. And very, very funny. Basically, the program traces civilization and its downfall.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>You go into Romans, Greeks and, you know, and then you get to America. But I want to save that. The first thing I want to ask you is Jerry Seinfeld directed the show. Is that correct?</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Correct.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. You just stand there. That's all you do. What exactly did Jerry do? Say, &quot;Stand a little closer to the audience? Stand a little further back?&quot;</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN: </strong>What if I had moved around? He's the one that said, &quot;Don't move. Stand there.&quot;</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Don't move, just stand there. Now, did you pay Seinfeld for that? It's just you!</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> He needs the money. I had to -- I had to take care of him.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Because I was watching, you know, &quot;directed by Jerry Seinfeld.&quot; It's one guy on the stage. He's just standing there. All right. America today, are we going down the tubes like the Greeks and Romans?</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Sure we are.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>We're going down?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Sure. Don't you think?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>You know, I'm asking you, man.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Well, I mean, the whole point of the -- one of the themes of the show is how, you know, we keep doing the same things and expecting different results, things that used to work. So I talk in the show about dissent and debate used to work for our country, but it doesn't work now. It's just like, you know, tribalism worked. The Greeks thinking -- they tried to think their way. We're like a compilation of every empire as they fall. We're like the Costco of empire declines. We have to combine every aspect of every decline.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Everything that everybody in the past has done wrong we're doing now?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> We're doing it all.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And how does it manifest itself?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN: </strong>Just excess. I mean, with us, it's like excess, but it's also the fact that like now, like even years ago, people used to say we left -- all the manufacturing jobs left the country.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> And it was just all moving money. But now it's not even moving money. Now it's not even moving the idea of money. People's -- most people's job is talking about the future or like money not even in the present tense. It's not even paper. Doesn't even...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Like the hedge fund guy. Nobody knows what that is.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Right. Because it doesn't exist.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> You're trading the idea of somebody else doing something...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And they make all this money and don't really do anything. There's nothing there.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> How long do we have, Colin? Because I'd like to, you know, make a getaway if I could just before it all goes down.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN: </strong>A lot of people saying next year, 2012, but that's the Mayan. That's South America.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Keep an eye on the Visigoths. They're in Canada now and kind of inching their way. But also you think that we're culturally in decline because of things like the &quot;Jersey Shore,&quot; right?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Well, I mean, we've always been like that. I mean, that's -- &quot;Jersey Shore&quot; is an easy one to attack, of course, because the Greeks, at least -- at least when they watched plays they weren't frivolous. They were, like, watching nobility and all these, like, deep thoughts. We're just watching, like I say, Snooki losing her cell phone.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes, because you know, if you look at what's going on on the &quot;Jersey Shore,&quot; it's almost like the Greek plays in a way. Because, remember, the Greeks, Oedipus, the mom and all of that? You know, if you just look, boil it down, I don't know what they're doing. I never watched the &quot;Jersey Shore.&quot; It frightens me, and I thought it was a zombie thing at first. But they're really -- they're real people.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> I think it's -- yes, it frightens you because you're -- we're Irish.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> That's not our part of the Jersey Shore.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, no, that's the Italian...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> We're Belmar. That's an Italian show.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>I know Italians who are just appalled by that.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Yes, I know, too.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Even -- even...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Probably too close to them.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Here's how bad the &quot;Jersey Shore&quot; thing is. Even organized crime is appalled by it. Even the Mafia is going, &quot;Hey, you're giving us a bad name.&quot; Tony Soprano, trying to make me look bad.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN: </strong>People are making that into, like, the focal point when it just kind of symbolizes everything. They're just, you know, they're not even like the Italians when I was growing up. They just basically -- they don't even fight.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> They just get mad. They sit down. You never see them growing up. And they're sitting there like, &quot;I feel like we're not connecting.&quot; They start having these heart to hearts with each other.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> It's like a touchy-feely new nation.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes. I liked &quot;West Side Story&quot; better when they just attacked each other.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Yes, attacked.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Now, do you -- do you watch, like, &quot;The Factor&quot; and cable news and...?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Of course I do.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> And listen to talk radio to draw from your material?</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> I do. I will prove to you I watch &quot;The Factor.&quot; I think you've been very bullying to Steve Doocy lately. I think Steve brings out something in you...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> ...from, you know, teenage years where you just bully him.</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Well, I see Steve as part of the decline of America.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Of course you think he's...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY: </strong>Yes, but it's fun. Miller...</p>
<p><strong></strong></p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> His insouciant attitude.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Miller, who you know very well, has done very well here on &quot;The Factor.&quot;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> That's why I said insouciant. Insouciant because you know?</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes. Combining the humor with the, you know, actual culture. You do the same thing on the Broadway stage. Very Miller-esque. And I want everybody to go see it. &quot;Long Story Short,&quot; directed by Jerry Seinfeld, who does absolutely nothing, but there you go. Colin, thanks for coming in. Good to see you.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>QUINN:</strong> Thanks a lot, Bill.</p>
