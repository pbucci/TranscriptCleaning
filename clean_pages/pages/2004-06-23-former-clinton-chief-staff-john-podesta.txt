			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Former Clinton Chief of Staff John Podesta</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-06-23T14:34-04:00">Published June 23, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;June 22, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp; In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, a tale of two Democrats.&nbsp; As you may know, we have good relationships with a variety of Democrats, including&nbsp;<b>John Kerry</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=John%20Kerry">search</a>),&nbsp;<b>John Edwards</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=John%20Edwards">search</a>), and&nbsp;<b>Joseph Lieberman</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Joseph%20Lieberman">search</a>), just to name a few.&nbsp; But we do have trouble with the Clintons who don't seem to approve of the No Spin Zone and some others, many of whom live on the far left fringe.&nbsp; Joining us now from Washington is the former chief of staff to President Clinton, John Podesta, who apparently is mad at me for some reason.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Now I'm just going to quote the CyberNews (ph) service here.&nbsp; I don't know whether this is true or not.&nbsp; You can set me straight.&nbsp; This is a quote. &quot;John Podesta also took a jab at FOX News Channel's Bill O'Reilly, when an audience member asked about the possibility of filing lawsuits against news outfits that quote, 'propagate direct lies,' unquote<strong>,</strong> Podesta responded, what about television?&nbsp; It's time to sue Bill O'Reilly.&quot; The crowd of liberal activists showed their approval by erupting in applause.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Well, what are you going to sue me for?</p>
<p><strong>JOHN PODESTA, FORMER WHITE HOUSE CHIEF OF STAFF</strong>:&nbsp; Well, that's a little bit off, Bill.&nbsp; I said that one of our fellows, Eric Alterman, was contemplating a lawsuit against you because you told a lie about him.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And what lie was that?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You said on this show that he was a confidant of Fidel Castro.&nbsp; That's not true.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Did you see that show?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; It's in your transcripts.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; You saw the transcript but you didn't see the show.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I did not see the show.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Would you like to hear it?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You have gotten a letter...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, we got some dopey letter from his lawyer.&nbsp; And they are going to sue.&nbsp; But&nbsp; you, John <b>PODESTA</b>, former chief of staff to a president of the United States, you firmly believe in your heart that I told a lie about your pal, Eric Alterman, is that correct?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You called him a confidant of Fidel Castro.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Let's roll the tape.&nbsp;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Now, Jane, you're in academia.&nbsp; You teach at American University.&nbsp; Do you know Orville Schell, the dean of the graduate school of journalism at the University of California Berkeley?</p>
<p>JANE HALL:&nbsp; I don't know him, I know who he is.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp;&nbsp; You know Orville, right?&nbsp; How would you categorize him as far as politics are concerned?</p>
<p>HALL:&nbsp; I would say he was probably to the left and liberal.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp;&nbsp; Yes, around Fidel Castro, right?&nbsp; Somewhere in there?&nbsp;</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Somewhere in the Straits of Florida, because we have had Dean Schell on the program.&nbsp; And he wanted to take my car and I wouldn't let him.&nbsp; Anyway, he reviews a book by Eric Alterman, another Fidel Castro confidant, &quot;What Liberal Media?&quot;, and Orville loves it.&nbsp;</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; And I was obviously being facetious.&nbsp; And you can see the laughter and the smiles and all of that.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; But you know, Bill, you can blow all this stuff off by saying, it's all a big joke.&nbsp; But Eric Alterman is an anti-Castro liberal.&nbsp; He has got a strong record of it.&nbsp; And you're making him seem like he's a confidant of Fidel Castro.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; So you believe, John Podesta, former chief of staff to a president of the United States, that I was actually accusing Mr. Alterman of being a confidant of the dictator of Cuba?&nbsp; You believe that?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Bill, look, you know, you could...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Wait, wait, Mr. Podesta, do you believe it after hearing the tape or not?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I believe that that's your description of him, and you owe him an apology for it.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Now look, fine, we'll let the audience -- they all saw it, they all saw the context.&nbsp; And you believe that I should be sued for making that remark, correct?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Bill, I think all Eric wants is to set the record straight and say that he is an anti-Castro liberal.&nbsp; If you're just willing to admit it...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; And you...</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; If you're willing to admit it, we could let this thing go.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Oh, OK, sure.</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You do it all the time, Bill.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All the time?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You do it all the time.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; What do I do all the time?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Well, you compare Bill Moyers to Mao Zedong.&nbsp; You say that's a joke.&nbsp; You compare Al Franken to Joseph Goebbels, you know, the Nazi propagandist...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; That was Michael Moore, by the way.</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; ... you say that's a joke.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; That was Michael Moore.</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You know, it's all a big joke to you, Bill.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; It's satire.&nbsp; You know how that satire thing goes now, don't you?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; That's not funny, Bill.&nbsp; It's just not funny.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Oh, fine, I mean, you may not think it was funny.&nbsp; But it was done in jest.&nbsp; It was done to make...</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Watch &quot;The Daily Show,&quot; put on Comedy Central, watch &quot;The Daily Show,&quot; you will see what funny is.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Fine.&nbsp; But you do know that many, many people watching you now are going to say you're totally unreasonable, this was a joke, he was being facetious, and you, Mr. Podesta, a man of esteem, all right, took it out of context, ran with it, used your little Web site to say O'Reilly did this, he's an evil guy, he's a liar.&nbsp; Now, I'm not...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I didn't call you a liar.&nbsp; And I didn't say you were an evil guy.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, you did.&nbsp; You used the word on this interview, Mr. Podesta,&nbsp; that you told a lie about Eric Alterman.&nbsp; And that's what you guys trade in all the time.&nbsp; And that's what I object to, sir.&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Hey, you know, it's a two-way street here, buddy.&nbsp; You do this all the time as well, you label people, you smear people.&nbsp; And we're just asking for the record to be set straight.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Can you give me one example where I smeared someone?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; How about referring to Al Franken was Joseph Goebbels?&nbsp; That was...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I did not refer to him as Joseph Goebbels.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You compared him to him.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; That was Michael Moore.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Come on, you do this...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Look, I asked you for one example where I smeared somebody.&nbsp; You just misstated what I said.&nbsp; I said that Michael Moore is a propagandist just as Joseph Goebbels is a propagandist?&nbsp; Would you deny that?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I think that comparing Michael Moore to a guy who set up the propaganda for the Nazi regime that resulted in the killing of 6 million people through the Holocaust, yes, I think that's an over-bloviated...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I said that Michael Moore is a propagandist and so is Joseph Goebbels.&nbsp; And then I explained what propaganda is.&nbsp; Now Michael Moore has called the president of the United States in a time of war -- Michael Moore has called the president of the United States in a time of war a deserter.&nbsp; Is that OK?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; You know, I'm not here to defend Michael Moore...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, that's good.&nbsp; Is it OK for anybody to call the president of the United States a deserter, in your opinion?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; That does not compare him -- that does not equate him to Joseph Goebbels, which is your -- you know, that's what you do.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I mean, I did it in the sense that they were both propagandists.&nbsp; I didn't equate what they do.&nbsp; I said, here's a propagandist and that's another propagandist.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; And that's what we seem to be engaged in now, your propaganda to try to smear these people.&nbsp; And I think it's...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I'm not trying to smear them.&nbsp; Would you deny that Michael Moore is a propagandist, are you denying that?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Michael Moore is a documentarian, whether you want to call him a propagandist or not is your opinion.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You wouldn't call him a propagandist?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I haven't seen this film so I...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, I mean, based on his statements and his other work, you wouldn't call him a propagandist?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Yes, well, I think he's a man of strong views who disagrees with most of what I believe in, including -- he said the other day that he never voted for Clinton, he doesn't vote for Gore.&nbsp; He's a pretty far left-wing guy, I don't know.&nbsp; But I don't equate him with Joseph Goebbels.&nbsp; I think that's really unfair and out of bounds.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, the allusion was made to explain what propaganda is and where it emanated from.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Oh come on.&nbsp; You know that that's not why we...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And we have got the transcript.&nbsp; We've got the transcript.&nbsp; I can do just what I did with Alterman if you want.&nbsp; But anyway, I've got one more question.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Then put it up.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I want to talk to Bill Clinton.&nbsp; Is there any way on this earth that I can talk with him?&nbsp; Would you ask him to come in here?&nbsp; Because we have been pretty respectful of him.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Call his staff.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; We haven't (ph) called.&nbsp; They said we're on the list, I think we're right below Al-Jazeera in line for that interview.&nbsp;</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, why wouldn't he come in to chat with me?&nbsp; Sell a lot of books, could change some minds.&nbsp; We have by far the biggest audience in cable news.&nbsp; Why won't he come on in?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Well, I think you ought to take that up with him.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Would you recommend that he come on?</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I think that he's out talking to -- I think he's out talking to millions of American people through the media outlets that he's finding.&nbsp; I think he's doing just fine.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, but he's cherry-picking, though.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I don't know, it depends on whether or not you will admit that Eric Alterman isn't...</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I will play the same clip.&nbsp; I mean, I was fooling around there.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; Would you admit that he's anti-Castro?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Sure.&nbsp; Eric, baby, you're anti-Castro.&nbsp; All right?&nbsp; That was a joke, Eric.&nbsp; I'm having a little fun at your expense.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, Mr. Podesta, when you see Bill and Hillary, tell them, look, here we are, and we want -- ready to believe them.&nbsp; Thank you, sir.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>PODESTA</b>:&nbsp; I will do it.&nbsp;</p>
