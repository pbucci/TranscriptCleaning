			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">John Stossel Names Christmas-Friendly Companies</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2009-12-09T11:26-05:00">Published December 09, 2009</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>

					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; December 8, 2009. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Stossel Matters&quot; segment tonight: A few years ago it was very hard to find companies that actually said &quot;Merry Christmas&quot; to their companies — to their customers, I should say. But that has changed, partially because of &quot;The Factor's&quot; coverage. So what's going on this year?</p>
<p>We asked Fox Business guy John Stossel, who has a new program debuting on Thursday, to make a list of Christmas-friendly companies and those who are Bah humbug people. Here's Stossel.</p>
<p>Let's take the good guys first. Who's doing a good job? How did this come about, by the way? I guess we should say.</p>
<p><a onclick="foxVideoPlayer('12342968','','Tis the Season');return false;" href="#"><b>Click here to watch the segment!</b></a></p>
<p><strong>JOHN STOSSEL, FOX BUSINESS NETWORK ANCHOR:</strong> Probably because of you talking about it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Well, yes, but there was a group — there's a group that did this.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> The American Family Association.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> StandForChristmas.com, StandForChristmas.com. And this was people who actually voted for the companies they thought were best.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Right. Or they talked about their good or bad experiences. But it's not that scientific.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No, it's like a BillOReilly.com poll. Right. But it's worthy because of the people — I trust the folks, and I think you do, too, right, to tell the truth?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Depends on which folks we're talking about.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Regular folks. Just the people who — customers who go into a store. They're not going to lie. They come in, said I had it good or had it bad. First one is the Bass Pro Shop.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Top of the list was the Bass Pro Shop.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Cabela's, K-Mart, Lands End and Sears. And at the bottom of the list: Old Navy, American Eagle Company, Best Buy, Banana Republic and Gap.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now, the Gap, Best Buy, Banana Republic, American Eagle and Old Navy. They're hip, young; most of them want to sell to young people. So they say, &quot;Happy Holidays.&quot; They don't say, &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot; That's what tees them off, people off.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Except sometimes they do say, &quot;Merry Christmas,&quot; but some customers complain that it wasn't being said enough.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Enough.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> But that's the beauty of the free market. If you don't like their presentation — some of these companies are more sensitive about wanting to appeal to non-Christians — the market will sort it out. If you don't like it, you won't go there. If enough people don't like it, they'll lose business and probably change their policy.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now, as a libertarian, you don't really care whether people say, &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> I do not care.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So you walk in and somebody goes, &quot;Happy Holidays,&quot; that doesn't bother you at all?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Doesn't bother me.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Do you say, &quot;Happy Holidays&quot; back, or do you say &quot;Merry Christmas&quot;?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Sometimes I say &quot;Merry Christmas,&quot; just to — if I think they're being politically correct, I say, &quot;Merry Christmas&quot;...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Just to annoy them.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> ...just to annoy them.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> OK, that's the kind of guy you are.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now, retail-wise, do the companies that say, &quot;Merry Christmas&quot; and go Christmas big-time do better?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> The companies at the top of the list increase sales 1 percent. The companies at the bottom of the list increase sales 1 percent.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> OK, so it was a wash?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> It was a wash.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> OK. So it comes down to people, they would rather — I think most Americans, 72 percent, the polls say, like the greeting &quot;Merry Christmas&quot; in the Christmas season. Twenty-four percent like &quot;Happy Holidays.&quot; So I think that, if you have a good product that they want and you say, &quot;Merry Christmas,&quot; that's a winner right there. Would you disagree with that?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> I think these people know more how to run their business than I do. And if it was good business practice, they'd all be saying, &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Do you really believe though that pinhead CEOs — I mean, come on, you know what corporations are — that tell their — and this is why we got involved about four or five years ago. They were ordering their employees not to say, &quot;Merry Christmas.&quot; Do you really think that they know how to run a company better than you do? Would you do that?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Well, I think they figured it out pretty quickly.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Why did they figure it out? Why?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Because they were losing business.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Why were they losing business?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Because Bill O'Reilly gave them a bad rap.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You bet! And I got a lot of good presents from Santa for doing that. But my point is that I thought it was fascism, fascism, which offends a libertarian like you, for a CEO or a store manager to tell their employees, &quot;You better not say, 'Merry Christmas,' even though the reason we're selling stuff is because of Christmas.&quot; Isn't that fascism?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> No. It's — it's ownership. He built the business. If he says, &quot;Stand on your head, sing when people come in,&quot; you don't have to work there. You can quit. It's his business.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> And the union won't tolerate that. You can't stand on anybody's head if you're in the — if you're a Teamster. They'll cut your head off if do you that, Stossel.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Well, that's closer to fascism. But I couldn't call the unions fascism.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So you weren't — you weren't offended by the management saying to employees, &quot;You can't say 'Merry Christmas'&quot;?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> No, these are scared people who were frightened by the left. &quot;Oh, we're going to get sued for religious discrimination&quot; or something.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> So...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right. I want to do two things. The — the Christmas-friendly by the folks: Bass Pro Shop, Cabela's. Is that how you say it?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Cabela's.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> OK. It's a gun shop. Lands End, K-Mart, Sears. OK.</p>
<p>Now, Stossel's new program debuts this Thursday, 8 p.m. on Fox Business Channel all across the country. Why should I watch it?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Because it's going to be great, and it's going to crush your show. We're going to talk about...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You're up against &quot;The Factor&quot;?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> That's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Right. And you know why they did that?</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Because they're tired of you? And they want — they want...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> They want you out of here. You're making too much money. DVR Stossel. And you're going to be on again Friday, right? Friday at 10.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> Against my old show, &quot;20/20.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's right.</p>
<p><b>STOSSEL:</b> I'll crush you. I'll crush &quot;20/20.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Anybody want to make a wager on that? All right. John Stossel, everybody.</p>
