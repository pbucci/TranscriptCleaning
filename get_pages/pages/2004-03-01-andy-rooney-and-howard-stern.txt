			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Talking Points</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Andy Rooney and Howard Stern</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-03-01T13:38-05:00">Published March 01, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>	                
		            </div>
	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>Hi, I'm Bill O'Reilly.&nbsp; Thanks for watching us tonight.</p>
<p><b>Andy Rooney</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Andy%20Rooney">search</a>)&nbsp;and&nbsp;<b>Howard Stern</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Howard%20Stern">search</a>), those guys are the subject of this evening's &quot;Talking Points Memo.&quot;&nbsp; As you may know, Mr. Rooney made some personal comments about Mel Gibson I found unfair.&nbsp; And I would like to talk to Mr. Rooney about them.&nbsp; But the CBS commentator doesn't want to explain himself and he told radio guy&nbsp;<b>Don Imus</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Don%20Imus">search</a>)&nbsp;why.&nbsp;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>ANDY ROONEY, &quot;60 MINUTES&quot;:&nbsp; You know, O'Reilly swore at me because I won't go on his show and make a fool of myself.&nbsp; He said I'll go on &quot;Larry King,&quot;&nbsp; but he won't go on with me.&nbsp; I mean, he's the worst interviewer there is, and -- but I don't want to go on there and get killed.&nbsp; But what was your question?</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Now if I'm the worst interviewer there is, a bright guy like Mr. Rooney could make me look foolish.&nbsp; But we all know that's not what's going on here.&nbsp; What's actually happening is that a group of very powerful media people, including Mr. Rooney, are trying to destroy Mel Gibson because they don't like this movie</p>
<p>In today's &quot;New York Times,&quot; for example, there's an article that says Gibson may have trouble finding work in Hollywood, a thinly-veiled threat to others who might do what Gibson did.&nbsp; And Rooney himself continues to blast Gibson in the movie.&nbsp;</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>DON IMUS, RADIO SHOW HOST:&nbsp; Have you seen this Mel Gibson movie?</p>
<p>ROONEY:&nbsp; No, I haven't.&nbsp; I don't plan to see it.&nbsp;</p>
<p>IMUS:&nbsp; Do you...</p>
<p>ROONEY:&nbsp; I mean, I don't want to pay $9 just for a few laughs.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Laughs?&nbsp; What does that is mean?&nbsp; What's funny about the execution of Jesus?&nbsp; Again, if you're in a position of power, you smear someone or say something that might demean millions of Christians, you have an&nbsp; obligation to clarify your remarks in a debate situation.&nbsp; These cheap drive-by by Rooney comments are brutally unfair and deserve scrutiny, as does the continuing campaign to injury Mel Gibson, being run by secular media people.</p>
<p>Now Howard Stern is also under scrutiny.&nbsp; His program has been yanked from six&nbsp;<b>Clear Channel</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Clear%20Channel">search</a>)&nbsp;radio stations because of sexual content.&nbsp; As you know, Congress is conducting hearings on media indecency and companies such as Clear Channel could get hurt big time.&nbsp; You can thank&nbsp;<b>Janet Jackson</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Janet%20Jackson">search</a>)&nbsp;for all fuss.</p>
<p>As for Mr. Stern, he has always run a sex show.&nbsp; It's what he does.&nbsp; For adults, there's no problem.&nbsp; For children, big problem because the cavalier attitude and explicit tone of the sex conversation is definitely for what they call mature audiences.</p>
<p>If Janet Jackson didn't happen, however, Howard Stern would not be feeling the heat.&nbsp; But many Americans and their elected officials have had it with libertine entertainment.&nbsp; And the backlash is in play full force.&nbsp; And that's <em>The Memo</em>.</p>
<p><strong>The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day</strong></p>
<p>Time now for &quot;The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day.&quot;</p>
<p>I visited the ABC program&nbsp;<b>&quot;The View&quot;</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=%22The%20View%22">search</a>)&nbsp;to promote my novel,&nbsp;<b>&quot;Those Who Trespass,&quot;</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=%22Those%20Who%20Trespass,%22">search</a>)&nbsp;this morning.&nbsp; As usual, things got wild when Mel Gibson's movie came up.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He has a right to put this movie on the screen without being branded an anti-Semite and all of this stuff.&nbsp; Now why are they doing that?&nbsp; Why are they doing it?&nbsp;&nbsp;-- They're doing it because there's a war in this country between traditionalists and secularists.</p>
<p>You see this on the gay marriage thing.&nbsp; And the secularists do not want Jesus being injected into any policy debate.&nbsp; They don't want it in the public arena, and they're going to send a message, as they sent today in &quot;The New York Times.&quot;</p>
<p>Gibson will never work again, they're not going to hire him, that if you do this kind of a thing, a film, you're going to be punished for it, and I think that's un-American.&nbsp; I think that's...</p>
<p>JOY BEHAR, &quot;THE VIEW&quot;:&nbsp; I disagree with that completely.&nbsp; I don't agree with that.</p>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp;&nbsp;<b>Joy Behar</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Joy%20Behar">search</a>)&nbsp;and I kissed and made up a few minutes after that.&nbsp; Always enjoy talking with the ladies.&nbsp; It's never ridiculous.</p><div class="show-callout oreilly-factor fn2">
