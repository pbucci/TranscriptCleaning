			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Kerry and the Swift Boat Veterans</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-10-19T14:23-04:00">Published October 19, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;</em> Oct. 18, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong>&nbsp; In “The Factor Follow-Up&quot; segment, as we mentioned in the &quot;Talking&nbsp; Points Memo,&quot; one of John Kerry's big problems is that many Americans&nbsp;simply don't understand what he's all about.&nbsp;One way to get a clue is to&nbsp; read the book &quot;Tour of Duty: John Kerry and the Vietnam War.&quot;&nbsp;It's now out&nbsp; in paperback.</p>
<p>And joining us from New Orleans is the author Douglas Brinkley.</p>
<p>You know, I just -- after -- this is amazing.&nbsp; Just after our segment&nbsp;with Ray Flynn, I got handed a statement from Stephanie Cutter, who's the&nbsp; spokesperson for the Senator, and -- talking about his partial-birth abortion vote against the ban, and it's the same old story.</p>
<p>He wants the life or health of the woman.&nbsp;The life of the woman is already in that ban. He wants the health, which people could object to,&nbsp;because it could be a headache or anxiety or anything like that.</p>
<p>Is Kerry a true abortion believer in your research?</p>
<p><strong>DOUGLAS BRINKLEY, AUTHOR, &quot;TOUR OF DIRTY&quot;:</strong>&nbsp; No.&nbsp; I mean, I think he's&nbsp; somebody who's for Roe vs. Wade, and that's going to be his stance.&nbsp;He said&nbsp; it in the last debate.</p>
<p>I think when he gets into abortion or gay rights or what we call the&nbsp;cultural wars in America, he's not on as firm a footing as he is when he's&nbsp; dealing with some of the traditional Democratic issues like Social&nbsp;Security, health care on the domestic front, and so it's a tough issue for&nbsp; everybody.</p>
<p>And Bush in the same way -- you saw in the debate -- tries to avoid&nbsp; answering a straight yes or no about Roe vs. Wade.&nbsp; Kerry did answer it on&nbsp;the third debate.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp;Well, I think Bush, if I recall, said he wouldn't overturn&nbsp;it.&nbsp;He just wants to work for fewer abortions, which I think is probably&nbsp; the best thing to do.&nbsp; Now -- but the partial-birth is a whole deal.&nbsp;You&nbsp; know, it passed so overwhelmingly in both the Senate and the House, and to&nbsp; be out there with it, I think, hurts him.</p>
<p>Now, in Boston, as I told the audience before, John Kerry was known as &quot;live shot&quot; because he was showing up at your house to get on TV news, and now he's shy.&nbsp; Does that surprise you?</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; Well, you know, Kerry after -- in the '80s for a period of&nbsp; time was actually a radio broadcaster in Boston and did -- he used to do&nbsp; interviews like you do on your &quot;Factor Radio&quot; show.</p>
<p>I think what he's trying to do this year by&nbsp;[doing]&nbsp;less interviews than one&nbsp;would expect is to seem presidential.&nbsp;There's a feeling like -- John Edwards is out on the talk-show circuit constantly --&nbsp;and there's a danger of going&nbsp; on these shows if you slip up.</p>
<p>It's really a &quot;I-got-you&quot; politics now.&nbsp; Every time President Bush,&nbsp;whether he does the Tim Russert show or your show, there's always going to&nbsp; be this headline that, you know, the media grabs and throws at you, and I&nbsp;think Kerry's trying to be cautious.</p>
<p>He wants -- we have Bush as commander in chief.&nbsp; I think Kerry wants&nbsp; to be seen as in kind of an equal footing in being very select.</p>
<p>With that said, Bill, I think they have a decision to make whether&nbsp; they want to do a number of more interviews these last three or four weeks&nbsp; and try to control the agenda, and I think we'll have to wait and see what&nbsp; they do.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Well, they better get on it because if they say the last three or four weeks, there's only two.&nbsp; So they'll -- if they book us in mid-November, it ain't going to happen.&nbsp; It's not going to help them.</p>
<p>But I really believe that John Kerry is a mystery to many, many&nbsp; Americans.&nbsp; They simply do not know who he is, and unless he starts to open&nbsp;up a little bit -- and I know the gotcha game, I mean, and it's vicious --&nbsp; he's going to lose, and that's -- you know, we've said it, and we'll leave&nbsp; it there.</p>
<p>Now your book is interesting, because in the paperback, which is just&nbsp; out, you did some further research on the swift boat vets, and I believe that these guys have hurt the Kerry campaign more than anything the Bush&nbsp;people have done.&nbsp;What do you say?</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; Well, right now -- certainly in August and September, they&nbsp; did.&nbsp;The only -- and we all know all of that story.</p>
<p>The -- what I have to think -- another way to look at it, Bill, is&nbsp; that these -- in many ways, Kerry is known for Vietnam, and, compared to&nbsp; Bush's service in Vietnam and now, when Kerry is criticizing Iraq, the fact&nbsp; that he criticized the Vietnam War in many ways makes his biography&nbsp; interesting.</p>
<p>Unlike, say, Dukakis or Mondale or other Democratic nominees, people&nbsp; know who Kerry is, and so in a lot of ways, you're seeing the left and some&nbsp;people that didn't like Kerry unifying as backing, vouching for Kerry&nbsp;versus the swift boat attacks.&nbsp; I think we'll only know the impact of the&nbsp;swift boat attacks on Kerry after the election.</p>
<p>If Kerry wins, they will look like they were failed.&nbsp; If Kerry loses, it will seem like perhaps the most -- or one of the most significant&nbsp;strikes against him.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Did you find anything new about the swift boat guys?</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; Well, I had interviewed in my original book all of Kerry's&nbsp; guys that were on the boat.&nbsp; I asked myself a simple question.&nbsp; Who was on&nbsp; the boat with Kerry?&nbsp; Everybody except one guy on the boat is for John&nbsp; Kerry.&nbsp;Many of them are Republicans.</p>
<p>The one guy I've added to the paperback is Steven Gardner, who was&nbsp; known as &quot;the wild man.&quot;&nbsp; He was a gunner on 94, and what I found out is when&nbsp;Kerry -- the animosity that Gardner has for Kerry is because he was firing&nbsp;his gun in non-stop fashion, Kerry screamed at him and threatened to court-martial him.&nbsp; Hence, Gardner has been sore all these years on that.</p>
<p>There were other things that I found out, you know, in the book,&nbsp;mainly more about a man named Roy Hoffman who's been leading the attack against Kerry and just how many Vietnam veterans have come forward and e-mailed to me talking about the things that Hoffman did over there.</p>
<p>And I think if there was -- in the coming months and even after, even if Kerry loses and he's dipping out of the news, I think people will be&nbsp; evaluating what Hoffman did in Vietnam very carefully.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; What about the aftermath?&nbsp; I think that's the genesis of&nbsp; the swift boat ads.</p>
<p>You see, I believe the swift boat guys have a right to speak out, on both sides, supporting Kerry and also not liking him.&nbsp;I believe that all of these guys are patriots.&nbsp;They put their life on the line --&nbsp;and certainly can&nbsp;speak out and say whatever they want.&nbsp;-- I have no problem with it.</p>
<p>But it seems to me that the anger about him is really what he did when he came home.</p>
<p>Now I've got some very straightforward questions.&nbsp; Did he say that he himself, John Kerry, committed atrocities in Vietnam?</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; On a television show, while showing footage, he talked about&nbsp; -- saying that I'm guilty, too.</p>
<p>What he meant by that is that there's a free-fire zone in Vietnam.&nbsp;So, when you fired in a mangrove or were ordered to, you know, blow up&nbsp; things on the side of a river bank, you didn't know what exactly you were&nbsp; doing.</p>
<p>There is not an incident where John Kerry purposely killed a human&nbsp;being that was a civilian.&nbsp; But, by random shootings that they were ordered&nbsp; to do, you never knew who was being killed.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Yes, but, I mean, that doesn't seem like an unreasonable&nbsp; order, to spray mangroves when you're going into enemy territory.</p>
<p>Now the second question is: Does John Kerry realize that POWs were&nbsp; confronted with some of the words that he used in the press?&nbsp;Does he&nbsp; understand that, and what does he think about that?</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; I don't know the answer of what he thinks.&nbsp;What I can tell&nbsp;you is that I have just now today got to see this film they're talking&nbsp; about putting on Sinclair, and the -- it's -- first off, you're right.&nbsp;&nbsp;What's difficult is to criticize any POW. They went, they served, they&nbsp; went through hell.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; What I have to do is [look at]&nbsp;this story and to say: Are they&nbsp;telling the factual -- is this a factual presentation?</p>
<p>And, for example, there are only three POWs in this whole film of --&nbsp; with loads of them that were -- who say that Kerry's&nbsp;testimony&nbsp;somehow affected their lives, and the problem is two of them have previously said&nbsp;that he had not affected their lives, that they had not been tortured after&nbsp; April '71.</p>
<p>So they've kind of contradicted themselves, and that's been the&nbsp; problem with this whole swift boat thing.&nbsp; One person says one thing; one,&nbsp;another.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Yes, it's the fog of war all over again.&nbsp; But there is no&nbsp;question that a person like&nbsp;<b>Jane Fonda</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Jane%20Fonda">search</a>)&nbsp;gave the North Vietnamese comfort by&nbsp;her activities and what she did, and now I think a lot of people say Kerry did the same thing.</p>
<p>Your assessment?&nbsp; And this is the last word.&nbsp; Go ahead, Douglas.</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; No.&nbsp; John Kerry and Jane Fonda have no interaction. They met at one rally where they were with a lot of people.&nbsp;Then they were in Detroit in the same room.</p>
<p>She, after all, was&nbsp;<b>Barbarella</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Barbarella">search</a>)&nbsp;before 1972.&nbsp; She was a star.&nbsp; People in the armed forces even liked her.&nbsp;Young people.&nbsp;She was a sex symbol. Kerry never -- their paths never crossed.</p>
<p>Kerry's testimony, April '71, was an attempt to save lives, and, even&nbsp;after his testimony, thousands of Americans were -- continued to be killed&nbsp; in Vietnam.&nbsp; he thought that he somehow wanted torture to come to POWs is&nbsp;just ridiculous.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Professor, as always, we -- thanks for your time.</p>
<p><b>BRINKLEY:</b>&nbsp; Thanks, Bill.</p>
