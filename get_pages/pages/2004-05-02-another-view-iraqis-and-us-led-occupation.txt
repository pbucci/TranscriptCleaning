			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Another View of the Iraqis and the U.S.-led Occupation</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-05-01T21:19-04:00">Published May 01, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;April 29, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp; Now for the top story tonight, another view of the Iraqi population.&nbsp; Joining us now from Washington is Entifadah Qanbar, the D.C. director of the Iraqi National Congress and spokesman for Hamad Chalabi, a member of the Iraqi Governing Council.&nbsp; Mr. Qanbar is just back from Iraq.</p>
<p>All right, where am I going wrong here, sir?</p>
<p><strong>ENTIFADAH QANBAR, IRAQ NATIONAL COUNCIL</strong> :&nbsp; I think you have a national ally in Iraq, the Shi'ia.&nbsp; However, the Shi'ia have the 1991 complex.&nbsp; I think the biggest mistake happened that lost a lot of ground for the U.S. popularity is the acceptance of resolution 1483 and accepting becoming an occupier.&nbsp; I think the U.S. could have taken the higher moral ground again and become a liberator and let the Iraqis rule themselves.&nbsp; We said from day one we made this advice and people did not listen to us.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right now, you know, a lot of people don't know what that resolution is.&nbsp; So why don't you tell us what that is?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; The resolution is, according to the resolution, the U.S. accepted herself to become an occupier in Iraq and to become&nbsp; the direct ruler in Iraq.&nbsp; Iraqis...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; What you wanted was as soon as Saddam was deposed, you wanted your guys to go in and take over without a vote or anything?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, so what you wanted was as soon as Saddam was deposed, you wanted your guys to go in and take over without a vote or anything?</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; No, not our guys.&nbsp; We wanted to have a provisional government that could have worked between all types of Iraqis.&nbsp; We would leave seats open for those areas to be liberated.&nbsp; We would have our own flag.&nbsp; We would have a difficulty to put a flag...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes but who is &quot;we?&quot;&nbsp; I mean, you've got -- the&nbsp; Sunnis don't like the Shi'ites and vice versa.&nbsp; Nobody likes the Kurds.&nbsp; The Kurds are -- feel oppressed.&nbsp; Come on, I mean you know what kind of tribalism you have in your country, sir.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; I don't think that's true completely.&nbsp; I think if there was any sectarian animosity there, you would have seen much worse wars between those sectors.&nbsp; And the wars happened and sectors of the Iraqi people did not fight...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; All right, so say you're right and America&nbsp; made this mistake by not handing over immediate authority to an&nbsp;&nbsp; Iraqi provisional council, although I don't know how they could do&nbsp; that with all the chaos, but say you're right.</p>
<p>I think anybody would say 700 dead, thousands wounded,&nbsp; shouldn't the Iraqi people say, hey, we're going to cut them a little slack?&nbsp; Maybe they did make some mistakes here, but they're really looking out for us.&nbsp; They want us to have freedom, but that's not happening in Iraq.&nbsp; And that's what I object to.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; Bill, we have the 36th battalion Fallujah, fought along the U.S. Marines.&nbsp; We could have expanded this battalion and made it all over Iraq.&nbsp; And the Marines do not have to fight the front line.&nbsp; Iraqis could fight the front line.&nbsp; You have some bureaucrats here in Washington -- politicians do not accept this idea.&nbsp; Every time the Iraqis want to take over their own political leadership, somebody...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But you're -- all right.</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; ...will jump individual and stop it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But you're getting at June 30.&nbsp; You're going to get your governing council June 30.&nbsp; And next year, there will be an&nbsp; election.&nbsp; The U.S. will provide security and so will the Brits.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; Excuse me.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Go ahead.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; Bill, they're trying to give it now to the United&nbsp; Nations.&nbsp; They want to give an Arab nationalist, who says all over the place I am proud to shake as few Jews as possible, to rule Iraq, and to become the person who would put Iraq back and...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, I have no use for Rahimi either, but look,&nbsp;&nbsp; you're always going to have political problems in this kind of a situation.&nbsp; What it comes back to is the Iraqi people at this juncture don't appreciate what America and Great Britain have done for them.&nbsp; And that really angers Americans, particularly military families who lost people, sir.&nbsp; You've got to understand that.&nbsp; This is -- this is tilting the whole thing here.&nbsp; You know, in the beginning, we all wanted to liberate you guys.&nbsp; We all were on your side.&nbsp; And now. it's about 50/50.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; Well, we appreciate that, but being an occupier is not necessary.&nbsp; I think America was put in this hole by some permanent members of the security council.&nbsp; And that was not necessary.&nbsp; America could have stayed as a liberator and the Iraqis could have appreciated.&nbsp; This happened in France after the second World War.&nbsp; This happened in Germany.&nbsp; To go directly, rule Iraq did not work.&nbsp; And you know, a lot of Arab countries are, you know...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; I got it, but I'm not buying it.&nbsp; I really am not buying it.&nbsp; I mean, we have done a tremendous favor for the Iraqi people.&nbsp; We have tried to rebuild the infrastructure.&nbsp; $200 billion of&nbsp; American tax money is going over there.&nbsp; And you guys don't appreciate it.&nbsp; You don't.&nbsp; And that's going to turn -- that is going to popular opinion against the Iraqi situation.&nbsp; And in the long run, it's going to come back to bite you all right you know where.&nbsp; And I'm going to give you the last word.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; The thing is we can have an Iraqi government with American help as a strategic ally, and you'll see the situation will improve dramatically.&nbsp; And the Shi'ia still feel this is only a honeymoon and there's some minority will come back and rule them again.&nbsp; And this recruitment of generals is making the situation worse.&nbsp;</p>
<p>We need to have a new Iraq, where the Iraqi government represents and reflects the true combination of the Iraqi people, ruled by Iraqis with American help as a strategic ally.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, Mr. Khanbar, we appreciate you taking the time to help us out.&nbsp; Thank you very much.</p>
<p><b>QANBAR</b>:&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
