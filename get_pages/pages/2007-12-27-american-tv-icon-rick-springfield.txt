			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">American TV Icon: Rick Springfield</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2007-12-27T12:15-05:00">Published December 27, 2007</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p><em>This is a rush transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; December 26, 2007. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>JOHN KASICH, GUEST HOST: </strong> In the &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, a TV icon and rock 'n' roll heartthrob.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>RICK SPRINGFIELD, ACTOR/SINGER (singing):</strong> Jessie's girl. I want Jessie's girl.</p>
<p>Don't talk to strangers. Baby don't you...</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> Don't talk to strangers</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>KASICH:</strong> Remember those songs? I guess I'm old enough that I do. They put Rick Springfield on the map back in 1981, setting off a frenzy among young women.</p>
<p>At the same time, Springfield bolstered his fame with a star turn as Dr. Noah Drake on TV's daytime drama &quot;General Hospital.&quot; Now, at age 58, he's out with a new Christmas album called &quot;Christmas With You,&quot; and proceeds go to the Gold Star Mothers. Springfield recently entered the No- Spin Zone with Bill.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE)</strong></p>
<p><b>RICK SPRINGFIELD, MUSICIAN/ACTOR:</b> Today is Rick Springfield day.</p>
<p>(MUSIC: &quot;Don't Talk to Strangers&quot;)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now it's 1985. You're a big star, one of the biggest stars in the country, &quot;General Hospital.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> You don't have to throw bouquets my way just because struck on triage together.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I wouldn't throw them if they weren't...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Had huge albums and all that.</p>
<p>(MUSIC)</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> And then you kind of disappear, and I understand, it's because you had a depression situation?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> Well, actually, it was triggered by — the &quot;disappearance&quot; was triggered by the birth of my son. I was pretty burned out in '85 and was getting — starting to get into some issues. I'd lost my dad earlier and hadn't really dealt with it, and suddenly I became a father. And I had to — it was the right time for me to stop and start to look at things.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So your son is born, and you want to spend more time with him and the family. But you did have a depression situation?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> Yes. Yes, I went through some pretty dark stuff.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Tell me about that. Because some people don't understand. I mean, guys like me, we remember you. I mean huge.</p>
<p><strong>(MUSIC: &quot;Jessie's Girl&quot;)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Women throwing themselves at you. How could you be depressed?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I think it was to the point where you realize that you're — that the material things won't bring you happiness in and of itself. And that was really the wall I hit, was that wall.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So you weren't happy with all this fame and fortune?</p>
<p><strong>SPRINGFIELD (singing):</strong> You've got the power, you've got the love, to lead me to heaven or lead me to heartbreak.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I did feel fortunate that I'd, you know, finally hit something, but in the end when it was just me by myself, it was — you know, I was still me.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> So you were lonely, was that it?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> Darkness. That this wasn't what I thought it would be. This didn't heal me is basically it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> OK. Why did you need to be healed?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I don't know anybody who — who truly goes, you know, I'm right there. You know, everybody is dealing with issues.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You were born with great talent, obviously.</p>
<p><strong>(MUSIC)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Did you ever pinpoint the fact that why you were unhappy, when you got into this situation, where you were one of the biggest stars in the world?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I'd been a pretty — a pretty dark teenager, you know, it's...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Not a happy kid?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> No. I was a happy kid up until I hit the teen years.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now were you drug involved at all back then, in the '80s, you mean?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> No, no, I'd actually gone through my whole drug phase in the '70s. When I was in Vietnam, I started smoking dope, and — and I got into acid for a while. But it was all — that was not — I've always been a very driven person, and that was very counter to me doing what I wanted to do.</p>
<p><strong>(MUSIC)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now, you're 58 years old. And you have a new Christmas album here.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> The whole point of me doing a Christmas record and what I centered it around was the song &quot;Christmas with You&quot; from the point-of- view of the soldiers in Iraq.</p>
<p><strong>(MUSIC)</strong></p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> The proceeds go to the Gold Star Mothers, who are parents who have lost sons and daughters over there.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You do understand the sacrifice.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> Absolutely.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You know, I just got back from Afghanistan. It's hard to get people to go over there, celebrities like yourself to even shake their hands.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I performed for the troops in Vietnam in '68 and '69. And it's a very different war now. And I think, you know, I see soldiers walking through the airports, see people applauding or saying hi to them.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> But not the celebrities. The celebrities haven't stepped up. No Bob Hope.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I think that's probably mainly fear.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You think so?</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> I know Gary Sinise has gone over there.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Great guy.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> And I know — I know people who have gone over there. And — and we're planning to go over there hopefully April, May.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> We applaud that. You're helping out the Gold Star moms, and we wish you the best with the album, Mr. Springfield.</p>
<p><b>SPRINGFIELD:</b> Thank you.</p>
<p><strong>(MUSIC)</strong></p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEOTAPE)</strong></p>
