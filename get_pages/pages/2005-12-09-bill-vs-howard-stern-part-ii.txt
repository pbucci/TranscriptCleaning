			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Bill vs. Howard Stern, Part II</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2005-12-09T10:20-05:00">Published December 09, 2005</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> December 8, 2005, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight — and this might not be for children — part two of our interview with <b>Howard Stern</b>. As we told you last night he's now the highest paid entertainer in American history and his success is based upon a very simple concept, saying whatever pops into his mind, no matter how offensive it is. No, I have not copied that concept. Roll the tape.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEOTAPE)</strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Who's your primary audience? All entertainers on all these programs have a primary audience. Who is your primary audience?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> You know as well as I do, my primary audience are strippers, hookers, and crack whores. And I love them all. My primary audience is anyone who likes to laugh. I don't look at my audience as a demographic. I look at it as a psychographic.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Do you think it's more men than women?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Yes. There are more men than women.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Why is that?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> I have always envisioned the guy I'm doing the show for is driving to work. He's a buddy of mine. We're in the locker room, and man, we're talking reality. We're not talking politely. We're being honest, we're being real.</p>
<p>The secret to my show isn't dirty words. The secret to my show isn't potty humor. The secret to my show is honesty, reality — that I will say the truth.</p>
<p>I was interviewed by &quot;60 Minutes.&quot; They asked me, &quot;How did you wish death on a guy?&quot;</p>
<p>I said, &quot;Do you mean to tell me that you never wished that somebody was dead? When you saw those planes go into the Twin Towers, did you wish that <b>Usama bin Laden</b> was caught and killed?&quot;</p>
<p>Well, I feel the same way sometimes when my back is up against the wall. I want Usama bin Laden dead. Our whole country does. And I've wished death on people on my show. People go, &quot;That's outrageous!&quot;</p>
<p>I go, &quot;No, I'm just willing to admit it.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. So your audience is blue-collar guys, working guys, regular guys?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> No. It's not just blue-collar guys. People like to think it's like a bunch of beer-guzzling guys. There's a thing called &quot;The Scarborough Report&quot; and maybe you're aware of it, because you're doing radio now. They analyze not who's listening by age...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes, demographics and everything.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> They find out income, college education, and I have probably the most educated audience and highest grossing income audience. Dare I say more than you, <b>Rush Limbaugh</b>, anybody else. It is a phenomenal audience, a brilliant audience.</p>
<p>It's not, you're going to judge my audience by the guys who call in? No. That's not right. That's about a half of one percent.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Is it still going to be lesbians on parade and strippers and all that?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Bill, there will always be lesbians on this show. I make this vow to you. As long as I'm breathing, there will be lesbians. I will give the people lesbians, because there is nothing sexier in this world, besides you, there is nothing sexier than two women getting it on.</p>
<p>And man, I'm going to do it. In fact, I'm going to take a lesbian dating game, and I'm going to blow it up into an hour show on my channel. You'll see the date.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Will it cost extra? Is that under the $12?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> That's all under the $12.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Not as good as the Bill O'Reilly mug. How much is that?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You want to get into that now?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Let's talk about that <em>chazarai</em> [Yiddish word for &quot;junk&quot;] now you're selling. It looks like you emptied out a junkyard and slapped your name on everything.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK, here's where we go.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> And you've got a briefcase, the Bill O'Reilly briefcase. I wouldn't be caught dead with that. No offense.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, of course not.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Who's walking around with a Bill O'Reilly briefcase? I never would.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, of course there's no offense. You wouldn't offend me at all, would you? You would never do that.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Yes, I would. Sure, I would.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I'm stunned. You didn't wish me to get cancelled, did you? On your show?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Not yet.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. Now listen, you go on your show, and you say, O'Reilly's selling all these, what <em>tchotchkes</em>? Is that what you said?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> <em>Tchotchkes</em>. Garbage.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Garbage. OK.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Welcome mats. Bill O'Reilly welcome mat.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. All right, whatever. Now you know that all the proceeds go to charity.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> I don't believe that.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You don't believe it?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> What charity?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. <b>Habitat for Humanity</b>, I just wrote them a big check.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> What is Habitat for Humanity?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> That's when people, like you, if you cared about other people...</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Me? Yes.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> ... would then build a house for poor people. Novel concept. In fact, you know what you could do?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> And 100 percent of the profits?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes. Everything I get. You know what you can do, though?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Doesn't this appeal to your ego? You make a lot of money. Can't you donate money to charity?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I do that anyway. This is extra.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Why does this news...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Are you filibustering now? Because I have a good idea for you...</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Come outside with me right now. Let's...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You can join Habitat for Humanity and build houses, Howard Stern, for poor lesbians. I mean, this would be perfect.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Now you're thinking.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> This would be perfect.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Now you're thinking like a degenerate.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All the money that I derive from BillOReilly.com from &quot;Factor&quot; gear goes to charity, to help kids and poor people. And you are mocking it.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> I'll tell you why I mock it. I mock it because you have such an ego.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You're telling me?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Yes. At least there's some thing I don't slap my name on.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> What? What?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> You have your name on jackets, mugs, pens, papers.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> It's all for charity.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Listen, you're a wealthy guy. Give money of your own to Habitat.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I do that.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Let FOX News donate money to Habitat. You should take those welcome — when I'm walking around with a billboard of the Bill O'Reilly Show.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Do you want a &quot;Factor&quot; jacket, by the way?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Yes, give it to me for free.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right. Give me the &quot;Factor&quot; jacket. I'm only going to give you this &quot;No Spin&quot; jacket if you wear it. Are you going to wear it? Look, this is a beautiful, beautiful jacket.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> It's not. Let me tell you why it's not. Can I be a fashion designer for a second?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Look at the way you're dressed.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> You see this? This grabs you around the middle. A guy's got a gut, this is going to squeeze his fat.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> This is a terrific garment. Now are you going to wear it? I'll give it to you.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> The &quot;No Spin&quot; jacket?</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes. With the flag, the American flag. You are American.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> I won't wear it, but I will give it to a crack whore.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No. You're giving it back. I'm not having this on some lesbian somewhere. I'm not going to have it.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> But I'm proud of you. You're doing well.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Thank you. I know you're only kidding around.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> No, I'm not! I mean, you...</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> This stuff does good for a lot of people. Let's talk about you.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> You like your name on that stuff. It's ego.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> No, it's no spin. It's no spin.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> All right. Go ahead.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> We were both raised in working-class families on Long Island, OK? Stern and O'Reilly. We both wind up at Boston University at the same time.</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> That's right.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> We're Terriers together, OK? We both reached the top of our professions. Ten million-to-one shot for me...</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Eight hundred billion-to-one shot for me.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> How did it happen for you?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Well, I mean, I had a dream — you know, like Martin Luther King. When I was 5 years old, my dream was to be on the radio and to do something so revolutionary — to make radio sound real. Some people say our show was the first reality show. And I believe that.</p>
<p>I went on the air with a concept that I had when I was 5 years old. I was fascinated by the medium, but at the time, no one looked at me, especially my own father — and he'd say, &quot;How could you be on the radio? I know announcers.&quot;</p>
<p>My father was a recording engineer and a radio engineer, and he said, &quot;I know announcers. You don't speak well. You don't have a nice voice.&quot; He couldn't grasp the concept that maybe I could be a success at this.</p>
<p>And so my whole life has been devoted to proving him wrong, and showing him that I could be on the radio, and that I could make radio one of the most important mediums.</p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Does that still drive you today?</p>
<p><strong>STERN:</strong> Absolutely. I think so.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEOTAPE)</strong></p>
<p><strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Now, tomorrow we'll talk about Stern's very unusual private life. It is somewhat difficult.</p>
