			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Are Two CBS News Icons Out of Control?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-03-08T17:01-05:00">Published March 08, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;March 5, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the &quot;Impact&quot; segment tonight, for decades,&nbsp;<b>CBS News</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=CBS%20News">search</a>)&nbsp;has been charged with a liberal tilt by some conservatives, a charge the network firmly denies.&nbsp; But two CBS icons,&nbsp;<b>Walter Cronkite</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Walter%20Cronkite">search</a>)&nbsp;and&nbsp;<b>Andy Rooney</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Andy%20Rooney">search</a>), both continue to make very provocative left-wing comments.</p>
<p>In an interview with &quot;The San Francisco Chronicle,&quot; Cronkite calls religious opposition to gay marriage obnoxious and says he would have been happy to marry a man.</p>
<p>And in his column -- weekly column, Rooney wrote the same thing he said on Don Imus' radio program, that he would not see the movie &quot;The Passion&quot; because he doesn't want to spend $9 for, quote, &quot;a few laughs.&quot;</p>
<p>With us now are&nbsp;<b>Liz Trotta</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Liz%20Trotta">search</a>), former CBS News correspondent and current Fox News analyst, and Jonathan Klein, a former CBS News Vice President and&nbsp;current CEO of &quot;The Feedroom,&quot; an online news service.</p>
<p>Let's go with your first, Mr. Klein, because you used to manage these guys.</p>
<p><strong>JONATHAN KLEIN, FORMER CBS NEWS EXECUTIVE</strong>:&nbsp; Try to.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Sometimes.&nbsp; You add to the mix,&nbsp;<b>Bill Moyers</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Bill%20Moyers">search</a>), a left-wing bomb thrower, if there ever was one, and you have three very prominent presences at CBS that are coming out of the closet as real far left guys.&nbsp; Did you know they were that far left when you were working there?</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Well, I didn't overlap with Walter at all.&nbsp; Walter...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He was on the board of directors, though, when you were...</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Yes, but I mean I came into CBS in 1982, and Walter had left there in 1981.&nbsp; So he really wasn't a figure with any kind of center of gravity there as soon as he got off the air.&nbsp; He may have been on the board of directors, but he really didn't have an influence anymore the way that, you know, all -- his successors and the people who came in after him did.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; But still he was revered by CBS and put up there as the stalwart for many, many years.&nbsp; Are you surprised at this left-wing tilt he's taken?</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Well, I'm not sure if it's left wing.&nbsp; It's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, let me read you -- are you sure you want to go there, Mr. Klein?&nbsp; I've got 15 quotes.&nbsp; You know, I&nbsp; mean listen to this.&nbsp; &quot;I think very&quot; -- this is Walter Cronkite.&nbsp; &quot;I think very definitely foreign policy could have caused what has happened on September 11.</p>
<p>&quot;The problem is this great division between the rich and the poor in the world.&nbsp; We represent the rich.&quot;&nbsp; I mean our foreign policy caused 9/11?&nbsp; This is about as far left as you get.&nbsp; This is Noam Chompsky stuff.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; If you take stuff out of context, but, in the whole, I think the whole has probably uttered more words in public than any human ever, and I think, on the whole, you can look at what he said over time and know pretty much that, you know, the guy lives in a townhouse, you know, sails a yacht, lives the good life.&nbsp; He's hardly what I would think of as a bomb thrower.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He wasn't married to a man when you were...</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Not that we know of.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>What do you think, Liz?</p>
<p><strong>LIZ TROTTA, FORMER CBS CORRESPONDENT</strong>:&nbsp; Oh, this is hilarious.&nbsp; I mean, you know, Walter is a -- is not a homosexual man.&nbsp; He's a heterosexual man.&nbsp; But he's also an older man now, and I think he was caught on the fly.&nbsp; I think he said it in jest.&nbsp; And I think he also said it because there's a sort of fashionable liberalism about it, and he is an old-time liberal.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK, but that's the point.&nbsp; Is he?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Classic.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So he's...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Old-time.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... a classic old-time liberal, all right?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And Rooney is what?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Rooney's a freak.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What does that mean?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, Rooney, is -- you know, has made a whole career out of trying to be a professional curmudgeon, when, in fact, there's quite a mean streak there, especially when he goes on Imus and says that everybody has invented God so they can tell their problems to somebody.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, he's an anti-religionist.&nbsp; I mean...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, it's a sort of fashionable paganism, which permeates network news, by the way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Would he -- would Rooney be a left-wing guy, in your opinion?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Oh, I think he -- well, he would die if you said that to his face, but, you see, these people all live in an echo chamber, and nobody thinks they're left wing.&nbsp; They just all think the same way.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; But I mean Rooney's insulted women.&nbsp; He's insulted blacks and Native Americans.&nbsp; He's -- you know, he's insulted a rainbow coalition of Americans.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, I understand, but what I'm trying to get at is I think there's some validity to the charges over the decades that CBS News was primarily a left-wing-driven organization.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Well, this we know.&nbsp; We know that you think that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK, but is that true?</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; I don't think so.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You don't?</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; So you've got a Moyers, you've got a Rooney, you've got a Cronkite.&nbsp; Rather -- I think Rather is more interested in being a reporter than an ideologue, but his daughter is a big Democrat in Texas and -- you know, come on.&nbsp; So why would you not think it?&nbsp; If everybody's quacking, why wouldn't they be a duck?</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; Well, you have to ask who everybody is, first of all, because these guys...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, these are three powerful guys.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; These guys are -- Andy is a commentator, and he's paid to have outlandish opinions, and so, you know, in fact, without an Andy Rooney, there wouldn't be a bill O'Reilly today having a place in a network news environment.&nbsp; The others have, you know, long passed through the halls of CBS and gone on to other things.</p>
<p>But I think, actually, what it is -- if you want to get a glimpse inside the workings of these networks, and you and I are both veterans of CBS News, along with Liz, you know that most of these people -- I'll admit there's a cult of radicalism, but it's radical narcissism more than anything else.&nbsp; You know, people...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I agree with that.</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; No, it's more than that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; I think it's more than that.&nbsp; First of all, these people -- you mentioned Rather, for example.&nbsp; These people don't live like average Americans, nor do they have contact with average Americans.</p>
<p>One of our favorite sayings among the correspondents when I was there -- and I like to think it was part of the glory days was -- God, does any of this stuff ever leave the building, or is it closed circuit?&nbsp; And well, you know, this is really what we believe about everything.</p>
<p>Now take the abortion story, for example.&nbsp; There is no way in the world that anybody could defend NBC or CBS or ABC on coverage of the abortion center.&nbsp; I'm sorry.&nbsp; It was simply a left of center approach.&nbsp; Most of the people they had were radicalized feminists who were the producers working on this stuff.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; But you...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; That is the single most paramount example of where their politics come from.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; I won a Peabody Award at CBS news for coverage of&nbsp;<b>Randall Terry</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Randall%20Terry">search</a>)&nbsp;and the Right to Life movement, a one-hour&nbsp;<b>&quot;48 Hours&quot;</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=%2248%20Hours%22">search</a>)&nbsp;episode that I reported with my friend,&nbsp;<b>Bernie Goldberg</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Bernie%20Goldberg">search</a>), and...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Who is conservative.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; ... we -- yes, exactly.&nbsp; So he gave them a lot of air time...</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; ... conservative, and so I was.</p>
<p>KLEIN:&nbsp; ... and we made sure of it, and we won awards for it.&nbsp; So I never sensed, either as a producer, which I was for 12 some odd years, or as an executive, any kind of agenda setting.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I felt that -- and I only worked there a short time -- that there was an exclusionary bias at CBS, that if you brought a story in that was praising a pro-life person, it would never make the air, and, if you brought a story in, as I did, a very controversial story about gays in Provincetown acting out on the streets and the Portuguese people who live there were appalled, it never ran.&nbsp;&nbsp;<b>Howard Stringer</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Howard%20Stringer">search</a>)&nbsp;killed it with the blessing of&nbsp;<b>Dan Rather</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Dan%20Rather">search</a>).</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, he's the epitome of what we're talking about, but, again, these people have dinner with, shop with, socialize with -- they don't go to church, for the most part.&nbsp; They only do this with people of the same level or above.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Of their own...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; That's it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Are Cronkite and Rooney -- are they past their prime and just saying stuff to get attention?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; I mean, you know, if you -- what bothers me is that there's a whole bunch of people out there in America who really believe he is a funny, little, nice-hearted, warm-hearted curmudgeon.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You're talking about Andy Rooney.</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; As for Rather -- I think, you know, Rather knows his days are numbered.&nbsp; I think network news' days are numbered.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What about Cronkite who's still put up as the epitome of the American anchorman?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, the thing that surprised me is when Cronkite said just recently in the same interview that he came out against the war on Vietnam because CBS urged him to.&nbsp; I think that was rewriting history a little.&nbsp; There's no question in my mind he did it on his own, and it changed the whole face of opinion in this country.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; And that actually was not a bad thing because the Vietnam War at that point...</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, I thought it was a very bad thing.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Did you?</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because I thought that war was really a destructive war.</p>
<p>TROTTA:&nbsp; Well, we'll have to talk about that on another show.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, sure.&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Well, there you go.&nbsp; And we tried to keep it fair and balanced, but Walter wants to marry a man, and Andy thinks the crucifixion is funny.</p>
<p>All right, Liz, Mr. Klein, thanks very much.</p>
