			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Will Pres. Bush Support a Constitutional Amendment Defining Marriage?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-02-23T17:27-05:00">Published February 23, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;February 20, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p>Now for the top story tonight, will President Bush endorse a&nbsp;constitutional amendment to declare marriage exclusively between a man and&nbsp;a woman?</p>
<p>Joining us now from Washington, Bay Buchanan, president of the&nbsp;<b>American Cause</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=American%20Cause">search</a>)&nbsp;organization, a traditional conservative group.</p>
<p>You know, today in New Mexico, they started issuing marriage licenses&nbsp;to gays. And it's over now. The dam is burst and every little&nbsp;municipality who wants to defy the law will. And what do you think Bush is&nbsp;going to do about it?</p>
<p><strong>BAY BUCHANAN, THE AMERICAN CAUSE:</strong> You know, Bill, it's astounding&nbsp; that these leaders have taken so long already to respond. What's he going&nbsp; to do about it? I believe that George Bush is in total agreement with the&nbsp; millions of Americans that feel the sanctity of marriage does need to be&nbsp; preserved in this country, that it's the backbone, that its tradition is&nbsp; the backbone of this nation, its strength is the nation's strength.</p>
<p>And I think that he will come out in favor of it. But the key here is&nbsp; we need him to do more than that. We need him to be a leader, and to take&nbsp; charge of this, and to spearhead it, and be a voice for millions and&nbsp; millions of Americans, and to make certain that this amendment passes so&nbsp; that marriage is preserved. We need him to take the kind of leadership on&nbsp; this issue, on this war, as he has done in Iraq and in Afghanistan.</p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> Yes, but he clearly has not done it in four years. He has&nbsp; not been a culture warrior. All right? Meantime, the secular forces have&nbsp; made enormous gains. And you're seeing that now.</p>
<p>You know, out here in California, one of the most liberal states in&nbsp; the union, if not the most liberal, you know, prop passes, direct vote,&nbsp; marriage between a man and a woman. Two-thirds of Californians say, yes,&nbsp; that's what we want. They agree with you, Ms. Buchanan. And then Newsom&nbsp; gets elected and he says, look, I don't care what they want. I'm going to&nbsp; do what I want. Schwarzenegger goes and hides. And Bush is not an&nbsp; aggressive guy in the culture war.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: It's almost -- it's incredible to me. It's such an easy&nbsp; issue that could be presented here, as you pointed out. The majority of&nbsp; Americans are obviously with us on this. It's an extremely important issue&nbsp; and paramount to the base of George Bush.</p>
<p>But you know, when you go out there to San Francisco, what should&nbsp; happen? Somebody should take that guy and recognize what he's doing. He&nbsp; has no respect for the law. Neither respect or regard...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You say -- what do you mean somebody?</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: He should be locked up. He...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: OK, yes. But we know...</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: ... costing California millions and millions of dollars.&nbsp; There are going to be lawsuits left, right, and center. And for what?&nbsp; Because this guy has a personal selfish objective he's pursuing?</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But Ms. Buchanan, look, you can say he should be locked up,&nbsp; but the truth is we've been out here all week covering this.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Nobody is going to do it. The attorney general's not going&nbsp; to do it, Schwarzenegger's not going to do it, and the feds really don't&nbsp; have any jurisdiction here. I mean, they can't come in and do anything.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: I understand that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And it should have been done through the courts. If Mayor&nbsp; Newsom thought it was a violation of the California constitution, we&nbsp; respect that. Go through the courts, have your city attorneys litigate it.&nbsp; And that's how we are in this country.</p>
<p>But now we have unilateral action. But you know, Ms. Buchanan, I'm&nbsp; going to be -- go back to my &quot;Talking Points&quot; memo. There is no leadership&nbsp; on the traditional side. The only people who really speak out are the&nbsp; religious people themselves, Robertsons and the Falwells. But in the&nbsp; public arena, there's just nobody there. Nobody.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: You know, that's not quite true. There's enormous...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Who?</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: ... strength in the states. There's enormous movement.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, give me leader? Give me one elected leader&nbsp; who's a leader to protect traditional values? Give me one.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: The problem with your question is you used the word&nbsp; &quot;elected.&quot; If you want to talk about...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, that's -- but isn't that a problem?</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: ...(UNINTELLIGIBLE), no, there are...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: We have a Congress -- we have a president -- all right,&nbsp; look, we have both houses of Congress controlled by Republicans and a&nbsp; Republican president. And Ms. Buchanan...</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: I'll tell you what...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You cannot name one leader...</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Yes?</p>
<p>O'REILLY: ... that will stick up for traditional values. That&nbsp; means...</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Tom Tencredo out there.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: ... you're going to lose the war.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Tom Tencredo...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's an immigration war.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Well, no, no, he's good on all issues. But let me just&nbsp; make this point. There are leaders in this country, not all of them are&nbsp; elected. And there's millions of Americans that listen to those leaders.&nbsp; And this is a key issue. There's a coalition they put together. And we&nbsp; want to see marriage preserved. And we're willing to fight for it on every&nbsp; single front, Bill.</p>
<p>And what we're going to be talking to the president of the United&nbsp; States --we've talked to them now. We understand he's going to support us.&nbsp; If there's hesitancy, as there has been this last week, he is going to be&nbsp; in trouble on election day. Now I don't expect that because the most&nbsp; important thing is to have him with us and spearheading this.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, let me ask you one more question. Why is there&nbsp; hesitancy on the part of President Bush to get out in front of this issue&nbsp; and the culture war in general?</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: I believe there's clearly -- there's different messages&nbsp; coming out of that White House. There must be an internal battle going on&nbsp; over there as to what the proper thing to do is.</p>
<p>What George Bush has got to understand is he's got to look into his&nbsp; heart and soul and recognize he's been put in this position as president at&nbsp; a critical time for this nation. And this issue is one of those issues&nbsp; he's got to spearhead. He's got to present real leadership on, or he will&nbsp; falter because this is what he's called upon to do right now. He can't&nbsp; listen to the pollsters. He can't listen to his strategists. He has got&nbsp; to do what is right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: He is a decisive, bold leader. That is his greatest asset&nbsp; is his leadership quality. He's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, he's decisive and bold on terror issues. He has not&nbsp; been decisive and bold...</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: He has not.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: ... on security issues inside this country and on the&nbsp; culture war.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: They are intimidated.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Ms. Buchanan, thanks very much.</p>
<p>BUCHANAN: Thank you very much.</p>
