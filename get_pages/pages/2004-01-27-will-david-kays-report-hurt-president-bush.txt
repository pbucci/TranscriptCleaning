			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Will David Kay's Report Hurt President Bush?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-01-27T12:58-05:00">Published January 27, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;January 26, 2004.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong><em>&quot;</em>The O'Reilly Factor&quot; is preempted on January 27&nbsp;due to special coverage of the New Hampshire Primary. Our coverage begins at 8 p.m. ET! &quot;The Factor&quot; returns on&nbsp;Wednesday, January 28 at 8 p.m. ET and 11 p.m. ET!</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;Now for the top story tonight, the Republican reaction to this.&nbsp; Joining us from Washington is Clifford May, president of the Foundation for the Defense of Democracies.</p>
<p>You know, Mr. May, we wanted Ed Gillespie on tonight.&nbsp; And I don't really want any part of this.&nbsp; A little running for cover in the GOP, but you're a standup guy.&nbsp; How do you see it?</p>
<p><strong>CLIFF MAY, FOUNDATION FOR DEFENSE OF DEMOCRACIES</strong>:&nbsp; Well, I'm speaking for myself, not for the Republican party.&nbsp; Actually, I agree with about 98&nbsp; percent of what you said.&nbsp; The problem here is that we had an intelligence failure.&nbsp; And our intelligence capabilities have not been what they should be, going back at least to end of the&nbsp;<b>Cold War</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Cold%20War">search</a>)&nbsp;when a lot of people, not least in Congress, but also in every administration said ah, the Communists are gone.&nbsp; What do we need intelligence capabilities for?&nbsp; Guess what?&nbsp; We did.</p>
<p>And in some of the cases, our intelligence capabilities have been&nbsp; weakened, even going back all the way to Watergate.&nbsp; You remember things like the Church Committee, which tied the hands of intelligence.&nbsp; We do need an overhaul.&nbsp; We do need much better intelligence.</p>
<p>That said, Bill, I would only add this.&nbsp; The war against&nbsp;<b>Saddam Hussein</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Saddam%20Hussein">search</a>), I think, was both just, no question about that and necessary.&nbsp; This guy was a rogue dictator.&nbsp; Terrorists were going through Iraq all the time,&nbsp;as <b>David Kay</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=David%20Kay">search</a>)&nbsp;said and as you mentioned.&nbsp; And there were weapons there that -- for the taking.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK, but here is the problem.&nbsp; We live in a republic where the people are supposed to decide crucial issues, all right?&nbsp; And the people can't decide the issue if they're given erroneous information.&nbsp; And the Bush administration gave us erroneous information, not because they lied, but&nbsp; because they got erroneous information from the Central Intelligence Agency.&nbsp; And I believe President Bush hasn't been nearly aggressive enough in holding those people in the agency accountable.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I think we need to totally refurbish the CIA.&nbsp; The question is whether George Tenet, who served Clinton, served Bush, is the best man to do it.&nbsp;&nbsp; And if somebody thinks not in the Democratic party, they should say what they're going to do the about it.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, what about Bush, though?&nbsp; Why isn't he doing it now? He knows the 9-11 situation better than anyone on the planet.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; His...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And he knows this is a screw-up.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; His marching orders to&nbsp;<b>George Tenet</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=George%20Tenet">search</a>)&nbsp;ought to be, we need to do --&nbsp; overhaul the CIA so they can do the kind of intelligence gathering we need.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right, come on...</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; But understand this.&nbsp; It's very...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Mr. May...</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; ...the most important thing to understand, it seems to me is this.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Saddam Hussein was himself a weapon of mass destruction.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, that's baloney.&nbsp; That is right-wing spin.&nbsp; And a guy as smart as you, I don't want to hear you say that.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; No, no, no, no.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I don't want to hear you say that right-wing talking point business.&nbsp; Look, let's get back to the issue here.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; It's not the weapons of...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, no, no, no.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; ...mass destruction.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; The Americans can decide for themselves whether that policy was good for America or not.&nbsp; But we got hurt overseas.&nbsp; We still hurt overseas.&nbsp; This Colin Powell went to the U.N., put this big WMD scenario in play.&nbsp; And it turned out not to be true.&nbsp; Our image overseas is hurt.&nbsp; OK?</p>
<p>Here, half the population is with you and President Bush.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; Bill?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And half isn't.&nbsp; Now I think I want to see a little leadership here on the part of the president.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I understand.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I want to see him get out there and engage this issue.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I understand, but Bill, knowing what you know now, would you change your mind?&nbsp; Would you think, ah, I wish Saddam Hussein would had&nbsp; been left in power?&nbsp; We had no business going there?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, I wouldn't.&nbsp; Not me.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; Absolutely.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But I want the American people to have accurate information.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I agree.&nbsp; I agree with you entirely, but you know what?&nbsp; There is&nbsp; nobody who is in favor of&nbsp; this war against Saddam Hussein, who has changed their mind.&nbsp; The only people who are angry about this really are the people who weren't in favor of it before and they're not in favor of it now.&nbsp; Saddam...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But that's not the question here.&nbsp; Look...</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; The question is...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ...the question is going to be decided in nine months in the ballot box.&nbsp; And the American people can decide whether President Bush is looking out for them or not.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; Bill?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But here's the fundamental constitutional question.&nbsp; All right? You have an administration, which is closed.&nbsp; The Bush administration is not&nbsp; open with the folks.&nbsp; Everybody knows that.&nbsp; All right?</p>
<p>Now you have, and I believe this report, and I've got to say, I will say this in President Bush's defense, President Bush could have booted this.&nbsp; He could have said to Kay, hey, find something and this and that.&nbsp; He wanted the truth.&nbsp; He told Kay, even if it's black and bad and going to hurt me, you get it.&nbsp; I think we all -- that reflects very, very well on the president, but he's got to now step up.&nbsp; And&nbsp; he's got to admit the mistake.&nbsp; And he's got to take strong action to protect us, Mr. May.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I don't disagree with you.&nbsp; But understand this.&nbsp; Saddam Hussein did have weapons of mass destruction.&nbsp; And he was further along than nuclear&nbsp; development.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He had Ricin.&nbsp; That's what he had.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; He had Ricin.&nbsp; He had -- but he wanted to develop it.&nbsp; There's no question that his scientists stole money from him and lied to him.&nbsp; And we got the&nbsp; same -- we have the information he had about that.</p>
<p>But since 1998 under President Clinton, the&nbsp;<b>Iraq Liberation Act</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Iraq%20Liberation%20Act">search</a>)&nbsp;has been official U.S. policy of both parties.&nbsp; All President Bush did was to implement that policy of Iraqi liberation.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; It's be very interesting to see...</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; You know the good thing?&nbsp; You agree and I agree that it was a good thing.&nbsp; We do need, in an age of terrorism, better intelligence than we've had.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; But we also need openness on the part of the president.&nbsp; And he can't be sitting up in the White House not saying anything about it.&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; But let me -- but understand this.&nbsp; The last thing the president wants&nbsp; to do is say to the CIA, say to those operatives who were brought, you haven't&nbsp; been doing a really good job.&nbsp; He wants to quietly say we have got to get -- you and I can say the truth here with no spin.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I think the time for quietly saying it, Mr. May, that's way, way in the past.&nbsp; After 9/11 and this, no more quiet.&nbsp; I'd start to bang a couple of desks here.&nbsp; All right, we got go.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I -- no spin means that you and I say that the CIA needs to totally&nbsp; refurbish itself for the new era.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; I don't expect the president to bash them on public TV.&nbsp;</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Mr. May, thank you.&nbsp; We appreciate it very much.</p>
<p>MAY:&nbsp; Good to be with you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And a brand new poll question on billoreilly.com is should President Bush admit his administration made a mistake regarding WMDs?&nbsp; Should President Bush admit his administration made a mistake regarding WMDs?&nbsp; Billoreilly.com poll.&nbsp; Please go on in.&nbsp; We'd like to hear from you.</p>
