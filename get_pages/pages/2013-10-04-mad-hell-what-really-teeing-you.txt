			<h1 itemprop="headline">Mad as Hell: What is really teeing you off?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2013-10-04T00:00-04:00">Published October 04, 2013</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/2717754626001/mad-as-hell-what-is-really-teeing-off" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/100313_reilly_hell1_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; October 3, 2013. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: &quot;Mad as Hell Segment&quot; tonight. What's really teeing you off? This evening we've got a brand new segment where you sound off about things that are driving you crazy. We owe the segment to Patty Tsaiatsky (ph) and the movie &quot;The Network&quot;.</p>
<p>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>UNIDENTIFIED MALE: I want you to get up right now and go to the window, open it and stick your head out and yell: I'm as mad as hell and I'm not going to take this anymore.</p>
<div></div>
<p>(END VIDEO CLIP)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: That's exactly how I look with Barney Frank.</p>
<p>Here now to help us with mad, very mellow woman Juliet Huddy, I wanted that contrast.</p>
<p>JULIE HUDDY, FOX NEWS CORRESPONDENT: No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So first off Huddy is Lori Martin who lives in Illinois. She's mad as hell about Obamacare chaos. Quote, &quot;Blue Cross, Blue Shield of Illinois informed me that my policy no longer available because of Obamacare. I've had this policy for years and I'm happy with it.&quot;</p>
<p>So what's -- what's the deal on Blue Cross Blue Shield of Illinois?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Well it's not just Blue Cross Blue Shield, it's a lot of different policies. You have to meet a certain criteria. It's like a 10 point list of criteria to meet the current standards for Obamacare. And hers -- Lori's obviously didn't meet it. A lot of people aren't going to continue that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So your insurance policy has to say, what? Give me an example.</p>
<p>HUDDY: For instance, let me give you an example. So you have to have maternity and newborn care. That has to be included in your policy. You could be --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well what if you are 75 years old?</p>
<p>HUDDY: It doesn't matter.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: It doesn't matter.</p>
<p>HUDDY: If doesn't matter if you are 75 years old. You have to have pediatric care in your policy as well.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Ok so if Lori doesn't have that then Blue Cross can't ensure.</p>
<p>HUDDY: It's not a la carte you have to hit the 10 point.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right and there's a lot of insurance agencies like Blue Cross, Blue Shield who doesn't want any part of this so they are dumping everybody.</p>
<p>All right second e-mail comes from Dave Reynolds who lives in Las Vegas. &quot;I'm mad because the White House has approved $300 million to bailout Detroit. Aren't we broke? Is it all about a strong Democratic voting base?&quot; So first of all who allocated the $300 million to Detroit?</p>
<p>The Obama administration. They say it's not a bailout, but then their critics say this is kind of a back way bailout. Essentially this is money that Detroit would have been eligible for anyway.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: In what sense? Money for what?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Money for different things. Many cities are eligible for this. It just happened to be that Detroit was going through red tape.</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Part of the stimulus program or something like that for public works?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Correct. Yes. This money is allocated specifically though.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So Detroit was eligible for the money and they get the 300 million. But it is a Democratic controlled city that is bankrupt.</p>
<p>HUDDY: Bankrupt because of mismanagement.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Are we confident the 300 million is going to be mismanaged like every other cent was?</p>
<p>HUDDY: That's the problem. That's why this person is upset about this.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And I'm upset too. Although I like the Tigers and the Pistons.</p>
<p>Third selection this evening comes from Diane Balbo (ph) who lives in California, is a Catholic.</p>
<p>&quot;I'm mad as hell at you O'Reilly, for claiming the Holy Spirit inspired to you write the books. Why are you placing yourself above all of us?&quot; Certainly not doing that I think you understand this. Right?</p>
<p>HUDDY: You would never do that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Are you Catholic?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: You understand what I'm talking about, do you not?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Well, first of all, believe it or not. I do actually understand what you are saying here, Bill. You were I object -- inspired. You are not saying God came down off a fleeting staircase and grabbed your hand and brought you --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: He has before but not in this one.</p>
<p>HUDDY: But you can't joke around sometimes with certain people when it comes --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes I can. I can joke around as much as I want.</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: God has a sense of humor or else I would not be sitting here. Okay? So that's a fact. But, Christian doctrine says that there is a God and that God interacts with every human being. Every human being.</p>
<p>HUDDY: And you just had a personal interaction --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: When something good happens to you, you are inspired to do something that turns out positive, that comes from God.</p>
<p>HUDDY: I'm okay with that. I'm okay with that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And I'm glad you are.</p>
<p>Okay. Here is finally the last one. Sharon Eubanks from Redding, California, a nice town.</p>
<p>&quot;What makes me mad are people who take their dogs with them everywhere they go. I see them in shopping carts, restaurants, everywhere. Dogs have taken over.&quot;</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And they have!</p>
<p>HUDDY: Dogs are people too.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No they are not. Comfort dogs, right?</p>
<p>HUDDY: People say they are comfort dogs and they say they get --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Little fluffy dogs.</p>
<p>HUDDY; I don't understand what the problem is if you have little Fifi, your Chihuahua, in a little bag and you're sitting there and you are having -- not drooling or barking.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But if you're going to the deli and taking a chunk out of your ankle come on.</p>
<p>HUDDY: Well, that's a problem.</p>
<p>(CROSSTALK)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Sanitary thing. I don't want to be in a restaurant with the little cocker spaniel at the table next to me.</p>
<p>HUDDY: Guess what, you can go to the restaurant next door that does not allow cocker spaniels.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: I think there is a board of health situation here. I like dogs. I have a dog. You have a dog?</p>
<p>HUDDY: Yes, Gomez.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Like dogs. Don't take the dog everywhere.</p>
<p>HUDDY: I totally disagree with you. You can take them anywhere you want.</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Can we replace her on this segment? What was that lady's name? I'm with you. What was her name?</p>
<p>HUDDY: I don't know.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Sharon Eubanks. I'm mad like you are. Keep the dogs reasonable. You don't have to go everywhere with the dog.</p>
