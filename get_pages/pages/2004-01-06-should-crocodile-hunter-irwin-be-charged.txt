			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Should Crocodile Hunter Irwin Be Charged?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-01-06T10:26-05:00">Published January 06, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;January 5, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the <em>Back of the Book</em> segment tonight, crocodile hunter&nbsp;<b>Steve Irwin</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Steve%20Irwin">search</a>)&nbsp;facing trouble in Australia.&nbsp; Last Friday, he dangled his 1-month-old baby near a crocodile.&nbsp; You might have seen these pictures.&nbsp; They've been all over the place.&nbsp; And that set off a wave of angst, although Australian authorities say Irwin will not be charged.</p>
<p>With us now is Phillip Coorey, the New York bureau chief for&nbsp;<b>News Limited Australia</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=News%20Limited%20Australia">search</a>).&nbsp; This is still a big story down under, huh?</p>
<p><strong>PHILLIP COOREY, NEWS LIMITED OF AUSTRALIA</strong>:&nbsp; Yes, very big, Bill.&nbsp; Probably about as big as it is here.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; People -- are they angry?&nbsp; Like when&nbsp;<b>Michael Jackson</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Michael%20Jackson">search</a>)&nbsp;dangled his kid over there, I mean everybody said, hey, this is a nut, Jackson should be -- they were calling for his -- child abuse and all of this.&nbsp; Is it same as Irwin?&nbsp; Are people against him?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes, the -- it's similar to -- with Jackson.&nbsp; I mean Jack already had a bit of a record, so it wasn't...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, Irwin isn't considered a weirdo.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; No, he's not, but...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Jackson was.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; But it's the same anger.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But it's the same thing.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; The anger is similar, yes, at the moment.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; So most Australians are saying Irwin did a bad thing here?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But Irwin himself's very defensive about it.&nbsp; He was on the <em>Today</em> show with Matt Lauer, I believe, and let's role the clip.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p>MATT LAUER, <em>TODAY</em>:&nbsp; Steve, let me...</p>
<p>STEVE IRWIN, CROCODILE HUNTER:&nbsp; I would be considered the worst dad in the world if I didn't teach my children about the dangers that are in their back yard.&nbsp; Mate, we live in the middle of Australia zoo.</p>
<p>I am sorry, Matt.</p>
<p>LAUER:&nbsp; OK, but let me jump in here.</p>
<p>IRWIN:&nbsp; You know what.&nbsp; I will never let this happen again.</p>
<p>LAUER:&nbsp; Let me get a word in edgewise.&nbsp; It's...</p>
<p>IRWIN:&nbsp; My life is under the microscope, and that is very unfortunate, Matt, and I can't change it.&nbsp; It's happened.&nbsp; I'll deal with things a heck of a lot differently.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Well, that's crazy.&nbsp; I mean the little girl -- what is the little girl, 5 years old or something like that?&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; The baby's&nbsp;one-month-old.&nbsp; You don't teach a baby about the dangers of anything at&nbsp;one-month-old.&nbsp; This was a stunt, was it not?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Oh, absolutely.&nbsp; A lot of things Steve does,&nbsp;are a stunt.&nbsp; So...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But you don't use a 1-year-old baby for a stunt.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Well, I mean...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Come on.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; But look at the guy.&nbsp; His whole life is a stunt.&nbsp; I mean that's how he became famous.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I don't care about him doing whatever he does with crocodiles or what else.&nbsp; But you don't use a baby -- he's exploiting this baby.&nbsp; Look at this.&nbsp; He's exploiting this baby near a deadly animal for entertainment purposes.</p>
<p>I mean I don't -- I would have charged him.&nbsp; If I were in Australia, I would have charged this guy with -- I don't know what.&nbsp; Endangering a minor or something like that?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; That's not going to happen.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Why?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; I guess we have maybe a slightly different view of it.&nbsp; I mean the guy's obviously paying the price and may pay a future price.&nbsp; I mean...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What is that price?&nbsp; Bad publicity?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Oh, yes.&nbsp;... You know, lots of commercial interests.&nbsp; I mean there's -- I mean, ultimately, his fan will decide whether -- you know, how badly this affects him.</p>
<p>But there's a few things that are happening at home.&nbsp; He's a candidate at the moment for Australian of the year, which is an award we give to a citizen every January, for -- it's like our national...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, forget about that award.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Well, he's a...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's gone.&nbsp; He's...</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Well, he was a -- he was the hot...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That would be like Michael Jackson winning Californian of the year.&nbsp; It ain't going happen.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Exactly.&nbsp; Well, I don't think so.&nbsp; No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So no&nbsp;<b>Australian of the Year</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Australian%20of%20the%20Year">search</a>)&nbsp;for this guy.&nbsp; But he's not going get criminally charged.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; He's not going to pay...</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; He's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I think he'll pay a promotional price...</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... maybe lose some endorsements or whatever, but you -- come on, Phillip, you've got to admit this is stupid.&nbsp; Isn't this just stupid?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Oh, absolutely.&nbsp; And I mean he's what we'd call a drongo, Bill.&nbsp; I mean it -- you know, it's just a stupid thing to do.&nbsp; I think it's sinking in slowly that -- to him.</p>
<p>I mean Steve Irwin is probably the last guy who thought it was silly.&nbsp; You know the rest of the world thought it was.&nbsp; He -- he saw it a different way.&nbsp; But I think now it's sinking in he's done something.&nbsp; He's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; I mean to say, look, I want to teach my children about the dangers -- this kid is 1-month-old, all right?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; He doesn't know what he's doing.&nbsp; What if he slipped or anything happened?&nbsp; Oh, it just -- it just boggles the mind.&nbsp; With babies, you don't do this kind of thing.</p>
<p>Now how big is this guy Irwin in Australia?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Well, he...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Is he like Hogan or the Hulk?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; No.&nbsp; Well, no, Hogan -- Hogan -- I guess he's like a real life Hogan.&nbsp; Hogan was his own...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, he's a Hollywood...</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; ... enigma, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Irwin was semi-big.&nbsp; Most people thought he was a bit nuts really, to be honest.&nbsp; Anyone wrestling with crocodiles.&nbsp; He became famous in America -- more famous in America, and, as a result of that, he then became famous in Australia.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Is that right?&nbsp; So it boomeranged, pardon the pun...</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; No, it's true.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... back down under when he became a bigger star here.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Australians are very sensitive about the way they're perceived overseas, and, often, we wait for imprimatur of sort of the Americans or the British.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Is this going to hurt his career in Australia?</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; I'd say so.&nbsp; But I don't think it's got the commercial implications as it will in America.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; It will hurt his career here.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; For how long?&nbsp; If he apologized and said it was bad judgment, I'm sorry, now that I look at the tape, then Americans, I think, would forgive him.&nbsp; But if he doesn't and keeps a defiant attitude he showed on the <em>Today</em> show, that's going to hurt him here.</p>
<p>COOREY:&nbsp; Yes, I think the same goes at home as well.&nbsp; A little bit of contrition would be a good idea.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Mr. Coorey, thanks very much.&nbsp; We appreciate you coming on in.</p>
