			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Newt Gingrich on President Bush's News Conference</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-04-15T13:19-04:00">Published April 15, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;April 14, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp;Now for the top story tonight.&nbsp; A couple of other views on the press conference.&nbsp; With us, Fox News analyst Newt <b>GINGRICH</b>.</p>
<p>All right, where am I going wrong here?</p>
<p><strong>NEWT GINGRICH, FOX NEWS POLITICAL ANALYST</strong>:&nbsp; I don't think you're very much wrong at all.&nbsp; I saw the press conference about the same way you did.</p>
<p>I think the president's challenge is to drive a system in Iraq where you get the Iraqis a lot more involved in their own government and where the Iraqis have a lot more at stake.&nbsp; I think General Abizaid probably understands this as well as anybody in the world.&nbsp; And the fact that he's now their full time and has really taken charge in Baghdad of the military side is a very good step in the right direction.</p>
<p>He speaks Arabic.&nbsp; He is of a Lebanese Christian background.&nbsp; He is a tremendous specialist in the region.&nbsp; And I think what you'll see in the next few weeks is a lot better American ties with the Sunnis and the Shias, and the Kurds and a lot more political involvement of the Iraqis in their own self government.&nbsp; And I think at that point, you're going to see the killers being isolated pretty dramatically.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, now that's an optimistic view, you have to say.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; I'm an optimist.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, but you know, you were critical of the -- they called it an uprising, which was very unfair I thought.&nbsp; Did you think the word uprising on the front page was unfair?&nbsp; Because I didn't see it as an uprising.&nbsp; An uprising is when the popular folks rise.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Yes, they don't -- they got two factions.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; The Sunni faction in Fallujah and this one extremist&nbsp; leader of the...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Shi'ites.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; ...of about 3,000 or 4,000 Shia out of about 15 million.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But the American press portrayed it as an uprising across the country.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Sure.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; The people trying to throw us out, which was very misleading.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; That's totally false.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; But you said in a paper I read, and maybe you were misquoted, that you know, look, Bush is at risk here if he doesn't get the military situation under control.&nbsp; You still feel that way?</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Well, he is -- of course he is a risk.&nbsp; This country is&nbsp; not going to watch five more months of Fallujah and of the kind of ambushes and the kind of killings of hostages, and say gee, let's just keep it up.&nbsp; The president has to have a strategy, which I think they're moving towards now, of very aggressively putting the Iraqis in charge of their own future, having us as the reinforcers, not the enforcers.&nbsp; You and I talked about this a year ago.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, but the Iraqi army ran a way, a quarter of them in the fight for Fallujah.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; But...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And the police -- yes?</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Look, I'm not defending the Iraqi performance here.&nbsp; But let me draw a distinction.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; In Korea in 1950, when we were short of manpower, we invented what was called the &quot;KATUSA&quot; program, Koreans Attached to the U.S. ARMY.&nbsp; As many as 20 percent of the troops were Korean in some of our units.</p>
<p>Iraqis who are working closely with Americans, if you go to northern Iraq right now, you'll see Iraqis working closely with Americans.&nbsp; When you put an Iraqi in the same humvee, you suddenly have an Iraqi family saying boy, we better not have a bomb along that road there.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, so you say -- they almost have to integrate.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; You've got to integrate them...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; ...for the transition until they learn.&nbsp; Remember, these are people brutalized by 25 years of a truly vicious, murdering torturing dictatorship.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, I understand.&nbsp; It's understandable.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But Bush doesn't have the luxury of time here.&nbsp; He doesn't have the luxury of time.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; They need to do it immediately.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right, he's got to get...</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; And I think Abizaid will.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; The president is not big on details.&nbsp; Last night, he was criticized for when a question was well when are you going to hand the government over to?&nbsp; And he goes, &quot;you know, well we'll know soon.&quot;&nbsp; And everybody goes, &quot;oh, you should know now.&quot;</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You know?&nbsp; Legitimate?</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; No, I don't think so.&nbsp; But they're in the process of working that out.&nbsp; They're going to work that out.&nbsp; June 30 -- look, if June 30 is the deadline, how come all of a sudden for the White&nbsp; House press corps April becomes the deadline?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, I didn't -- you didn't defend me...</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; No, I think the right answer the president say -- the press says you're the press, I'm the president.&nbsp; You'll know soon.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But I would have said hey, I'm evaluating a lot of different people.&nbsp; Now we'll make a decision or -- in conjunction with the Iraqis.&nbsp; You always have to say that to be politically correct.&nbsp; But we don't have the strategy nailed down.</p>
<p>The other question was Clarke apologized to the families.&nbsp; Do you want to apologize?&nbsp; Now loaded question.&nbsp; You're shaking your head here.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; But he should have been ready to just knock it out of the park.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; And well, are you telling me how you would have knocked it out?&nbsp; And I tell you how I would have.&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Well, I would have said that the people who owe an apology are the murderers who came to America, who plotted to kill Americans.&nbsp; And the duty of the rest of us is to get revenge for our loved ones, and to make sure that these people aren't around to ever do it again.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You'd use the word revenge?</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; You bet you.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I would have said that -- but not before I said this.&nbsp; I would have said I wish I had done more.&nbsp; I wish I had done more in hindsight.&nbsp; Just a little bit of humility, you see?</p>
<p>But -- and then I would have given them your answer.&nbsp; You see what I'm talking about?&nbsp; Because the perception the Bush administration, among many Americans, is they're arrogant.&nbsp; They&nbsp; never apologize for anything.&nbsp; They never admit any mistakes.</p>
<p>And you're right, he knew that was coming.&nbsp; If he had just softened it a little bit.&nbsp; See, a teeny, little...</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; You may be right, but let me tell you why I'm probably not very much in the softening phase today.&nbsp; When you look at the pictures I just saw of the hostages, and you hear reports of&nbsp; them being killed and really brutal, vicious ways...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; ...I don't think this is a time for us to be complicated.&nbsp; I think this is a time for us to say to the planet there's civilization and there's barbarism.&nbsp; There's nothing in between.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And we're going to wipe out barbarism?</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; And we have to wipe out barbarism, because if we don't, it will destroy civilization.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I think that's true.&nbsp; I think that that's got to be the theme.&nbsp; They're not going to give up.&nbsp; They're not going to stop.</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; That's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And if we stop, we're going to get it.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>GINGRICH</b>:&nbsp; Then our whole way of -- and let me say, by the way, in talk -- I used to work with a big picture guy named&nbsp;<b>Ronald Reagan</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Ronald%20Reagan">search</a>).&nbsp; And Ronald Reagan may not have known a lot of details,&nbsp; but he knew how to take out the Soviet Union so it didn't exist anymore.&nbsp; And if George W. Bush manages to hold together a team, and they manage to eliminate this threat to us over the next few years, that's a pretty enough big picture victory for me.&nbsp; And...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Newt Gingrich, thanks very much.&nbsp; We appreciate it as always.&nbsp; Good to see you in New York.</p>
