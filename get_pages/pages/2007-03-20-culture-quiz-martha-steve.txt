			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Culture Quiz: Martha & Steve</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2007-03-20T09:59-04:00">Published March 20, 2007</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> March 19, 2007, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment. Tonight, the second installment of &quot;The Great American Culture Quiz&quot;, where we ask you five questions to see how much you know about American pop culture.</p>
<p>Here to help us out, Steve Doocy, who got all of the questions right last week, which was a disgrace, and Martha MacCallum, who got four out of five. So today we've toughened them up.</p>
<p>Now, these are multiple choice. I'm going to read you the question, give you the, you know, choices. And then you don't have a lot of time. This isn't one of those dopey TV shows on, you know, NBC.</p>
<p><strong>MARTHA MACCALLUM, FOX NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> Can we call someone?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No crying, either.</p>
<p><strong>STEVE DOOCY, CO-HOST, &quot;FOX &amp; FRIENDS&quot;:</strong> You're no Howie Mandell.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That's for sure. On which network did <b>Bill Clinton</b> answer the infamous boxers or briefs question? A, <b>NBC</b>; B, <b>Comedy Central</b>; C, <b>MTV</b>; D, <b>VH1</b>.</p>
<p>All right. What's your answer, Martha?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> C.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> What's your answer, Doocy?</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> C.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Is that right? Was that right?</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> It is right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Wrong.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> What?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Was it? Did I get it wrong? So you both got them right. C, MTV.</p>
<p>President Bush the elder launched <b>Operation Restore Hope</b>. What country did it take place in? A, Bosnia; B, Rwanda; C, Somalia; D, Kuwait.</p>
<p>Operation Restore Hope. Doocy.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> Can I see them again?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No, this isn't fifth grade, Doocy. Do you know the answer or not?</p>
<p>I knew you wouldn't get this. You know nothing about — D, A, both wrong. Kuwait, Doocy? A, Bosnia?</p>
<p>Somalia. That was &quot;<b>Black Hawk Down</b>&quot;.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> That was my next choice.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That was Black Hawk Down. I bet you all you guys at home got that.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> That's a good question.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> &quot;Black Hawk Down&quot;. Here's a better question. <b>The Blob</b> terrorized a small American town and only one guy figured it out. Not Brian Kilmeade. What actor played the guy? Warren Beatty; James Garner; C, James Dean; D, Steve McQueen?</p>
<p>Who saved the world from The Blob? MacCallum has Steve McQueen.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Love Steve McQueen.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Who do you have? C. You believe — you're telling me, Doocy, that you believe James Dean was in &quot;The Blob&quot;? Is that what you're telling me?</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> I thought it was Jimmy Dean. Sorry.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> James Dean, &quot;East of Eden&quot;, the esteemed actor. You're telling me — MacCallum is right. MacCallum is ahead. Steve McQueen, first movie was &quot;The Blob&quot;.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> He was great in &quot;The Blob&quot;.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Now if anybody knows this, I'm impressed. Who wrote the theme song to &quot;The Blob&quot;?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> No clue.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Burt Bacharach. And it was sung by? The Five Blobs. Am I only one who knows &quot;The Blob&quot; here?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> You're the only one who knows the Five Blobs.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Burt Bacharach wrote the theme song for &quot;The Blob&quot;. I'll sing it next week.</p>
<p>No. 4, <b>Bill Cosby</b> co-starred in a hit program called &quot;I Spy&quot;. Who was the other lead? A, Sidney Poitier; B, Jack Lard; C, Robert Culp; D, Robert Vaughn.</p>
<p>Who was the other lead with Bill Cosby? Doocy, C. C. MacCallum, D. MacCallum, you're doubting your wisdom or what?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I think it's D.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Do you know Robert Vaughn?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Do you know who Robert Vaughn is?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> &quot;The Man from U.N.C.L.E.&quot;</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> &quot;The Man from U.N.C.L.E.&quot;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> How could he be &quot;I Spy&quot; and — how could he be in both shows at the same time?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> U.N.C.L.E.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> How could he? Doocy got it right. What a disgrace. Robert Culp.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> <b>Lucille Ball</b>, did she do anything.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You have to — you have to deduce if you don't know. If he's in &quot;The Man from U.N.C.L.E.&quot;, he can't be in &quot;I Spy&quot;. He can't be in both.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> It's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right. Final question, and this is one of my favorites. Ann Margaret became a star in the movie &quot;Bye Bye Birdie&quot;. Which actor played her father and then went on to game show fame? Richard Dawson; Wink Martindale; Monty Hall; Paul Lynde.</p>
<p>Her father in &quot;Bye Bye Birdie&quot;.</p>
<p>Doocy, D. Doocy, Doocy, Doocy. D, Paul Lynde. D, correct. You're both correct. Paul Lynde. You didn't know. You just guessed. Now you weren't cribbing off him.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I know &quot;Bye Bye Birdie&quot;.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You weren't cribbing off him?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> No.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Paul Lynde was the dad. And what was his famous line? No idea.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Kids today. What are you going to do with kids today?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Very good. Martha MacCallum, what's the matter with kids today? Why can't they be like I am?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Yes. Perfect in every way.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> She's so much better than you, Doocy. She's so much better.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> And who was that movie about?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I just want everybody to know, because we're going to come back next week with these two, that Doocy thought James Dean was in &quot;The Blob&quot;. All right. You got that wrong.</p>
<p>MacCallum, you win tonight.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Awesome.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> It was fixed!</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> She just blew it with using the word awesome. Now I really think I'm on American Bandstand.</p>
