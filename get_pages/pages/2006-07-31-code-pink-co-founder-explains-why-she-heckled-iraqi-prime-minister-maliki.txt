			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Code Pink Co-Founder Explains Why She Heckled Iraqi Prime Minister Maliki</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2006-07-31T13:05-04:00">Published July 31, 2006</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> July 28, 2006, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>LAURA INGRAHAM, GUEST HOST:</strong> In the Personal Story segment tonight, twice this week, members of the anti-war group <b>Code Pink</b> have interrupted events on Capitol Hill by heckling politicians. There was this at Ambassador John Bolton's confirmation hearing:</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> And I strongly oppose the nomination of this man, John Bolton, as U.N. ambassador. He is a discredit to the United States.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> And this during the Iraqi prime minister's speech to Congress.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> Bring them all down.</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> The chair notes the disturbance in the gallery. The sergeant in arms will secure order by removing those engaging in disruption.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> And with us now is one of the culprits. Medea Benjamin is the co-founder of Code Pink, who was charged with disruption of Congress. You don't have any leg irons on you or handcuffs, or anything? They sprung you.</p>
<p><strong>MEDEA BENJAMIN, CO-FOUNDER OF CODE PINK:</strong> That's right. And I must say you look very pretty in pink, Laura.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Well, I would like to say that I was wearing it for you, but I just — it was only the thing in my closet. OK, here's my question to you. How does heckling help the cause of human rights? I would assume you would agree that you're a human rights activist, right?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Yes. I don't like the word &quot;heckling&quot;. I would say...</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> ...that's what you were doing.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Speaking — well, that's the world you use, but we saying that we're speaking up for the people who weren't being heard.</p>
<p>We tried through many means to meet with the prime minister. We called his office before he came. We.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Are you kidding me?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Oh, no, I'm serious.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> You actually thought you were going to meet with the prime minister of Iraq?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> We came very close. We spoke to the ambassador three times. And he said he got us a meeting. And then they never showed up for it. So we didn't have a chance. He spoke to George Bush. He spoke to Congress.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Why should he meet with Code Pink?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Just because we represent now, the majority voice in the United States.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> How many - oh, so Code Pink speaks to the majority of Americans?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Did you see the latest poll of the The New York Times with CBS News?</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Do you think that the majority of Americans see what you guys did this week on Capitol Hill, and are standing up going, yes, be rude to the Iraqi prime minister. Yes, be really rude and act like you're about 12 years old when the U.N. ambassador, who's trying to represent our country's interest is up on Capitol Hill testifying? You think people really think you guys are cool for doing that?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Well, I tell you, we get hundreds and hundreds of emails.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Hundreds?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> ...and calls saying thank you so much for standing up to us.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> No.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> But Laura, let me just say one thing. The Iraqi prime minister was not representing the majority view of Iraqis.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Oh, so not only do you represent the majority view of Americans, you also actually represent the majority view of Iraqis? That's pretty cool.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Well, 87 percent of Iraqis want a timetable for withdrawals.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Yes, yes. Well, I've seen those polls.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> And the majority of Americans want it.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> I have a couple questions for you.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> OK.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Number one, are you a human rights activist?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Yes.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> What do you think would happen to you if you were trying to organize the group that you have now, let's say in Saddam's Iraq or in Iran today? What do you think would happen to you?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> You'd probably be in jail if I was lucky and maybe dead if I wasn't.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> So then you would agree, would you not, that the current situation in Iraq, as difficult as it is, is much better for the cause that you say that you represent, which is human rights?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> I fought against <b>Saddam Hussein</b> when <b>Donald Rumsfeld</b> was there shaking hands and saying this is a guy we can work with. I'm against all dictatorships. I'm against all human rights violations.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Now that's a <b>John Lennon</b> song. That's not a plan. What's the plan to keep America safe today?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> The plan to keep America safe, well let's start with Iraq. In Iraq, there <em>is</em> a reconciliation plan that the Iraqis have come up with. And we are saying let's support the Iraqi, Sunni, Shia, Kurds.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Right.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> ...who are trying to bring reconciliation.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> And that's what prime minister Maliki said that he was actually pursuing, but you think he's not really doing that.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> He <em>tried</em> to pursue it. In fact, he was about to announce it, but it included one thing, Laura, that George Bush forced him to take out. And that was a timetable for withdrawal of U.S. troops. They president of Iraq, vice president of Iraq...</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> You think that's a good message for the terrorists today?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> ...they all said we want a timetable. Well, I think the only way out of Iraq is that people sit down and start negotiating.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Yes, this is my question to you. If you live in a — let's say you live in a dangerous neighborhood, and you had an alarm system on your house, would you ever advertise a decision that you were making to do away with your alarm company's monitoring of your house? Wouldn't that be signaling to criminals, come in, take what you want? Would you do that?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> And if you were about to go off the cliff, which is what George Bush is doing to us in Iraq...</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> You see my point.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> ...in this incredible quagmire, wouldn't you stop and say hey, let's sit down. We should never have invaded Iraq.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> OK.</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> They do not threaten us. We should not be there. And the Iraqis don't want us to be there. They have to come up with a solution to their problems</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> Who's responsible today for women in Afghanistan being allowed to vote? People being allowed to freely choose among lots of different candidates for the Iraqi parliament? Who's responsible for that?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Well, I think in the case of Afghanistan, it's positive that women have the right to vote, but there is still a...</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> You don't want to give George Bush credit for anything, do you? You don't want to give him credit for Afghanistan, having the vision, or Iraq?</p>
<p><b>BENJAMIN:</b> Are we supposed to go around the world freeing people? Or are we supposed to find who attacked us on 9/11? We let <b>Usama bin Laden</b> go. And instead, we invaded the wrong country, Iraq. And we should get out of there.</p>
<p><b>INGRAHAM:</b> I think you should try to give those talking points on a military base in Iraq and see how far you get. But we appreciate you — [you're a] stand up [person] to come in and talk to us. I'm glad you're out of jail.</p>
