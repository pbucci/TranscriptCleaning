			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Talking Points</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">The Vatican and Kofi Annan Weigh In On Saddam</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2003-12-17T12:21-05:00">Published December 17, 2003</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

			 <p>Hi, I'm Bill O'Reilly.&nbsp; Thank you for watching us tonight.</p>
<p>The Vatican and [U.N. Secretary General] Kofi Annan weigh in on the capture of Saddam.&nbsp; That is the subject of this evening's <em>Talking Points Memo</em>.</p>
<p>Mr. Annan is a one-trick pony, a talker not a fighter.&nbsp; He loves to inject theory into life and death situations, which is naive at best, dangerous at worst.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Now the U.N. Secretary-General is demanding that Saddam not be executed.&nbsp; With all due respect to Secretary Annan, it's not his call.&nbsp;</p>
<p>He allowed Saddam to impose the death penalty on hundreds of thousands of people, so Annan has zero moral authority.&nbsp; If this man had backed up tough action against Saddam five years ago, thousands of human beings would be alive today.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Not quite as offensive, but still outrageous are the remarks of the&nbsp;<b>Cardinal Renato Martino</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Cardinal%20Renato%20Martino">search</a>), the head of the Vatican's Justice and Peace Department.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The cardinal said,&nbsp;&quot;I felt pity to see this man Saddam destroyed, the military looking at his teeth as if he were a beast.&nbsp; They could have spared us these pictures.&nbsp; Seeing him like this, a man in his tragedy, despite all the heavy blame he bears, I had a sense of compassion for him.&quot;</p>
<p>Now, let's break down the cardinal's statement.&nbsp;</p>
<p>One, the U.S. military doctors looking in Saddam's mouth to see if poison was secreted in a tooth, a common form of suicide for war criminals.&nbsp; Get a clue, Cardinal.</p>
<p>Two, showing pictures of a humiliated butcher may prevent other butchers from doing evil.&nbsp; Get it, Cardinal?</p>
<p>Three, you can afford to feel compassion for Saddam, Cardinal, because the USA protects the Vatican from the forces of evil.&nbsp; America has been doing this since 1945.&nbsp; While you pray, we protect at the cost of American lives.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The cardinal's words were immediately used against the USA.&nbsp; The dreadful BBC ran this headline: &quot;Vatican slams handling of Saddam.&quot;&nbsp; This, of course, causes more anti-American feeling throughout the world.&nbsp;</p>
<p>As a practicing Catholic, I respect my church's message of peace on earth and goodwill toward men, but I don't believe Saddam deserves compassion, respect or any other civility.&nbsp;</p>
<p>He has committed crimes against humanity.&nbsp; He must pay for those crimes.&nbsp; And his treatment should be based upon repentance.&nbsp; If he gives up information that will help in the war on terror, he gets the minimum any criminal in the USA would get.&nbsp; If not, then he suffers.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The problem with the Vatican and the U.N. and others who have no solution to fascism, terror, atrocities and mass murder is that they live in a dream world.&nbsp; And they are afforded that luxury by America, Britain and other free nations who stood up to Nazis, communists, Japanese imperialists and now the terrorists.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Cardinal Martino and Kofi Annan may be well intentioned, but they're not looking out for us.&nbsp; And we are the only thing standing between them and a bullet.&nbsp;</p>
<p>And that's <em>The Memo</em>.</p>
<p><strong>The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day</strong></p>
<p>Time now for &quot;The Most Ridiculous Item of the Day.&quot;</p>
<p>Hanukkah is just three days away, Christmas nine days away, and I'm afraid to go to the mall.&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; I fear the mall zombies who will eat your flesh if you get between them and a product they want.</p>
<p>Now because I'm looking out for you, I have a way around the zombies.&nbsp; Both <em>The Factor</em>, which we'll give you the address in a moment, and <a href="http://www.billoreilly.com/" target="_blank">billoreilly.com</a> offer a wide variety of gifts to please everyone except Barbra Streisand.&nbsp; There just is no pleasing that woman.</p>
<p>Now, if you don't have a computer, you can dial 1-877-4 -- the number four -- ALL-GIFTS.&nbsp; And Santa or one of the elves will be there to take your order.&nbsp; So we're looking out for you, OK.&nbsp; We're looking out for you with the computer or without.&nbsp; 1-877-4-ALL-GIFTS.</p>
<p>And you see this pen right here?&nbsp; This is like the &quot;Spin Stops Here&quot; pen that I use here.&nbsp; The official pen.&nbsp; We have it.&nbsp; And all this stuff is just designed to make your life easier.&nbsp; And I'm writing some big checks to charity this evening.&nbsp; This evening.&nbsp; My hand's shaking, OK, because that's what we do with a lot of the money.</p>
<p>And if you don't have a phone or a computer, I just can't help you because that is truly ridiculous.&nbsp; Even Santa has a phone.</p>
