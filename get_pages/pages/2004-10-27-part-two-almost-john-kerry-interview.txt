			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Part Two of the 'Almost' John Kerry Interview</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-10-27T12:08-04:00">Published October 27, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>

	            </div>           
	            
            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> Oct. 26, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, as we told you last night,&nbsp; we've been trying to get an interview with John Kerry for months, and we're not the only ones.&nbsp; Journalist&nbsp;<b>Bob Woodward</b> (<a href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Bob%20Woodward" target="_blank">search</a>), the assistant managing editor&nbsp;at &quot;The Washington Post,&quot; has been trying real hard as well.&nbsp;Mr. Woodward&nbsp; joins us now from Washington.</p>
<p>So we've been trying since the Fourth of July.&nbsp; How long have you been&nbsp; trying?</p>
<p><strong>BOB WOODWARD, &quot;THE WASHINGTON POST&quot;:</strong>&nbsp;Well, since June.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; And where did you — what happened?&nbsp; Tell us about how that went.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Well, you know, they don't want to do it.&nbsp; I, as you may know, interviewed President Bush for three-and-a-half hours for my book&nbsp;&quot;Plan of Attack,&quot; and the president answered hundreds of questions, very specific ones about why he did things, what he felt, his reaction to advice from Cheney, Powell and so forth.</p>
<p>And it seemed that this is a commander in chief election.&nbsp; We are&nbsp;electing the commander in chief and how whoever that person is functions is&nbsp;critical, and so I wanted to come up with some sort of parallel questions&nbsp; that would tell us about what — how Kerry would function as the commander&nbsp; in chief, how he would deal with threats.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; So you were using your book — yes, you were using your&nbsp; book and just inserting John Kerry in the place of Bush.</p>
<p>Now you did something unusual, and I want to tell the audience this&nbsp; because I know you don't do this usually and I never do it.&nbsp; You did submit&nbsp; the questions to the Kerry campaign in advance.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Why did you do that and what was the response?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Well, early September when it looked like this was going to&nbsp;happen, as you may recall, Senator Kerry said on Iraq he would do&nbsp;everything differently compared to what the president had done, so I took&nbsp;the 22 big decision points for Bush and just wrote them out and said this is what I'm after.</p>
<p>In fact, if you looked at the questions, they are not that hard.&nbsp;Kerry's advisers say that he would have strong answers, and I think that's&nbsp;quite possible.</p>
<p>But the mystery for voters, I think, is I've traveled around the&nbsp;country, well, what would Kerry — we know what Bush would do, and, whether&nbsp;you like it or don't like it, it's pretty clear.&nbsp; Kerry has not had an&nbsp;executive position.&nbsp;What would be the principals and so forth?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Absolutely. He hasn't defined himself.&nbsp;But, you know,&nbsp;you've given him the questions in advance, and he still wouldn't do the&nbsp;interview?&nbsp; I mean, I don't know — why?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Risk adversity, I think.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; You know, what's to gain?&nbsp; You know, maybe...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; What's to gain?&nbsp; You're Bob Woodward!&nbsp; You're a big shot!&nbsp;&nbsp; You work at &quot;The Washington Post.&quot;</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Oh, no.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Everybody reads you.&nbsp; What's to gain?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; You know, there's a down side.&nbsp; Every time a candidate opens his mouth, as we know, they get bombarded from the other side.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; That's — but, look — come on.&nbsp; Look, Bush...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; But, you know, my....</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; ... interviewed with you.&nbsp; Bush interviewed with you, and&nbsp; he interviewed with me, all right, and I think it says a lot about Kerry&nbsp;that he turned us both down, and — now I want to do this with you, and you&nbsp; can do this with me because you also have a bunch of questions.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; I want to ask you as the assistant managing editor of &quot;The&nbsp; Washington Post,&quot; not a reporter anymore, because your newspaper endorsed&nbsp; Kerry, as you know...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Yes, but — which I have — you know, I don't have anything to do with the editorial side.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; I know that, but your newspaper endorsed Kerry.&nbsp;Now I'm&nbsp; going to ask you if you know how Kerry stands on these things, and, if you&nbsp; don't, just say you don't.</p>
<p>How would Kerry seal up the Mexican border and prevent three million&nbsp;illegals from coming in here every year?&nbsp; Do you know?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; I think it would be real hard.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; But you don't know what he would do, do you?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; No, I — do you?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; No, I don't, and we both pay attention.&nbsp; We do this for a&nbsp; living, don't we?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; We try.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; So we don't know.&nbsp; Woodward doesn't know.&nbsp; And&nbsp; you're a much better journalist than I am.&nbsp; And O'Reilly doesn't know.&nbsp;&nbsp; He's a big mouth.&nbsp; So — and that's a pretty important issue.&nbsp; So we don't&nbsp;know what he's going to do on the border.</p>
<p>How many U.S. troops would he send to Iraq?&nbsp; He says he'll send more&nbsp; to try to calm things down.&nbsp; Do you know how many more?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; No.&nbsp; He says that he would add 40,000 to the Army, but he also says, as you know, that he wants to get out of Iraq and hopes there&nbsp; would be a withdrawal soon.&nbsp; He was not specific about it, but...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; So he says he'll send more, but then he'll withdraw&nbsp; some.&nbsp;I don't know.&nbsp; I'm confused on that, and that's a pretty big issue.&nbsp;&nbsp; And I'm sure you would have asked him that if you had interviewed him.</p>
<p>How will John Kerry pay for the universal health care he wants the&nbsp; government to be in charge of?&nbsp; Do you know?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Well, what he says is, you know, raising taxes on the&nbsp; rich...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; That's only going to cover 20 percent of it.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; ... is where he would get lots of money.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; That's only going to cover 20 percent of it.&nbsp; And where's&nbsp; the other 80 percent then?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; And it gets used lots of places also, so I don't know, in&nbsp;his case, and to be fair, Bush's case, the things that the president's&nbsp; talked about...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; But we got a chance to ask Bush.&nbsp;We didn't get a...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; We did.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; So you don't know how he's going to pay for the...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; That's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; And I don't know.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; I don't.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; And we both...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; And I don't think — I haven't read anything where anyone's&nbsp;come up...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; I haven't either.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; ... with kind of a concrete answer on that.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Nobody told me how we're going to pay for this universal&nbsp;health care.</p>
<p>OK.&nbsp; That's one, two, three, four.&nbsp; Did he make any mistakes on the&nbsp;record when he came back from Vietnam and was an anti-war protester?&nbsp; Has&nbsp;he admitted to any mistakes?&nbsp; Do you know if he has?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Yes, in part.&nbsp; He said he was over the top in some of his&nbsp;criticisms.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Which ones?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; He said that — specifically about the atrocities that were&nbsp;committed by others.</p>
<p>Can I ask you a question?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Absolutely.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Because I think this is an important issue.&nbsp; One of the&nbsp;things the president — when I talked to him, the president said very&nbsp; directly and jumped in his chair when he said this, he said he believes we&nbsp;in the United States have a duty to free people, to liberate people, and&nbsp; that's one of his reasons for going into Iraq. He said that he and some of&nbsp; the other leaders in the coalition — this is his word — have a zeal to&nbsp;liberate people.</p>
<p>When I questioned him about it, he suggested that I was an elitist.&nbsp;Now I think one of the key questions is, in the future, how do we use our&nbsp; military and other power?&nbsp; How would a commander in chief exercise that?&nbsp; And I'd like to know does Kerry believe we have a duty to liberate people&nbsp; or...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; But I'll answer that question.&nbsp; I think I know the&nbsp; answer.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; I think the Senator would say, yes, I do, because he can't&nbsp;say no, but in conjunction with the world nations, the United Nations, and&nbsp;we have to get a coalition before we do it, we can't do it unilaterally.&nbsp; I&nbsp; think that's what Kerry would say.</p>
<p>Now I've got one more for you, and then you can ask me one more.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Sure.&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Why are you against gay marriage, Senator Kerry?&nbsp;Do you&nbsp; know why he is against gay marriage?</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; I don't specifically.&nbsp;He has just said...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; So you don't know...</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; ... he is.&nbsp; He's just said he is.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Well, he has said he is.&nbsp; He said he opposes it,&nbsp; but you don't know and I don't know.</p>
<p>So you and I, Woodward and O'Reilly — I love the sound of that.&nbsp; See,&nbsp;you dump Bernstein, and it's Woodward and O'Reilly.&nbsp;I love that.</p>
<p>You and I, we can't answer four out of the five questions that are&nbsp;pretty obvious questions.&nbsp; The senator has not told us.&nbsp; Now I think that's&nbsp;pretty frightening, and I'm not being a partisan jerk here.&nbsp; I think the&nbsp; senator has got to answer these questions before next Tuesday.</p>
<p>I'll give you the last word, Mr. Woodward.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Well, one of the other issues in all of this — and it's a&nbsp; character issue and how-somebody-operates issue.&nbsp; I asked the president&nbsp;about doubt, whether he has any doubt about his actions in Iraq, and he&nbsp;just said no doubt, you may not believe it, but I have no doubt.&nbsp; Now the&nbsp;question is what is the role of doubt in John Kerry's world?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b>&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Mr. Woodward, thanks very much.&nbsp; We appreciate you&nbsp; taking the time in being here.&nbsp; And I've read all your books, and you're an&nbsp; excellent writer.&nbsp; I really appreciate it.</p>
<p><b>WOODWARD:</b>&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
