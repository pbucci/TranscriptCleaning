			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">The Debate Over the L.A. County Seal</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-06-28T16:40-04:00">Published June 28, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;June 25, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong>&nbsp; Now for the top story tonight, two other views [of the&nbsp;anti-Christian campaign by the ACLU].&nbsp;Joining us from Washington, Reverend Barry Lynn, the executive director of&nbsp;<b>Americans United for Separation of Church and State</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Americans%20United%20for%20Separation%20of%20Church%20and%20State">search</a>), and Andrea Lafferty, the executive director of the&nbsp;<b>Traditional Values Coalition</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Traditional%20Values%20Coalition">search</a>).</p>
<p>Do you see an anti-Christian jihad, pardon the pun, Ms. Lafferty?</p>
<p><strong>ANDREA LAFFERTY, TRADITIONAL VALUES COALITION</strong>:&nbsp; Absolutely.&nbsp; And the&nbsp;<b>ACLU</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=ACLU">search</a>)&nbsp;has proven itself once again to be hypocrites.&nbsp; You pointed out they've gone after the Los Angeles seal,&nbsp; but they wouldn't protect Christian kids who were forced to engage in Islamic behavior and prayers and&nbsp;<b>Ramadan</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Ramadan">search</a>)&nbsp;and all that kind of stuff.</p>
<p>But we see it all over from movies.&nbsp; We see it in the public sector, particularly from the government sector, teachers, elected officials.&nbsp; You know, you see the L.A. County.&nbsp; They just rolled over.&nbsp; I do believe there are a lot of Christians that are upset about it or people of faith, but they were more afraid of litigation than standing up for what's right.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, I disagree entirely.&nbsp; This litigation could have been conducted at no cost to the county of Los Angeles.</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; They're claiming...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; The three supervisors who voted against it were obviously anti-Christian.&nbsp; Even though one of them is a Catholic.&nbsp; It's Molina.</p>
<p>But listen, Ms. Lafferty, they could have -- this could have cost them five times as much to take the cross off the seal than it would be to litigate against the ACLU.&nbsp; Now...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; Well, they're going to have to take it off of everything.&nbsp; You're right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, it's going to cost five times as much.&nbsp;&nbsp; So the...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; And what about -- excuse me, but what about on the seal there's a pagan goddess?&nbsp; Are Christians -- and where's the ACLU on that?</p>
<p>&nbsp;<b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, you're talking about the goddess Pamona, who dominates the seal?</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, well, that is...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, I want to ask Reverend...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; Why -- you know, don't they take that off?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I want to ask Reverend Lynn about that.</p>
<p>Now, reverend, your organization should be not only the cross should go, but shouldn't the goddess Pamona go too or what?</p>
<p><strong>BARRY LYNN, REV., AMERICANS UNITED</strong>:&nbsp; Well, look, I'd be perfectly happy if we took all religious symbols off of these county and city seals.&nbsp; For one big reason, call me a conservative, but I don't like the idea of government adopting, embracing, seizing the images of any group.&nbsp; And frankly, if there are pagans who still worship the goddess Pamona, I don't know if there are or not...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, the Wickens do it.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; ...in Los Angeles, they ought to object, too.&nbsp; But...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, whoa, you know, this is theoretical, but the fact is the ACLU does not object.&nbsp; The fact is the ACLU did not go after the goddess Pamona.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Fine.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, it's not fine.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; I'm not here for the ACLU.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; It's not fine.&nbsp; But don't you object to the ACLU going on their anti-Christian jihad?</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; No, because they're not on an anti-Christian jihad, but let me tell you about those other incidents that you talked about.&nbsp; For example, the fact that Muslim prayers are being recited in some schools.&nbsp; I've sent letters to those schools every time we hear about it.&nbsp; Why?&nbsp; Because I think it's just as wrong to promote religion of Islam as it is to promote Christianity if you're a publicly paid schoolteacher anywhere in the United States.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; But you are making my point for me.&nbsp; You are making my point for me.&nbsp; This is a selective process aimed at Christians to get Christian symbols removed from the public marketplace.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Not on my part, Bill...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You're -- that's what it is.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Bill, you're talking to me.&nbsp; You're not talking to Ramona Rixton (ph) or the ACLU.&nbsp; You're talking to me.&nbsp; And I said that I have sent complaint letters about all of these incidents, because I do believe in a principle.&nbsp; It's a principle...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Did you send a complaint letter to the ACLU, reverend?&nbsp; Did you send a complaint letter to the ACLU?</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; No, I rarely send letters of complaint to other organizations.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Did you send a complaint letter to the judge who ruled in this, Judge Hamilton?&nbsp; Did you send a complaint letter to her?</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; No, no, no.&nbsp; But I'll tell you that as this is appealed, we would certainly take the position that it is inappropriate to have Muslim prayers recited, even as so-called part of history.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right, but I'm telling you that...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; Can I jump in?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ...you're -- go ahead, Andrea.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; A point of fact.&nbsp; Barry, did you not used to work for the ACLU?</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Of course...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; You're all of the ilk.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; No, we're not...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; I mean, why are you going to go after his former employer?&nbsp; They're going after religious symbols all across the country.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Yes...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; They seem to be gaining momentum.&nbsp; They -- and people need to -- people watching this show need to do something about it.&nbsp; Folks, you call L.A.&nbsp; Tell them you're outraged that they're removing the symbol.&nbsp; The cross is historical.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Well, you know, look, let me stop you both.&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, whoa, Andrea, Andrea...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; (UNINTELLIGIBLE).</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Hold it.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Bill?</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; No, not yet, Andrea.&nbsp; I'm going to jump in here now.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Bill, here's the point...</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Reverend, hold it, hold it, reverend, here's the point...</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ...that this is going to continue because it's well funded.&nbsp; ACLU is very well funded.&nbsp; And they know the judges, like Judge Hamilton, who will rule in their favor all day long.</p>
<p>Here's how people can fight back.&nbsp; They can get their rabbi or their minister and their priest, and pull them aside and go, listen, if you don't start, we are going to lose all our rights.&nbsp; Because, look, reverend, there's a historical context for many religious symbols in our society, sir.&nbsp; And you can't deny that there is.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Some.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; And to try to knock all of them out is not only foolish, but it's dishonest, because we are a Judeo-Christian nation.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; That's right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; That's the philosophy we were founded on.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Let me talk about...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; I got a minute left.&nbsp; I'm going to give you 30 seconds, Reverend...</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ...and you 30 seconds.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; We were not founded as a Christian nation.&nbsp; But the most important...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Judeo-Christian.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; ...things that I think what is important is that people fight back in this way, whether they're religious or non-religious, we need to have a dialogue in this country where everybody is heard.&nbsp; When you've got 2,025 Christian radio stations, when the highest grossing film of this year is Mel Gibson's &quot;The Passion of the Christ,&quot; for anybody to say that there's a war against Christianity, or at least a successful war against Christianity, is absolutely absurd.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Those are private concerns.&nbsp; That's -- those are private concerns.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Absolutely...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Come on.&nbsp; You know that, reverend.&nbsp; Those are private concerns, not public.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; And private concerns is what built America and American religion, not government supported religion.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Go ahead, Andrea.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; But Bill, what makes America...</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; (UNINTELLIGIBLE) government support for religion to survive.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Andrea, go ahead.</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; What makes America great, it was founded on Judeo-Christian principles.&nbsp; And that's what gives everybody religious freedom here in America, whether you're a Muslim, a Hindu, whatever.&nbsp; You have the right to worship here because we are Judeo-Christian.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I'm going to wrap it up.&nbsp; Thanks very much, reverend.&nbsp; As always, Ms. <b>LAFFERTY</b>.</p>
<p><b>LYNN</b>:&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
<p><b>LAFFERTY</b>:&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
