			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">The Spoiler Factor? Ralph Nader Responds</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-10-18T10:38-04:00">Published October 18, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;</em> Oct. 15, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>TONY SNOW, GUEST HOST:</strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;Our top story tonight: Will the spoiler factor be in play&nbsp; on November 2?&nbsp; Yes, we're talking about Ralph Nader.&nbsp; He's running for the&nbsp; presidency as an independent. He's on the ballot in more than 30 states,&nbsp;including nine where the numbers show Bush and Kerry in a virtual dead&nbsp; heat.&nbsp; He's been struck from the ballot in some states, but vows to stay in&nbsp;the race.&nbsp; He's the author of &quot;The Good Fight&quot; and he joins us now from&nbsp; Washington.</p>
<p>Mr. Nader thanks for joining us.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>RALPH NADER (I), PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATE:&nbsp;</strong> You're welcome, Tony.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; I want to get your reaction to a piece written in &quot;Newsday&quot;&nbsp; this week by Donna Brazile, who helped run the Gore campaign in 2000.&nbsp; She&nbsp; says that you were the beneficiary of Republican dirty tricks, and adds why&nbsp; doesn't Nader disavow the dirty tricks?&nbsp; And why can't he see the reasons&nbsp; why Republicans have jumped into his political bed after despising&nbsp; everything he's worked for all his life?&nbsp; &quot;The man we all once admired,&quot;&nbsp; she writes, &quot;is behaving like those he's always blasted, sacrificing&nbsp; principle for political gain.&quot;&nbsp; Your response?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; All right, almost anything goes in politics, Tony.&nbsp; Here's&nbsp; Donna Brazile, a veteran Democrat, part of the Democratic Party, that's&nbsp; hiring Republican lawyers like Ken Starr's firm, Kirkland and Ellis, in&nbsp; Ohio to get us off the ballot.&nbsp; And they have poured millions of dollars in&nbsp; phony lawsuits in dozens of courtrooms.&nbsp; And we're fighting back and have&nbsp; won eight out of nine state supreme court decisions.</p>
<p>But imagine Donna Brazile writing something like that when Reverend&nbsp; Jesse Jackson told me weeks ago that the Democratic Party was not actively&nbsp; registering nine million African-American voters, 90 percent of whom vote&nbsp; for the Democrats and could swing the swing states.</p>
<p>Too much scapegoating, too much fabrication, especially by Terry&nbsp; McAuliffe, who says our campaign's backed by corporate backers.&nbsp; I mean,&nbsp; this guy is talking about corporate backers?&nbsp; He's marinated in corporate&nbsp; money.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Look, well, needless to say there's no love lost between you&nbsp; and the Democratic Party right now mainly because they're afraid you're&nbsp; going to cost their guy the victory.&nbsp; Do you think you might deprive John&nbsp; Kerry a chance to become the next president of the U.S.?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; No, I've been going around the country, going after George W.&nbsp; Bush, who's marinated in oil, all right?&nbsp; And...</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; This is the marinating theme tonight.&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Yes, that's right.&nbsp; I mean, the Democrats, I don't know what's&nbsp; wrong with them.&nbsp; They've become so decadent.&nbsp; They've been losing, losing&nbsp; for the last 10 years at the local, state and national levels to the worst&nbsp; of the Republicans.&nbsp; And they don't introspect and say what have we been&nbsp; doing wrong?</p>
<p>I want, for example, some focus on George W. Bush when he was on the&nbsp; board of directors and the audit committee of Harkin Energy in Texas, sold&nbsp; his stock before the stock plummeted, which is of course the theme of the&nbsp; lot of Enron type corporate crime fraud and abuse that has drained&nbsp; trillions of dollars in the last five years, small investors, pension&nbsp; holders, 401K holders and workers.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; And the SEC, Tony, has not done a complete investigation of&nbsp; George W. Bush's role in Harkin Energy.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; That's the real issue.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Harkin Energy's a real issue.&nbsp; I want to get a little more&nbsp; contemporary.&nbsp; There are also a lot of fears of vote fraud going&nbsp; on right&nbsp; now.&nbsp; You've heard about it.&nbsp; Do you think either -- which party right now&nbsp; is the worst offender in your opinion and also your experience?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Well, I think the Republicans are the worst offender in&nbsp;&nbsp; getting voters off the rolls.&nbsp; And the Democrats are the worst offender in&nbsp; getting competing candidates off the ballot line, namely the Nader-Camejo&nbsp; ticket.</p>
<p>So they're both culpable.&nbsp; You know, Tony, our election machinery's in&nbsp; a mess.&nbsp; You have electronic voting machines in states where there's no&nbsp; paper trail.&nbsp; You have voters being disenfranchised, precinct addresses&nbsp; being switched at the last minute, people designated as ex-felons just&nbsp; because their names are similar and stripped of the right to vote.</p>
<p>I'm trying to make this a major issue in America, trying to deepen the&nbsp; substance of the presidential campaign and raise its tone.&nbsp; And all these&nbsp; politicians are engaged in trivia and gossip and vicious...</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Well, no, wait, wait, wait.&nbsp; You've got these guys having a&nbsp; pretty spirited debate on taxes, on regulation, on Iraq.&nbsp; You say you want&nbsp; to get the United States out of Iraq in six months.&nbsp; How do you do that&nbsp; without putting at risk the lives of people in Iraq right now?&nbsp; And&nbsp; furthermore, without running the risk that the rest of the world's going to&nbsp; look at us and say there they go again.&nbsp; They're bailing out, they've&nbsp; chickened out, they're fair game for us once more?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Well, if you look at our Web site, votenader.org, you'll see.&nbsp;&nbsp; And let me summarize.&nbsp; Six months responsible withdrawal, preceded by&nbsp; internationally supervised elections in order to get rid of a puppet regime&nbsp; that's trending toward dictatorship with our military support.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, wait, wait...</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Let me put it on the plate.&nbsp; Getting our oil companies out of&nbsp; there and getting international peacekeepers who will come in from nearby&nbsp; Islamic countries and other neutral countries if they know there is an exit&nbsp; deadline.</p>
<p>If we want to get the mainstream Iraqis, Tony, to distance themselves,&nbsp; and knock the bottom out of the resistance, we've got to give them their&nbsp; country back.&nbsp; Otherwise, it's civil war, it's a quagmire, it's another&nbsp; Vietnam.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Well, I hate to tell you this, but it sounds like you're&nbsp; endorsing what is going to happen in January already, which is there are&nbsp; going to be free elections.&nbsp; We've seen news in this week in Fallujah that&nbsp; finally the Iraqis have said, you know what?&nbsp; We've had it with you guys.&nbsp;&nbsp; And there's a good chance that the key of the&nbsp; resistance is going to be&nbsp; knocked out of Fallujah.&nbsp; I don't know.&nbsp; It sounds to me like you're&nbsp; endorsing the Bush policy?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; No, I don't think so.&nbsp; I don't think you can have free&nbsp; elections under military domination.&nbsp; They have to be internationally&nbsp; supervised elections.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; But these are -- these are going to be supervised by the U.N.&nbsp;&nbsp; That's part of the deal.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Well, not when the puppet regime people are running with our&nbsp; military backing.&nbsp; But we can argue about this.&nbsp; But you just&nbsp; mentioned&nbsp; that the -- that Kerry and Bush are talking about real things?&nbsp; The reason&nbsp; why they're so close in the polls is because they're so similar on so many&nbsp; issues.&nbsp; They both support the Patriot Act.&nbsp; They both support untouching&nbsp; the bloated military budget.&nbsp; They both are saying drive to victory in&nbsp; Iraq.&nbsp; They both are the same page on Israel-Palestine, supporting the&nbsp; Israeli government instead of the deep and broad Israeli peace movement.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Finally, just tell me quickly, who's going to win? Bush or&nbsp; Kerry?</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; I have no idea.&nbsp; All I know is I'm trying to go around the&nbsp; country so that the people can win, so that we break up the two-party&nbsp; system.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; We have cleaner and more competitive elections,&nbsp; which is what&nbsp; the good fight is all about.&nbsp; You got to read it, Tony.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; I'm going read it.&nbsp; Ralph Nader, as always, thanks.</p>
<p><b>NADER:</b>&nbsp; Thank you, Tony.</p>
<p><b>SNOW:</b>&nbsp; Next on the rundown, anti-Kerry Vietnam vets reignite their&nbsp; campaign against the senator.&nbsp; They're doing it in some big swing states&nbsp; and we're going to tell you how.</p>
