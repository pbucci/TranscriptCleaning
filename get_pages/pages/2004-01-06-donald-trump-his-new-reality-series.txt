			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Donald Trump on His New Reality Series</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-01-06T10:57-05:00">Published January 06, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;January 5, 2003.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the second <em>Personal Story</em> segment tonight, a new reality series starring Donald Trump.&nbsp; This coming Thursday, NBC will run <em>The Apprentice</em> in which 16 Americans compete for a $250,000 job working for Mr. Trump.&nbsp; Is any amount of money worth that?</p>
<p>With us now is the aforementioned Donald Trump -- aforementioned, I should say -- and Mark Burnett, the brains behind <em>Survivor</em> and this new program.</p>
<p>All right, Donald.&nbsp; We'll start with you.&nbsp; Now, look, are you a ruthless mogul?</p>
<p><strong>DONALD TRUMP, <em>THE APPRENTICE</em></strong>:&nbsp; I don't think so.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, you don't think so, but do other people think so?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Perhaps.&nbsp; Perhaps.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Really.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; But I don't think so.&nbsp; I think I'm a reasonable, nice person.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I've known you for a long time, and you're reasonable with me.&nbsp; But I don't know if I want to work for you.&nbsp; You know what I mean?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You're like Steinbrenner.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; You think?&nbsp; Well, that's a compliment.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You know, because if you don't produce, what happens?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Trouble.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You don't carry people, do you?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No, it's trouble.&nbsp; There's no question.&nbsp; And, you know, when you say I'm like George, George has done an amazing job.&nbsp; I said the other day when I saw George wasn't feel so well, some day, hopefully in a long distance, they're going to say the Yankees -- where's George Steinbrenner?&nbsp; We want him back.&nbsp; George is a good guy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I carry some people on <em>The Factor</em>.&nbsp; I do.&nbsp; I never fire anybody because I -- I feel bad if I do.&nbsp; I move them out.&nbsp; I'll put them on other places, other shows, or whatever, but I never fire anybody.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I'm very surprised to hear that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I know everybody thinks I'm like the meanest guy in the world, but I -- I just can't.&nbsp; I always feel that like there's some place for them.&nbsp; But you will say, hey, I gave you your shot, I paid you well, you didn't do it, out.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; You get fired.&nbsp; You know, it's sort of an interesting thing.&nbsp; I've been walking down the street lately.&nbsp; I was in Palm Beach over the weekend.&nbsp; People are pointing at me, saying you're fired.&nbsp; So that seems to be the buzz word.&nbsp; But it's not something I enjoy doing.&nbsp; You know, you...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Do you do it personally, or do you have a hatchet man?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I do it personally.&nbsp; I do it personally.&nbsp; Now, in my company, I'll sometimes do it personally.&nbsp; And, you know, it's interesting with firing.&nbsp; You can do it nicely.&nbsp; You can take -- I've taken months to fire certain people.&nbsp; Months and months.&nbsp; And years even.&nbsp; But you can do it nicely, slowly, quickly.&nbsp; In the end, they hate you because you fired them.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Sure.&nbsp; And there's no question about that, and sometimes you have to if the person's, you know, really incompetent.&nbsp; You're hurting everybody else in the company.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; It happens.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; How many chances do you give them?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, I think it depends on how badly they screwed up.&nbsp; If they -- if they made some terrible dumb mistakes, you -- you sort of like -- let's get rid of them, and -- this is in real life.&nbsp; Now, on the show, it's more obvious, and it's, in a way, perhaps as vicious or more vicious than the real world.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So you're a mean guy on the show?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I think I'm a tough guy, but I think I'm a fair guy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Are you Simon Cowell, the <em>American Idol</em> guy who's denigrating these people and...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I think it's a much different world.&nbsp; I mean we're dealing with the world of business.&nbsp; You know, we're...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, but do you do it like he does it?&nbsp; Do you let them have it?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, at -- I get nasty.&nbsp; There are a couple of instances where the people were really bad.&nbsp; They just...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And you really lay them out, huh?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I lay them out.&nbsp; I lay them out pretty good.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because that's what Americans want to see, don't they?&nbsp; Don't they, Mr. Burnett?&nbsp; They want to see Trump blast them.&nbsp; Why?&nbsp; Why?&nbsp; What is it about that?</p>
<p><strong>MARK BURNETT, <em>THE APPRENTICE</em>, HOST</strong>:&nbsp; I think it's really like -- people like watching a train wreck at times.&nbsp; You know, people like rubber-necking on the freeway.</p>
<p>But in this show -- we've all applied for a job interview, and -- after a 13-week job interview, Bill, and we've all been fired at some time.&nbsp; At least I have.&nbsp; Maybe you haven't.&nbsp; I have.&nbsp; And we can relate to it.</p>
<p>And when you screw up, Trump's going to get you and you're going to face Trump in that boardroom.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So here -- this nice Donald Trump -- this guy who -- he does nice things.&nbsp; I've known Trump for a long time.&nbsp; He does nice things.&nbsp; And you don't know about it because he doesn't put it in the paper.</p>
<p>But you've got him in the Simon Cowell slot.&nbsp; You've got him in the -- OK, now this guy's going to really get it.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; There's a big difference, though.&nbsp; That's denigration.&nbsp; This is affirmation, and, if you do mess up, you're fired.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; But there's...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No second chances?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; There's no second chances.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; But Donald Trump doesn't fire what we always thought would be the obvious one.&nbsp; Someone appears like they've done badly, but he sees something in that person that others don't see.&nbsp; That's what makes Donald Trump a billionaire.&nbsp; He's hired great people and kept great people.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So now you're saying that it's not always the obvious one that gets fire?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; That's true.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Are you going to fire the good-looking babes?&nbsp; I mean come on.&nbsp; You know, you're...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, I guess...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... a single guy now, right?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; Most people think that -- well, I have a great girlfriend, but most people think that I'm going to, you know, be stuck with about four supermodels with brains.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, that's what I would think.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; Yes, I know.&nbsp; And we'll find out how that all happens, but, you know, the interesting thing -- 215,000 people applied for the job, which is...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; ... I guess in the history of television...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Sure.&nbsp; Everybody wants to be on the show, but you can't -- you can't sit there and tell me you're not tougher on men than you are on women?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I think it's equal.&nbsp; I don't...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, come on!</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No, I think it's equal.&nbsp; I -- honestly, Bill, I...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You don't give good-looking women a little more slack hoping they come around?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No.&nbsp; Well, maybe I enjoy looking at them more, but I think I'm equally tough.&nbsp; I don't think...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So you take six months to fire the good-looking one...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Maybe a year.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... but me, I'm out the door in about 30 seconds.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Maybe even less.&nbsp; And maybe I'd take years...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Maybe even less.</p>
<p>You have been, Mr. Burnett, the most successful reality guy.&nbsp; In fact, you have ruined television.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Running in the...</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; Bill, I...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; The actors hate you.&nbsp; The actors hate you!&nbsp; I mean you know that.&nbsp; They're lining up outside.&nbsp; Give me that Burnett guy.</p>
<p>What's the psychology?&nbsp; You must have -- look, I have a psychology here on THE FACTOR.&nbsp; I know why it works, all right.&nbsp; You bring in an eye.&nbsp; You convince Trump, all right, to break away from making billions of dollars every year to give his time to you.&nbsp; What's the psychology?&nbsp; How do you know this is going to work?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; There's a number of factors.&nbsp; The Trump factor is a huge factor, Bill O'Reilly.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, he's a star.&nbsp; Fine.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; It's <em>Lifestyles of the Rich &amp; Famous</em>, inside Trump's world and seeing the inside world of a genuine mogul, the last big mogul.&nbsp; And the casting on the show was very, very good.&nbsp; These people never applied for another reality show, and they're all A-type plus plus.&nbsp; They all want to win.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; How many episodes before the person gets the $250,000 job?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; It's 15 hours of television.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Fifteen.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So you're going whittle them down one a week, throw them off the building, instead of the island.&nbsp; Are you going to actually throw them off one of your buildings there, Donald?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, I think...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Aaaaaaaaaah!</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, I think some of the firings are pretty violent, wouldn't you say?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Pretty violent?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Pretty -- pretty tough, yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What do you do, mess up their hair?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No, no, no.&nbsp; That would be violence if I messed up my hair.&nbsp; But pretty violent.&nbsp; And others are standard but tough.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Escorted from the building?&nbsp; You have the big security guys?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No, we had some problems.&nbsp; I mean, you know, these kids really want the job.&nbsp; I mean they camped out...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And they didn't want to get fired, and they said I'm not leaving the building.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, they camped out in front of Trump Tower -- for days, in some cases.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; When they got picked and, all of a sudden, they get fired, they weren't so thrilled.&nbsp; I mean they weren't happy about it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What are you doing this for?&nbsp; You've got -- you're worth $2-1/2 billion.&nbsp; You don't need this.&nbsp; Why are you doing this?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Is that all?&nbsp; I thought it was more than that.&nbsp; I am having fun, number one.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; You have fun.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Number two, there's an education -- I do.&nbsp; I have fun doing it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; You know, Mark told me -- he sort of misrepresented a little bit.&nbsp; He said two or three hours a week, that would be it, and it turned out to be a little bit more than that.&nbsp; I'm having fun.&nbsp; It's a huge educational factor, and I said from day one...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, whoa.&nbsp; What do you mean educational factor?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; People are going to learn about business.&nbsp; They're going to learn about the...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So you care whether they...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Yes, I think it's cool.&nbsp; I think it's good.&nbsp; That's what I do.&nbsp; Like you do what you do.&nbsp; I mean this is what I do.&nbsp; And they're going to learn about business.</p>
<p>They're going to learn about the jungle of New York.&nbsp; I said from day on that New York City is the toughest jungle.&nbsp; I've watched your jungles all over the place...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, it is.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; .. wherever they are.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; There's no question about that.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; This is the toughest jungle of them all, and we show that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So this is just basically recreation for you?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I have fun doing it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, this is like...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I do.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Instead of playing the 85th round of golf a week, you're doing this.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Well, I -- you know, I do this for...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; You don't need the money, do you?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; No, I don't need the money.&nbsp; I...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; The casinos are doing OK, right?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Everything is doing very well.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Now is he going to eat a rat or anything?&nbsp; Are you going to -- because is -- Trump looks look he's got the good deal here.&nbsp; Trump is the star, all right.&nbsp; Trump gets to fire the people.&nbsp; What's the downside for -- you've got to give Trump a little jazz, no?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; You know what?&nbsp; There's not really a downside for Trump.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Except doing this interview?&nbsp; Is this it?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; Actually no.&nbsp; Nobody wants to come on here.&nbsp; Let's face it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; Nobody wants to do this.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; The last time I was here, I was killed on here.&nbsp; &quot;Survivor&quot; guy.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; You told me that.&nbsp; He really did get you?</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; I didn't want to come on here.&nbsp; No, not again.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; There's got to be -- you've got to give him a little...</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; Did I tell you -- did I tell you he's a good guy?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Oh, come on.</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; He's not that good of guy, but to me he's a good guy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So what -- you've got give him a little jazz, though.&nbsp; You just can't let him just...</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; You know what?&nbsp; He does an awesome job.&nbsp; He's himself.&nbsp; What's great about the show -- you see Donald being himself.&nbsp; He's in the board room.&nbsp; He's either going to succeed or fail making money, and he cuts through the bull really fast.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Well, he does cut through it.&nbsp; There's no question about that.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; The show is called <em>The Apprentice</em>.&nbsp; NBC, Thursday night.&nbsp; And good luck to you guys.&nbsp; And the -- you know, the people might not like you now.&nbsp; Are you prepared for that?</p>
<p>TRUMP:&nbsp; I'm very prepared.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But with $2-1/2 billion, you don't care.</p>
<p>Or they might like me less.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Donald, thanks very much.&nbsp; Mr. Burnett, a pleasure as always.</p>
<p>BURNETT:&nbsp; Thank you.</p>
