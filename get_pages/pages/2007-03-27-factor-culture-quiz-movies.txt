			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">'Factor' Culture Quiz: The Movies</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2007-03-27T10:19-04:00">Published March 27, 2007</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>,&quot;</em> March 26, 2007, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> &quot;Back of the Book&quot; segment tonight, &quot;The Great American Culture Quiz,&quot; cinema. We're going to show you scenes from five movies and ask you questions based on the clips that you're going to see.</p>
<p>As always, it's a multiple choice quiz. And here to help us out Martha MacCallum, who won big last week. She's seen on &quot;Live Desk&quot; each day at 1 p.m. And &quot;FOX &amp; Friends&quot; guy Steve Doocy, who lost big.</p>
<p><strong>STEVE DOOCY, CO-HOST, &quot;FOX &amp; FRIENDS&quot;:</strong> Bring it on, O'Reilly.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Here we go.</p>
<p><strong>MARTHA MACCALLUM, FOX NEWS ANCHOR:</strong> The first week Steve won.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You guys ready.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I'm ready.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Lock in on the clip, and then I'll give you the choices.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Roll it.</p>
<p><b>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</b></p>
<p><b>JANE FONDA, ACTRESS:</b> Alfie, when do we get to the Towseti (ph) gravitational field?</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> One hundred and fifty-four hours, seven minutes elapsed in earth time.</p>
<p><strong>FONDA:</strong> Right. Wake me up in 154 hours. Good night, Alfie.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Excellent. That <b>Jane Fonda</b> classic was called &quot;Barbarella&quot;. It was directed by her then husband, A, Yves Montand; B, Roger Vadim; C, Jacques Chirac; D, Carlo Ponti. Which is it, MacCallum?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> B.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> B? Is that correct? And Doocy's got B. Both of them are right. I'm surprised you guys knew that.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> Thank you.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I thought it was <b>Jacques Chirac</b>. OK. The second clip features John Wayne and Ricky Nelson.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Uh-oh.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>JOHN WAYNE, ACTOR:</strong> If you or any of the rest are short eating money, I'll go good for it. I'll tell them at the hotel.</p>
<p><strong>RICKY NELSON, ACTOR:</strong> I wish you'd do that soon before anything happens to you. No offense again, Sheriff.</p>
<p><strong>WAYNE:</strong> It's nice to see a smart kid for a change.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Hair is perfect. The name of that movie was: A, &quot;Stagecoach&quot;; B, &quot;Hondo&quot;; C, &quot;Rio Bravo&quot;; D, &quot;El Dorado&quot;.</p>
<p>Doocy? C, he has. And MacCallum B. And it is C!</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> Yes!</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> &quot;Rio Bravo&quot;.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> ... <b>John Wayne</b>.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That was — that was a John Wayne movie but Ricky Nelson was not born yet when it was made. OK. So Doocy's one up. All right? Ready, MacCallum? You got to pay attention.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I'm paying attention.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> You're not paying attention. Our third presentation features <b>Jack Nicholson</b>. Roll it.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>JACK NICHOLSON, ACTOR:</strong> I'd like an omelet, plain, and a chicken salad sandwich on wheat toast. No mayonnaise, no butter, no lettuce. And a cup of coffee.</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> A No. 2, chicken salad sand, hold the butter, the lettuce, the mayonnaise, and a cup of coffee. Anything else?</p>
<p><strong>NICHOLSON:</strong> Yes. Now all you have to do is hold the chicken. Bring me the toast. Give me a check for the chicken salad sandwich, and you haven't broken any rules.</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED FEMALE:</strong> You want me to hold the chicken?</p>
<p><strong>NICHOLSON:</strong> I want to you hold it between your knees.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right. The name of that film is A, &quot;Five Easy Pieces&quot;; B, &quot;Easy Rider&quot;; C, &quot;The Last Detail&quot;; D, &quot;The Mummy Returns&quot;.</p>
<p>MacCallum says B, &quot;Easy Rider&quot;. And Doocy says...</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> He was in &quot;Easy Rider&quot;.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> A, &quot;Five Easy Pieces&quot;. Correct. MacCallum &quot;Easy Rider&quot;, they're on motorcycles.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I knew there was <b>Dennis Hopper</b>. I know.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Yes. Did you see Dennis in that clip?</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> No.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> No. OK. All right. Doocy's up by two. I'm rooting for you, MacCallum.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> OK.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> I'm rooting for you. You can still make a comeback.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> All right.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Fourth clip we have <b>Gene Hackman</b> in a bad movie.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>GENE HACKMAN, ACTOR:</strong> Have you ever been to Poughkeepsie? You've been to Poughkeepsie, haven't you? I want to hear it. Come on!</p>
<p>You've been there, right?</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>HACKMAN:</strong> You sat on the edge of the bed, didn't you? You took off your shoes, put your finger between your toes and picked your feet, didn't you?</p>
<p><strong>UNIDENTIFIED MALE:</strong> Yes.</p>
<p><strong>HACKMAN:</strong> All right. You put a sheet over my partner. You know what that means? <strong>(EXPLETIVE DELETED)</strong> all winter long I got to listen to him gripe about his bowling scores. Now I'm going bust your ass for those three bags and the — for picking your feet in Poughkeepsie.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> That movie, &quot;The French Connection&quot;, was based on a real case. What was Hackman's last name in the flick? A, Doyle; B, Reynolds; C, O'Malley; D, Ailes.</p>
<p>What is the name? Doocy? A, Doocy says Doyle. And you say Doyle. Popeye Doyle is correct. Now you can't beat him. He got them all right so far. I got to make these harder.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> These are hard.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right, the final — finally, who could forget <b>Raquel Welsh</b> in her early days?</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><strong>(ANIMAL NOISES)</strong></p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP)</strong></p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> And you think being a star is easy? That movie is, A, &quot;The Lost World&quot;; B, &quot;One Million Years B.C.&quot;; C, &quot;Journey to the Center of the Earth&quot;; D, &quot;Beach Blanket Bingo&quot;.</p>
<p>MacCallum? Ooh! Indecision. You can see it. Come on, MacCallum.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> A.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> A. She says &quot;The Lost World&quot;. And Doocy says?</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> I still remember it.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Got them all.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Oh, Doocy!</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> MacCallum, dismal.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> I cannot give you a hard time tomorrow.</p>
<p><b>DOOCY:</b> There's not one word of dialogue in that thing.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> MacCallum got three wrong. You got them all right. Doocy got them all right. You're still smarter than he is. He just watches all the crazy movies.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Thank you, Bill.</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> All right. There it is, the &quot;Great American Culture Quiz&quot;. How did you do? And if you want to take it again, it's on BillOReilly.com. You might get them right the second time around.</p>
<p><b>MACCALLUM:</b> Maybe I will, too (ph).</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY:</b> Martha, Doocy, thanks very much.</p>
