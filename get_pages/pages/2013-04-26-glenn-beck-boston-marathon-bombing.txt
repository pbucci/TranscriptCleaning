			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Glenn Beck on the Boston Marathon bombing</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2013-04-25T00:00-04:00">Published April 25, 2013</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="2328316044001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/2328316044001/glenn-beck-on-boston-marathon-bombing" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/042513_factor_beck_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>
							<div class="caption">

<p></p>
<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; April 25, 2013. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Thanks for staying with us. I'm Bill O'Reilly. In the &quot;Personal Story&quot; segment tonight, our pal, Glenn Beck reporting that a Saudi national in this country, on a student visa, should be under scrutiny in the Boston bombing case.</p>
<p>The 22-year-old was hurt in the bombing, treated in a Boston hospital. The situation was so intense that Homeland Security Director Janet Napolitano addressed it.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<div></div>
<p>JANET NAPOLITANO, SECRETARY, HOMELAND SECURITY: He was not on a watch list. What happened is, this student was in the -- really, when you -- in the wrong place at the wrong time.</p>
<p>He was never a subject. He was never even really a person of interest.</p>
<p>Because he was being interviewed, he was, at that point, put on a watch list. And then, when it was quickly determined he had nothing to do with the bombing, the watch listing status was removed.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: And joining us now from his studio here New York to refute the secretary, Glenn Beck.</p>
<p>GLENN BECK, FOX NEWS HOST: This is amazing, Bill. She first says he's not on a watch list, he wasn't even a person of interest, but we put him on the watch list -- but she just said didn't, because he was being interviewed.</p>
<p>So, in other words, she says we had no evidence. So it's her racism that put him on the no-fly list.</p>
<p>But not a watch list. That's not the same. His -- while she tries to play this down, his apartment was searched for nine hours.</p>
<p>His roommates were interviewed for five hours. He was given a designation of something called a 2123B. This is the front page of the document.</p>
<p>This was given to us by sources inside the administration. And we have about 10 sources on it. The problem is, the 2123B is not the no-fly list.</p>
<p>That is a terrorist designation of the highest order. What reporters are just taking the -- Janet Napolitano's word for it, why is beyond me.</p>
<p>But what they are missing is page two which they don't have. But our sources have given us. Page two also goes on to say he is armed and dangerous.</p>
<p>You don't put somebody on that. It takes two people to put you on the watch list, according to the Patriot Act. Armed and dangerous, in this watch list.</p>
<p>It says right here on the front that there is a second file on him. So, now, he's been a 2123B terrorist of the highest order. And it says in here, &quot;Oh, by the way, there's another file.&quot;</p>
<p>Then, suddenly, after the president meets with the Saudis and then John Kerry meets with the Saudis, all of a sudden, he's not a person of interest. And somebody goes in and tries to delete this file.</p>
<p>They tried to get rid of all of the evidence. After &quot;TheBlaze&quot; reports it, they tried to get rid of all of it. One other thing, he comes in here with a VIP status. He has never been vetted before. Who is he.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: He has some connections to the Saudi royal family. All right, so look, they -- well, the bottom line is, you say that you suspect that he's a bad guy.</p>
<p>BECK: No, no, no. The federal government designated him --</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. And all that backup is on the &quot;TheBlaze,&quot; right, at blaze.com.</p>
<p>BECK: Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And they say, he isn't a bad guy because, we thought he might have been, but we've investigated him and he isn't. Is there ever going to be any -- are we going to find out what the truth is.</p>
<p>BECK: Yes. There are six congressmen that are looking into it. It just requires some press that is willing to go along with us and look at it.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Here we are, Beck. We're always willing to give you a hearing.</p>
<p>BECK: What we need it, Bill, we need journalists that are not going to give up. Why, if this guy is no big deal, why was this -- and our sources say, this has never been done before. To a 30-year vet, one of our sources, said, &quot;I've been doing it 30 years. I've never seen anything like it.&quot;</p>
<p>What this says is, after &quot;TheBlaze&quot; started reporting this, I believe it was yesterday this was put out, if you go in and try to look at any of these documents, there's a warning, &quot;You're breaking the law. You're not allowed to look at it unless you are right in and part of this investigation.&quot;</p>
<p>This -- well, according to our sources in law enforcement, this puts the wall back up that, really, was one of the problems that we tried to get rid off of September 11th.</p>
<p>They're hiding the Saudi connections. I don't know what they are. But who is this guy. Where is this guy.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: He's supposed to be a college student.</p>
<p>BECK: He didn't show up to his college.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, but they said that he transferred to Boston and he's in Boston now.</p>
<p>BECK: He didn't. There's no record of it. In his 2123B, it does not say that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: No, I mean, this is new information.</p>
<p>BECK: And everybody is taking the word of Janet Napolitano and the DHS. That's like calling the Nixon White House and saying, &quot;Are you sure there wasn't a break-in.&quot;</p>
<p>(LAUGHTER)</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. So you're skeptical of the secretary.</p>
<p>BECK: Skeptical might be an understatement.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right, Beck. Beck is in town. By the way, he's getting the Best Picture of the Year award from Robert De Niro, right.</p>
<p>BECK: No.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Or something like that, tonight, the Tribeca Film Festival?</p>
<p>BECK: Tribeca Film Festival has awarded me the Disruptive Innovator of the Year award.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Disruptive Innovator of the Year. I'd like that. Were there any other people nominated. I don't think so. It was just you, right.</p>
<p>BECK: No, you know what, the people who are disruptors in different things. I think Justin Bieber is giving one tomorrow, the guy who did &quot;Gangnam Style.&quot;</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Oh, well, you're in good company there, Beck.</p>
<p>BECK: And me.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: All right. Check out &quot;TheBlaze.&quot; It's a serious story.</p>
