			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Is it a Sin  for Catholics to Vote for Senator Kerry?</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-10-14T13:35-04:00">Published October 14, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <em>partial</em> transcript from <em>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;</em> Oct. 13, 2004, that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> <strong>&quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot;</strong> <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and listen to the</em> <strong>&quot;Radio Factor!&quot;</strong></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp; In the &quot;Unresolved Problem&quot; segment tonight, the archbishop of Denver&nbsp;<b>Charles Chaput</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Charles%20Chaput">search</a>)&nbsp;says Roman Catholics should not support politicians who&nbsp; support abortion rights.</p>
<p>Says the archbishop, &quot;If you vote this way, are you cooperating in&nbsp; evil?&nbsp; And if you know you are cooperating in evil, should you go to&nbsp; confession?&nbsp; The answer is yes.&quot;</p>
<p>Joining us now from South Bend, Indiana, is Father Richard McBrien who&nbsp; teaches theology at&nbsp;<b>Notre Dame</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Notre%20Dame">search</a>), and, here in the studio, Father Frank Pavone, the national director for&nbsp;<b>Priests for Life</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://search.foxnews.com/info.foxnws/redirs_all.htm?pgtarg=wbsdogpile&amp;qcat=web&amp;qkw=Priests%20for%20Life">search</a>).</p>
<p>I do want to give the archbishop in Denver a break here because he did&nbsp; say some other things, and I want to put it in context.&nbsp; He goes, &quot;The&nbsp; reason I want to stress that,&quot; what he just said up on the screen, &quot;is&nbsp; because it's not like bishops are issuing edicts about who you should vote&nbsp; for.&nbsp; It's issuing statements about how a Catholic forms her conscience or&nbsp; his conscience.&quot;</p>
<p>So do you see a problem with the archbishop making a strong statement&nbsp; that was taken by most press people and compacted.&nbsp; They didn't do what I&nbsp; do and read, you know -- he's advising Catholics on conscience rather than&nbsp; who to vote for, I think, is apparent.</p>
<p>FATHER FRANK PAVONE, PRIESTS FOR LIFE:&nbsp; Which is exactly what I've&nbsp; been doing for years as well.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.&nbsp; And it's legitimate that he do that.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Exactly.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; However, it can be misconstrued as an endorsement for&nbsp; President Bush, can it not?</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Well, there's nothing with people coming to conclusions.&nbsp; WE&nbsp; want to help form people's consciences, and, if they come to the&nbsp; conclusion, well, this means I have to vote for the president or I have to&nbsp; vote against somebody else, that's the -- that's their responsibility as&nbsp; voters.</p>
<p>We respect the conscience of each American voter.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But you...</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Part of respecting the conscience is informing the&nbsp;&nbsp; conscience.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK, but, Father, you must be deluged with questions from&nbsp; people like me who say, you know, I might want to vote for John Kerry.&nbsp; Am&nbsp; I doing anything wrong?&nbsp; What will you say?</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Well, here's how I frame the response.&nbsp; You know, this past&nbsp; spring, Bill, there were court trials held across the country in which&nbsp; doctors who perform abortions took the stand and testified under oath to&nbsp; what they do.</p>
<p>Now they used the words, not me, not the Vatican, not the arch bishop,&nbsp; but they used the words &quot;dismemberment,&quot; &quot;decapitation,&quot; &quot;crushing the head&nbsp; of a baby,&quot; &quot;inserting poison in&quot;...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; This is partial birth.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; These were -- these were testimonies on the D&amp;E&nbsp; procedure and other pro...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; They were testifying about all the procedures.&nbsp; Now that's&nbsp; how we have to start answering this question.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But you're dodging the question.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; What is an abortion?&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; We're answering it because the&nbsp; question of whether or not it's a sin to elect somebody who says this&nbsp; should remain legal has to begin by understanding what &quot;this,&quot; namely&nbsp; abortion, really is.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right, but you're still...</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; People...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You're giving them a philosophy lecture, not an answer to&nbsp; the question.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; We're answering the question with our question.&nbsp; The&nbsp; answer to the question is: Do you want to elect somebody...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, I...</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; ... who's going to...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... want to know -- and this is a hypothetical.&nbsp; I'm not&nbsp; saying I'm voting for Kerry.&nbsp; So don't give me any of that.&nbsp; But I want an&nbsp; -- can you give me an answer, Father McBrien.</p>
<p>FATHER RICHARD MCBRIEN, UNIVERSITY OF NOTRE DAME:&nbsp; Are you asking me&nbsp; if I -- one can vote for Senator Kerry?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, no.&nbsp; Look -- Look, I'm a student in Notre Dame.&nbsp; I'm&nbsp; coming over to you.&nbsp; You're the theology pooh-bah.&nbsp; You know everything&nbsp; about God.&nbsp; You know him personally.&nbsp; And I say, you know, I'm thinking&nbsp; about voting for John Kerry, but I'm hearing the archbishop and I'm&nbsp; confused.&nbsp; I don't want to do anything immoral.&nbsp; What do you say to me.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Well, first -- first of all, I'd ask if they were from&nbsp; Denver because the archbishop's authority is limited to his own archdiocese&nbsp; of Denver.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; So it's a sin in Denver, but it's not a sin in&nbsp; Pennsylvania?</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Well, listen to me.&nbsp; The American Catholic Bishops as a body&nbsp; do not follow the approach that's been taken by the archbishops of Denver,&nbsp; St. Louis and Newark and some other bishops of smaller diocese.</p>
<p>They have made it very plain ever since 1988, the 1988 campaign when&nbsp; Bush -- President Bush's father was a candidate against Governor Dukakis of&nbsp;&nbsp; Massachusetts, that they do not presume to instruct anyone on how to vote&nbsp; by endorsing or opposing candidates.</p>
<p>Now the reason they added the words &quot;or opposing&quot; and they are still&nbsp; in every one of their quadrennial statements ever since 1988, is because in&nbsp; the '84 campaign -- the reelection campaign of President Reagan, Cardinal&nbsp; O'Connor of New York, Cardinal Law of Boston and other bishops were so&nbsp; strong in their opposition to the Mondale-Ferraro ticket, that it was clear&nbsp; to many people that they were, in effect, indirectly endorsing President&nbsp; Reagan.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So...</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; So the bishops added the words &quot;or opposing&quot; in 1988.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I got it, I got it, but both of you guys are not serving me&nbsp; as a confused parishioner.&nbsp; I want to do the right thing in this election,&nbsp; and I think most Americans do want to do the right thing in the election,&nbsp; and we come to men like you, Father Pavone and Father McBrien. and we say,&nbsp; you know, I know this is a big church issue.&nbsp; I know it's a big moral&nbsp; issue.&nbsp; But can I in good conscience take the other issues...</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; May...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; May I answer that?</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Sure you can.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; I'll take the position which may seem strange to some of my&nbsp; critics out there -- I'll take the position taken by Cardinal Joseph&nbsp; Ratzinger who is the head of the congregation for the doctrine of the faith&nbsp; in the Vatican, and he sent -- he had a paragraph in a memorandum he sent&nbsp; to the bishops June, and the paragraph is led off by the initials &quot;NB,&quot;&nbsp; which in Latin means nota bene, note this very well.</p>
<p>He said if a Catholic were to vote for a pro-choice candidate&nbsp; precisely because of that candidate's support of abortion, not abortion&nbsp; rights, but abortion as such, then it would be wrong.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; But he said if someone votes for a candidate for other&nbsp; reasons&nbsp; well, taking other issues into account as the American Catholic&nbsp; Bishops say they should, we should put everything in a consistent ethic-of- life framework, they said, then it would not be a sin to vote for...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; So -- and then now I'm starting to understand.</p>
<p>Go ahead, Father.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Father McBrien, are you forgetting the word &quot;proportionate&quot;?</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; No.&nbsp; In fact -- in fact, Ratzinger...</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Then how come you didn't mention it?</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Ratzinger used the word.&nbsp; I've just written a poem about&nbsp; this.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; But you didn't.&nbsp; Listen, you're misleading this audience.&nbsp;&nbsp; The cardinal said that one can vote...</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; I'm glad you're not.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; The cardinal said -- he used the words &quot;for proportionate&nbsp; reasons.&quot;&nbsp; Now I ask Father McBrien, I ask our viewers what is&nbsp; proportionate to 3,500...</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Well, let me give you...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Who, whoa, whoa.&nbsp; Let him finish, Father.&nbsp; I'll give you&nbsp; time.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; ... 3,500 innocent children being deliberately destroyed,&nbsp; not my words, but the words of these abortionists, heads being crushed,&nbsp; arms and legs being torn off.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So you put it at the head of the list.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; There's nothing more proportionate.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; I got it.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Now that -- we don't need a Vatican cardinal to tell us about&nbsp; that.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I got it.&nbsp; I got it.</p>
<p>Go ahead, Father McBrien.&nbsp; Go ahead.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Father Pavone and I agree, I think, on the teaching of the&nbsp; church regarding abortion.&nbsp; But I also -- but I don't know if Father Pavone&nbsp; agrees with the teachings of our bishops that we have to follow a&nbsp; consistent ethic of life.&nbsp; The proportionate reasons would involve taking&nbsp; other life issues in account.</p>
<p>I have here the statement of the bishop -- that Pope John Paul II&nbsp; himself gave to the Vatican diplomatic corps in&nbsp; January of 2003, and he&nbsp; said, &quot;War itself,&quot; war itself, &quot;is an attack on human life since it brings&nbsp; in its wake suffering and deaths.&nbsp; The battle for peace is always a battle&nbsp; for life.&quot;</p>
<p>The proportionate reasons would be that you're looking at a candidate&nbsp; and trying to see what that candidate's views and policies are on a broad&nbsp; range of life issues...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I got it.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; ... including, for example, the war in Iraq.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So we're all back to where we started again.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; It's a judgment call.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; That means that everybody's got to vote their conscience.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Absolutely.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; No, they may not.&nbsp; The bishops and the pope have said that&nbsp; abortion trumps the other issues because it...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; They have never said that.&nbsp; They have never said that.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; They certainly have.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; I don't want you guys to duke it out.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; Listen, Bill, this is not...</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; Bill, it would be a sin, I believe.&nbsp; I would...</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; This is not a card game.&nbsp; There's no trump card.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>PAVONE:&nbsp; I would consider it a sin for me to vote for Kerry.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.</p>
<p>MCBRIEN:&nbsp; This is not a -- well, that's fine.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; And you're entitled to it.&nbsp; I think -- all right.&nbsp; It was a&nbsp; very interesting discussion, and I'll let the folks decide because I'll&nbsp; just get into a lot of trouble.</p>
<p>Gentlemen, thanks very much.&nbsp; Very, very good discussion.</p>
