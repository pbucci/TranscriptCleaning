			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Pete Rose on Gambling, Baseball and His Future</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-01-12T10:55-05:00">Published January 12, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from <em>The O'Reilly Factor</em>,&nbsp;January 9, 2003.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY,&nbsp;HOST</strong>:&nbsp;&nbsp;In the second <em>Personal Story</em> segment tonight, baseball superstar Pete Rose has been banished from the game for 14 years, as you may know.&nbsp; He bet on sports, and that's forbidden.&nbsp; Now he's written a new book called &quot;Pete Rose: My Prison Without Bars.&quot;&nbsp; And here he is.</p>
<p>I want to have a man-to-man talk with you, all right?&nbsp; Can we do that?</p>
<p><strong>PETE ROSE, FORMER BASEBALL ALL-STAR</strong>:&nbsp; You're pretty big for me, though.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Can we strip away all the B.S.?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; OK.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right?&nbsp; Just like we're in the dugout here.</p>
<p>What do you want to accomplish?&nbsp; What do you want at this point in your life?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, first of all, Bill, the book come out this week, but, actually, I got this off of my shoulders 14 months ago when I met with&nbsp;<b>Bud Selig</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Bud%20Selig">search</a>), one-on-one with Mr. Selig in the commissioner's office in Milwaukee, and I told him that, and that was the first time since I was suspended that I was ever able to sit down with the commissioner and talk to him.&nbsp; That's how long and how hard it was for me to get a hold of the commissioner.</p>
<p>And to me, that's important, you know, because my -- I've been living with this lie for 14 years, and it's my fault, and I wish I could erase it, but I really can't erase it.&nbsp; It happened.&nbsp; I have to live with it.</p>
<p>And now it's kind of off my shoulders, and, this week, I took the same monkey off my shoulders as far as the fans are concerned because now everybody in the world knows it.</p>
<p>Fifteen months ago...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right, but -- all right, but what do you...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; ... Bud Selig...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; What do you -- look, the confessional -- we got it.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; It's fine.&nbsp; What do you want to happen now?&nbsp; What happens -- what do you want to happen?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, it -- I would love to be reinstated.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So you want to be reinstated to the game so you can be a manager?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, whatever I can do best to help baseball because...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So you want to get back on the payroll, if possible.&nbsp; What else?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Then, if you get reinstated, you're eligible for the&nbsp;<b>Hall of Fame</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Hall%20of%20Fame">search</a>).&nbsp; Now I can't separate the two, Bill.&nbsp; I think they're equally important.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; So back in the game, Hall of Fame.</p>
<p>Now the sportswriters -- and I don't have a lot of use for the newspaper people.&nbsp; I mean they come after me like crazy.&nbsp; But they want you to grovel.&nbsp; They want you -- you're not groveling enough for them.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, I think -- I think I can be well in that department on TV shows, remorseful on TV shows and radio shows, but, as you know, it's kind of hard to be as remorseful as I should be when you're sitting down writing it on pages of paper.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But do you want to be remorseful?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Sure, sure.&nbsp; I'm...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You do?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; I'm the sorriest guy in the world this happened.&nbsp; I mean I -- I know I embarrassed myself.&nbsp; I hurt a lot of fans.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But you did it for 14 years.&nbsp; I mean, you know, usually you get remorse after maybe a couple of months.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, if you have somebody to tell and I had no one to tell.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You could have told me.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, I...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; I'm your pal.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Yes, but I'm -- you're my pal, but you're not going to reinstate me into baseball.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, but...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; See, I'm a little different than you.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Whoa, whoa, whoa.&nbsp; You don't get remorseful because you want something.&nbsp; You get remorseful because it's wrong.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, you're right about that, but -- but my whole life from here on out is in the hands of one man, and that one man is Bud Selig...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; ... as far as doing things that I'm capable of doing.&nbsp; I'm a baseball person like you're a TV guy.&nbsp; You know, that would be like if you got in trouble next week and the guy says, Bill, you can never talk into a microphone again.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But what I'm getting from you is that this remorse is almost selfish.</p>
<p>Now maybe-- I can't read your mind.&nbsp; So I don't know.&nbsp; But let me -- let me put forth this to you.&nbsp; You know sports is in trouble these days because athletes do terrible things all the time.&nbsp; I mean, you know, rape and drugs -- on and on and on and on.</p>
<p>Wouldn't it have been better for you to say, look, I made a terrible mistake, all right, I don't want anything, I want to make this mistake right so that kids who idolize me and other sports figures won't do what I did?</p>
<p>See, if you had done it without the attachment to -- do you see what I mean?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; You're right.&nbsp; You're right.&nbsp; But I did also say that maybe -- maybe somehow we can take this negative and make it a positive as far as future baseball players, if they even think about gambling, will see what happened to Pete Rose because I think you'll agree I wasn't -- I wasn't slapped on the wrist.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; No, you got -- you got nailed, but you should have gotten nailed.&nbsp; I mean the worst thing...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; That's right.&nbsp; You're right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... you can do is bet on the games because then the whole thing falls apart.</p>
<p>But, see, my problem is I would never judge you, I wouldn't, and I think the sportswriters are, and that just tees me off because they've got no right to judge anybody those -- but I would never judge you.</p>
<p>But what I'm saying to you is from the outside just with no emotion -- you know, you're going on &quot;Oprah,&quot; right?&nbsp; You'll have plenty of emotion there.&nbsp; And if you cry, you'll sell a hundred thousand more books.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; But, see -- see, here's another thing in my head, Bill, and I -- help me with this.&nbsp; Now if I'd have took 15, 16 more pages of my book and said, I'm sorry, I'm sorry, I'm sorry, then I can just see it right now that Pete's phony in his book.</p>
<p>See, the problem I have is this.&nbsp; You know what kind of player I was, how I played.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Right.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; All out, aggressive, knock you on your butt...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You're a macho man.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; ... anything, and it's -- it's hard -- it's hard for me to be remorseful the way people want.&nbsp; It's not my personality.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; But here's -- here's the problem with it.&nbsp; It's the timing of it.&nbsp; That's the problem.&nbsp; The timing is that Rose is doing this because he wants to get back in the game, wants to get in the Hall of Fame.&nbsp; He's only got two more years to get in the Hall, and -- and the upside is you're going to make a ton of dough on this book.&nbsp; You're going to make a lot of money on this confessional book.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, there again, getting back to 14 months ago when I left that meeting with Bud Selig and Mike Schmidt and Warren Green and Bob DuPuy, I was very confident as far as getting reinstated.&nbsp; I mean we had good feelings from that meeting.</p>
<p>And, all of a sudden, 14 months go by, and I've been working with Rick Hill...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; It's a political thing.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; I've been working with Rick on this book three-and-a-half years.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; You know what I would do if I were?&nbsp; I wouldn't worry about getting reinstated.&nbsp; I would just tell my story in a cautionary tale, and I'd give half the proceeds of the book to some Gamblers' Anonymous charity or some kind of addiction charity.&nbsp; That's what I would do, but you do what you want.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; That's a good idea.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; How -- it must be tough for a guy -- it's like I -- I played a lot of baseball, and I interviewed you one time.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; You should have played basketball.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, I couldn't jump.&nbsp; You know, Irish guys can't jump.</p>
<p>But it must be really hard for you to sit across from a guy like me and all the other interviewers and get grilled like this.&nbsp; Don't you just want to slap us?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; No, because...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Come on.&nbsp; Tell the truth.&nbsp; Don't you want to slap me?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; No, what I -- no, no.&nbsp; Not at all.&nbsp; I want to kill you with kindness because...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Because that's what your handlers told you?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; You've got -- no, because I was wrong.&nbsp; I was wrong, I made a big mistake, and I just can't say I'm sorry enough about the mistake.&nbsp; And I'm just trying to put it behind me, Bill.&nbsp; I just...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes, I know.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; I want to go on with my life.&nbsp; I just want to put it behind me.&nbsp; And, you know, I'm still young enough that I've got a productive life ahead, I hope, you know, and...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; ... don't block home...</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Well, Americans are a very forgiving people.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; And don't block home plate with your book because I will bowl you over.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; You will, I mean, and that's why I know this is hard for you.&nbsp; But I want to say two things here.&nbsp; Americans are a very forgiving people...</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Yes.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; ... and I think they will forgive you.</p>
<p>Number two, I do some book signings because I -- you know, I've got a big book and all of this.&nbsp; Rose sits there for hours.&nbsp; He doesn't have to sit there contractually.&nbsp; He's not obligated to sit there.&nbsp; He sits there for hours when people wait on line.&nbsp; He doesn't bail like a lot of other people bail after two hours or so.&nbsp; He signs everybody's book who shows up.&nbsp; All right?</p>
<p>Now, as a guy who knows what this is, how painful it is to sit there for five hours and sign more than a thousand books, that impressed me more than your confession, than your appearance or any -- the fact that for the regular folks, you're signing the book, and you're not -- there's no attitude.&nbsp; So I just wanted people to know that because you wouldn't tell them and all of that.</p>
<p>All right.&nbsp; You've got the last word on this, Rose.&nbsp; What do you want to say?</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Well, I think that the most important thing to me is fans.&nbsp; I think you have to repay the fans, and I've got to work hard to get the respect back from the fans, just like in your occupation the most important thing -- and you've got it -- is viewers.&nbsp; They're what make you tick.&nbsp; They're what make you go.&nbsp; Without the viewers, without the fans, we have nothing.&nbsp; So I think the fans are awfully important.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; We wish you good luck.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Thanks.&nbsp; Thank you, Bill.</p>
<p>O'REILLY:&nbsp; Thanks for coming on <em>The Factor</em>.&nbsp; We appreciate it.&nbsp; I enjoyed the conversation.</p>
<p>ROSE:&nbsp; Continued success.</p>
