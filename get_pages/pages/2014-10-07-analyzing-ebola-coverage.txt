			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Analyzing the Ebola coverage</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2014-10-06T00:00-04:00">Published October 06, 2014</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

			 <div class="m video-ct" data-video-id="3824329163001">
							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/3824329163001/analyzing-ebola-coverage" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/100614_factor_ebola_640.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p><em>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; October 6, 2014. This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</em></p>
<p><em>Watch &quot;The O'Reilly Factor&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Thanks for staying with us. I'm Bill O'Reilly.</p>
<p>And we continue now with Bernie Goldberg from Miami. Yesterday, on CNN, Fox News Host Andrea Tantaros was verbally attacked by Miles O'Brien, a PBS correspondent.</p>
<p>Andrea, last week, was talking about how Ebola is treated in some West African rural areas.</p>
<div></div>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>ANDREA TANTAROS, FOX NEWS HOST: I've said it before, I'll say it again -- in these countries, they do not believe in traditional medical care. So, someone could get off a flight and seek treatment from a witch doctor who practices Santeria.</p>
<p>This is a bigger fear. We're hoping that they come to the hospitals in the U.S. They might not.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Now, Andrea is basing her opinion on a Doctors Without Borders statement that some villages in Ghana are hostile to western medicine and aid workers can't even get into those areas.</p>
<p>The British Red Cross confirms that. But on CNN, apparently, they are not paying attention.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>BRIAN STELTER, CNN HOST: Miles, not much to say here but witch doctors?</p>
<p>MILES O'BRIEN, PBS SCIENCE REPORTER: Well, I mean, we could digress into what motivated that. And, perhaps, the racial component of all this, the arrogance, the first world versus third world --</p>
<p>STELTER: Mmm.</p>
<p>O'BRIEN: -- statements and implications of just that, it's offensive on several levels. And it reflects, frankly, a level of ignorance which we should not allow in our media and in our discourse.</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, let me get this straight, Mr. O'Brien, you would not allow Andrea Tantaros' opinion.</p>
<p>We've asked Mr. O'Brien to come on THE FACTOR. We hope he does. And rejoining us from Miami, Bernie Goldberg.</p>
<p>So, this is cheap shot alley. I mean, Stelter, the guy who interviewed him --</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>-- is a notorious, you know, cheap shot artist, the far left guy CNN hired out of &quot;The New York Times.&quot;</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>He wants to diminish Fox News whenever he can.</p>
<p><strong>(BEGIN VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>O'Brien, I'm kind of surprised that he's a decent correspondent, he should know that in many rural areas of Africa, that aid workers have had - -</p>
<p><strong>(END VIDEO CLIP) </strong></p>
<p>-- a terrible time getting to Ebola victims because the people in the villages, in the towns are superstitious.</p>
<p>They don't trust outsiders. And what Andrea was saying is based on reality or am I wrong.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: No, you're not wrong at all. They talked about ignorance. The ignorance is on the part of Brian Stelter and Miles O'Brien.</p>
<p>Here's what they left out on CNN -- a British Red Cross worker who was working with Ebola patients in Sierra Leone said this -- &quot;Some Africans believe the disease is a punishment or a result of witchcraft. Some families believe that when their loved one goes into an ambulance, they will be given an injection to kill them.&quot;</p>
<p>So, if some Africans believe Ebola is the result of witchcraft, why is it a stretch to believe that some Africans may go to a witch doctor to get straightened out.</p>
<p>Here's what you're not allowed to say out loud and what you'll never hear from a liberal journalist at CNN or any other news organization -- &quot;Many Africans,&quot; -- I have to whisper this, -- &quot;many Africans in Liberia and Sierra Leone and places like that,&quot; yes, here it comes, &quot;are backward people. They do believe in witch doctors.&quot;</p>
<p>But it's a lot easier -- they won't talk about that, the liberals, that makes them very uncomfortable. It's a lot easier to go after someone on Fox for using the term &quot;witchcraft.&quot;</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Yes, we're all racists here. We know that. And we're all, you know, absolutely denigrating the third world.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: And imply unfairly -- right, and imply unfairly that she's a racist. Let me just say this very briefly -- shame on Brian Stelter and Miles O'Brien.</p>
<p>They have no idea how their liberalism affects and infects their journalism. Shame on them.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Well, and then you saw Mr. O'Brien say it should not be permitted. Therefore, Andrea Tantaros is not to be permitted to say that.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: That's a very liberal statement.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Right.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Now, in Miami where you live right now, there is a neighborhood called Little Haiti.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: And there are thousands and thousands of Haitians who have come over to the United States to seek a better life. A very small number of those Haitians practice voodoo, all right.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Exactly.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: They do. Bernie and I know it. I've done a story in Haiti on voodoo. It is there.</p>
<p>There are hungans, which are witch doctors. They are there. They charge for their services.</p>
<p>And, in Africa, it's the same thing. It may not be called voodoo, it's called something else.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: So, what Andrea was saying is that it is certainly possible that some infected patient with Ebola can get on a plane and go to Miami, go to Dallas, Texas where there are other communities who have these people in the United States.</p>
<p>And go there instead of going into the hospital. It's absolutely possible and it's in context.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Let me make something very clear. Andrea Tantaros said nothing, nothing wrong. This is how crazy liberal -- liberalism in general.</p>
<p>If they want to be crazy, that's fine with me. But when liberalism infects journalism, that really bothers me. It's that she used the term, &quot;witch doctor.&quot;</p>
<p>And these two people who didn't know -- how do I know what the Red Cross worker said about how they believe in witchcraft and you know it but these two geniuses at CNN didn't know it.</p>
<p>It's a liberal political correctness. You say &quot;witch doctor&quot; and they say, &quot;Ooh, you can't say that. You're a racist,&quot; or imply that you're a racist.</p>
<p>I've done stories on Haiti also. If I came back and said, &quot;You know, there are a lot of people there who believe in voodoo and go to witch doctors.&quot; Does that make me a racist.</p>
<p>Liberalism is wrecking journalism. And you know what, we're holding them accountable but nobody else is.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: It doesn't matter.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: The other media critics don't hold them accountable, newspapers don't hold -- television media critics don't hold them accountable.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: But the fact that we do and the fact that this segment will be on every Web site, political Web site in the country in about 45 seconds is -- we do what we can do, Bernie. That's all we can do.</p>
<p>GOLDBERG: Right.</p>
<p>O'REILLY: Nice to have you.</p>
