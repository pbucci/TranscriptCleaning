			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/on-air/oreilly/index.html">Interviews</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">Doris Day and Mickey Rooney Remember Ronald Reagan</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2004-06-08T12:21-04:00">Published June 08, 2004</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>

            <div itemprop="articleBody">			
			 <p>This is a <i>partial</i> transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot;&nbsp;June 7, 2004 that has been edited for clarity.</p>
<p><em>Watch</em> The O'Reilly Factor <em>weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET and</em> listen <em>to</em> the Radio Factor<em>!</em></p>
<p><strong>BILL O'REILLY&nbsp;HOST:</strong>&nbsp; In the &quot;Factor Follow Up&quot; segment tonight, as almost everybody knows, Ronald Reagan was a movie star in the 1940s and '50s.&nbsp; He was featured in 59 films, then when on to be president of the union representing actors.&nbsp; From there he leapt into national politics.&nbsp; Joining us from now from Los Angeles is&nbsp;<b>Mickey Rooney</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Mickey%20Rooney">search</a>), one of America's legendary actors.&nbsp;</p>
<p>And on the phone from Carmel, California,&nbsp;<b>Doris Day</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Doris%20Day">search</a>), who starred in two movies with Ronald Reagan: &quot;The Winning Team&quot; and &quot;Storm Warning&quot;, right, Ms. Day?&nbsp; Tell us about him.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>DORIS DAY, ACTRESS</strong>:&nbsp; Yes, Bill.&nbsp; Well, I can only tell you that he was wonderful.&nbsp; He was a marvelous actor.&nbsp; He was a gentleman.&nbsp; We had laughs because he's terribly funny.&nbsp; And we just got along great, and -- but I only made two films with him, which was, you know, too bad.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Now a lot of these guys back when you were making films were kind of philanderers, run-arounds, crazy nut cases.&nbsp; You know what I'm talking about, Doris.&nbsp; You've seen enough of them.&nbsp; But Reagan was totally opposite that?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Oh, totally.&nbsp; I don't know about all the philanderers, but he certainly wasn't one.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Mm-hmm.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Not at all.&nbsp; He was a gentleman at all times.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Did you ever talk politics with him?&nbsp; Were you aware of his interest in that?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; No, I didn't.&nbsp; And I was not interested in politics then at all.&nbsp; But I knew that, you know, most of the people at the studio were Democrats, and I knew that he was then.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; At that time he was...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ... a Democrat.&nbsp; And then he came over in 1962 and changed to a Republican.&nbsp; Now Mr. Ronney, you met Ronald Reagan when you were 15 years old, and that sounds like young, but you were already a huge star at 15 years old.&nbsp; People remember the Andy Hardy members and &quot;Boys Town&quot; and all of that.&nbsp; How did you meet him and what were your impressions of him?&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>MICKEY ROONEY, ACTOR</strong>:&nbsp; We were -- I was staying with my mother at Montisito (ph) Apartments and getting ready to do &quot;A Midsummer Night's Dream&quot; at the -- we were rehearsing at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel where Jan (ph) and I were going to do our show, the ninth through the twelfth.&nbsp; And my wife Jan and I were going to be there.&nbsp; And I came down and I saw this wonderful looking gentleman, very nice, and he said to me, who are you?&nbsp; I said, I'm Mickey Rooney.&nbsp; Who are you?&nbsp; He says, I'm Ronald Reagan.&nbsp;</p>
<p>I said, I'm very happy to know you, Mr. Reagan.&nbsp; And at that time, there was a dog that came out of the - out onto the street in front of the apartment house, and he got hit, he wasn't killed, thank God, and I grabbed him and I brought him back in.&nbsp; And I said, we've got to get him to a veterinarian right away.&nbsp; And he said, Mickey, you're a real humanitarian.&nbsp; And that's the way we started.&nbsp; Of course, he invited me over to Warner Brothers and we had lunch and I met a lot of wonderful people there.&nbsp; And...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; So he was almost like a mentor to you.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; Yes.&nbsp; Well, he wasn't a mentor, but he was a friend.&nbsp; But he could have been a mentor to anybody because he was one of the great Americans, and a great actor.&nbsp; He did 50 pictures, I believe.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; Mm-hmm.&nbsp; Were you aware of his political interests?&nbsp; Were you aware that he, you know, was interested in the world around him to a much greater extent than just being casual?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; No, I wasn't.&nbsp; We never talked politics.&nbsp; But everyone knew that he had it in him to do anything he wanted to do.&nbsp; He was the president of the Screen Actors Guild and he did a lot of things which we're very happy for.&nbsp; And...</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; You stayed in touch with him all these years.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; Well, not quite in touch.&nbsp; We've been traveling around our nation doing our show, but the fact is just hearing about his passing away brought our nation and brought Jan and -- my wife Jan and I to tears.&nbsp; We thought the world of him.&nbsp; And Nancy -- I met his lovely wife, Nancy, at -- before they were married, at Warner Brothers.&nbsp; And it was a thrilling time.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; I'll tell you that.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; OK.&nbsp; The show you're referring to is a musical act.&nbsp; And you're going to do a tribute to Ronald Reagan at the Roosevelt Hotel in Los Angeles from the ninth to the twelfth of this month.&nbsp; Now...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; That's correct.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; ... Doris Day, you have another story about a dog as well.&nbsp; Didn't you get a call from the White House about a dog?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Oh, that's funny.&nbsp; Well, I was reading &quot;Parade&quot; magazine and there was a story in there about Ronnie and the fact that Ronnie and Nancy had received a present, a big dog.&nbsp; I believe it was a&nbsp;<b>Bouvier des Flandres</b> (<a target="_blank" href="http://clickit.go2net.com/search?cid=307797&amp;site=srch&amp;area=is.clicktracking&amp;shape=link&amp;cp=info.foxnws&amp;clickurl=http%3A%2F%2Fsearch.foxnews.com%2Finfo.foxnws%2Fredirs_all.htm%3Fpgtarg%3Dwbsdogpile&amp;ext_qcat=web&amp;ext_qkw=Bouvier%20des%20Flandres">search</a>), a big black dog.&nbsp; And I thought that was a beautiful dog.&nbsp; And anyway, I find out later that they had taken him to Santa Barbara and they left him at the ranch.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Well, I thought, that's terrible to do that.&nbsp; I think that's very sad for that dog.&nbsp; And I gave an interview.&nbsp; I was doing an interview a little later, and I talked about that.&nbsp; And I said, I'm surprised that he would do that, that he would send his dog to Santa Barbara, because I know he loves dogs, and -- anyway, a little bit -- a few days later -- not a few days but a month or so later that came out, and, you know, the &quot;Parade&quot; magazine story came out.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>And I was feeding the dogs over in my dog kitchen, and one of the girls said to me, Doris, it's the White House calling.&nbsp; The phone rang.&nbsp; And I said, which White House?&nbsp; What are you talking about?&nbsp; And she said, it's the White House.&nbsp; Get on the phone.&nbsp; So I went to the phone and I said, yes, who is this?&nbsp; Who's putting me on?&nbsp; And I hear a voice say, this is President Reagan's secretary, and he would like to have a word with you.</p>
<p>Oh.&nbsp; I said, you're kidding.&nbsp; And she said, of course not, here he is now.&nbsp; And he gets on and he said, well, that was a fine story about my dog and about me and Santa Barbara.&nbsp; And I said, oh, I'm sorry you read it.&nbsp; And he said, yes, I read every word.&nbsp; And he said, I was furious about that, and I'm calling you to tell you that he loves Santa Barbara.&nbsp; He loves the ranch.&nbsp; He has lots doggie friends there.&nbsp; And he was not happy in the White House.&nbsp; I said, no?&nbsp; He said, no.&nbsp; He couldn't run without a leash.&nbsp; He couldn't do, he couldn't play.&nbsp; He couldn't...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; So that was -- that's an interesting story because it just shows you that Reagan was on top of just about everything that happened, and a lot of people thought he wasn't.&nbsp; That he was...&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Oh, you bet he was.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; He was sharp.&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Mr.&nbsp;Rooney, I'm going to give you the last word on this.&nbsp; We only have about 45 seconds left.&nbsp; You obviously have a great bit of affection, you're emotional, this is an emotional week for a lot of Americans.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>DAY</b>:&nbsp; Yes, it is.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; What was one thing meeting Reagan at 15 that he taught you that you can tell us in about 30 seconds, sir?&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>ROONEY</b>:&nbsp; Well, Bill, if you had time to listen to him, you could find out that you could learn most anything you want.&nbsp; He was proud of everything that he did and he loved America.&nbsp; And with our vote coming up very shortly, we should all remember, be you a Democrat, a Republican, no matter what, remember be a good American first.&nbsp;</p>
<p><b>O'REILLY</b>:&nbsp; All right.&nbsp; Ms. Day, Mr. Rooney, we appreciate you both coming on and sharing those stories with us.&nbsp;</p>
