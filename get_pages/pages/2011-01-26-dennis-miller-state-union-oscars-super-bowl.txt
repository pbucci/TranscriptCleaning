			<h1 itemprop="headline">Dennis Miller on State of the Union, Oscars, Super Bowl</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2011-01-26T00:00-05:00">Published January 26, 2011</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization">FOX News</div></div>

							<a href="http://video.foxnews.com/4512494/dennis-miller-locked-and-loaded" itemprop="url"><img	src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/img/fn2/video/876/493/012611_oreilly_miller_FNC_012611_20-56.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" itemprop="image"></a>

						<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p> <em><strong>This is a RUSH transcript from &quot;The O'Reilly Factor,&quot; January 26, 2011.</strong></em></p>
<p><em><strong>This copy may not be in its final form and may be updated.</strong></em></p>
<p><em> Watch &quot;<strong>The O'Reilly Factor</strong>&quot; weeknights at 8 p.m. and 11 p.m. ET!</em> </p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<div></div>
<p><strong> BILL O'REILLY, HOST:</strong> In the &quot;Miller Time&quot; segment tonight: lots to talk</p>
<p>about, so let's bring in the sage of Southern California, who joins us</p>
<p>now from Los Angeles. All right, Miller, State of the Union, go.</p>
<p> <strong>DENNIS MILLER, FOX NEWS CONTRIBUTOR:</strong> Well, before we start, what the</p>
<p>hell was Al Sharpton even talking about? That sounded like Professor</p>
<p>Irwin Corey explaining the infield fly rule. And you know, he's going</p>
<p>to get carpal tunnel from almost flipping the race card on you that</p>
<p>many times in one interview. You could see my man just about to lay it</p>
<p>on you, then he'd pull back for a second. That's a minefield, Billy.</p>
<p>Just as a &quot;Factor&quot; view, can I tell you I get nothing out of that. Dr.</p>
<p>King would have turned that off and watched &quot;Punked&quot; on MTV.</p>
<p> Anyway, back to the State of the Union. I did not watch it. If I want</p>
<p>to see a roomful of big asses, I'll watch the Kardashian show. Although</p>
<p>the clips I did see today, it was nice just not to have Contessa</p>
<p>Twitchie McMagpie over the president's left shoulder so I could focus</p>
<p>on some of this. I thought Boehner held up pretty good, and Obama was</p>
<p>trying to do, you know, a little Bermuda onion cutting under his eyes,</p>
<p>playing patches for him, turning around and doing scenes from &quot;Terms of</p>
<p>Endearment.&quot;</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY: </strong>He almost made him cry. Almost. He almost broke down.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> Yes, but he held onto -- he held onto his blanky.</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> O'REILLY:</strong> Right.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> Boehner had his blanky, and he held onto it. Hey, and look at</p>
<p>-- show Biden, Billy. Put Biden up. They told Biden, listen, everybody</p>
<p>thinks you are a doofus. Just sit there and don't move. So now he's to</p>
<p>the point where he looks like that dog, Ashley Whippet, sitting in a</p>
<p>park, eyeing the crowd, waiting for somebody to throw a Frisbee he can</p>
<p>go fetch. What's he on point over there?</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I don't know. I thought he looked very happy last night, the</p>
<p>vice president. You know, you compare him to how Dick Cheney looked,</p>
<p>Dick never really looked happy. But Mr. Biden -- well, he's not happy</p>
<p>there. Now, he's not paying attention. Whenever they focused on him, he</p>
<p>looked happy. Now, what about winning the future, Miller? Are you --</p>
<p>are you down with winning the future? Are you onboard with that?</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> You know, I don't really listen to -- the first 20 times I saw</p>
<p>the Barack speech, I thought it was -- I didn't agree with him but I</p>
<p>liked it. I mean, I thought, wow, what a great orator. I really don't</p>
<p>watch him anymore, Billy. But I can pretty much comment on it by not</p>
<p>watching it.</p>
<p> All I needed to see this week was the grownups are taking back over. He</p>
<p>whacked the imperious doofus, Gibbs. And he brings in Daly. Daly's</p>
<p>going to run the show. Daly brought him in, he said, &quot;Listen, if you</p>
<p>want to get re-elected, here's three things you've got to do. You've</p>
<p>got to get your head out of the clouds. They don't believe in global</p>
<p>warming. You've got to throw an eco-Mormon -- moron out the door, and</p>
<p>that climate chick two doors down, she's got to go.&quot; All right, she's</p>
<p>gone.</p>
<p> Secondly, he comes in and says, &quot;They don't like what you're doing.</p>
<p>They like Reagan. You've got to go out there and become like Reagan,</p>
<p>and you've got to hide everything you really want to do because they're</p>
<p>not buying what you want to do.&quot;</p>
<p> And thirdly he said, &quot;You've got to get back into bed with big</p>
<p>business. And if that means wearing a Mr. Monopoly monocle for the next</p>
<p>couple of years, you've got to do it, because you're not going to get</p>
<p>rehired again if you look like a buzz-kill about the engine that runs</p>
<p>this country. In other words, Barack, and I say this as a Chicago guy</p>
<p>to you, if you want to come back for another four and protect the</p>
<p>mothership nest egg that is health care, you've got to act like you're</p>
<p>not you anymore, because they're not going to have you back unless you</p>
<p>change to a completely different guy.&quot;</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Now, I like that analysis. I think that's good. I didn't see</p>
<p>him changing into a different guy though last night. I saw the same</p>
<p>stuff, but it was kind of disguised. It's investing rather than</p>
<p>spending. And it's like, well, yes, we can. Yes, we can. Hope and</p>
<p>change has turned into yes, we can now. We can beat the Chinese, and</p>
<p>we'll be smarter than the Korean kids. And there's no way that's going</p>
<p>to happen, by the way. So I don't know if he's a -- Daly's telling him</p>
<p>the right stuff, but I don't know if he can do it.</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> MILLER:</strong> Now, he's not a changed man at all. I'm saying all he has to do</p>
<p>is tweak it a half a dial to the right, and they'll buy it like they</p>
<p>did when he was running.</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> O'REILLY:</strong> He's a likeable guy.</p>
<p> All right. Academy Awards, &quot;True Grit&quot; and this British picture, right,</p>
<p>where they teach the king not to stutter? That's the one? I didn't see</p>
<p>it, but...</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> MILLER:</strong> It was a lovely -- it was a touching film. I think Firth will</p>
<p>win for the Oscar thing. I think &quot;The Social Network&quot; will probably</p>
<p>win, although &quot;Toy Story 3&quot; I thought was a beautiful film. For the</p>
<p>actors thing, I think it's probably Annette Bening's time. For the</p>
<p>under card, I'd go with the two people from &quot;The Fighter,&quot; Bale and</p>
<p>that Leo lady. They were great. And director, I don't know -- I don't</p>
<p>know who they give it to over there.</p>
<p> But I will say this. I like James Franco. I think he's going to be a</p>
<p>hell of a host, Billy. I don't know if you know this about Franco, but</p>
<p>this is a guy who's nominated for an Oscar, and he is still on a soap</p>
<p>opera called &quot;General Hospital.&quot;</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> O'REILLY:</strong> I did not know that. Didn't he -- didn't he gnaw his arm off</p>
<p>or something, this guy Franco? Didn't he cut his arm off? Did he do</p>
<p>that on &quot;General Hospital&quot; or in the movie?</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> MILLER:</strong> No, in the movie.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> He should have done it on &quot;General Hospital&quot; because he would</p>
<p>have been in the hospital, and he could have got sewn up.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> In &quot;General Hospital,&quot; he's just showing that he doesn't take this all that seriously. I dig the kid.</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> O'REILLY:</strong> OK. Miller digs the cat.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> By the way, Billy.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY: </strong>Yes.</p>
<p> <strong></strong></p>
<p><strong> MILLER:</strong> The reason I can't take the awards seriously this year, the</p>
<p>best thing I saw all year was a documentary called &quot;Waiting for</p>
<p>Superman&quot; about the corruption of the public education system in this</p>
<p>country.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Yes, that was terribleâ€¦</p>
<p> (CROSSTALK)</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> That should have been nominated like that, but it goes against liberal orthodoxy, so they threw it out the window.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> Exactly. That will show you what a rigged game it is. They should be ashamed that it's not in there.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> You and I should have been nominated for many, many awards, but we haven't been. I can't -- it's unbelievable.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> Billy, I think of you as Austin Powers and I'm a Mini Officer O'Reilly.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> Great. One million dollars.</p>
<p> OK. Super Bowl, who's going to -- who's going to win? You're a</p>
<p>Pittsburgh guy. Who's going to win? I know you're rooting for them.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> I like &quot;The Social Network.&quot; No, listen, I like -- I think the</p>
<p>Steelers are going to win because I was raised there. But I'll tell you</p>
<p>this: If Aaron Rodgers plays like he did against the Atlanta Falcons,</p>
<p>they're winning the game.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> I think Green Bay has got it. I think Green Bay is going to</p>
<p>get to Big Ben, the defense is going to get to him, and that will be</p>
<p>the difference in the game. 28-17, Green Bay.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> All right. We've got a steak dinner on that because I'm going with the Steelers.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> All right.</p>
<p> <strong>MILLER:</strong> But if Rodgers plays that Falcons game, yes, they win.</p>
<p> <strong>O'REILLY:</strong> OK. Dennis Miller, everyone.</p>
